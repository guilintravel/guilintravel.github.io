Guilin’s Cheapest (& Best) Hostels for Backpackers

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Nestled among the iconic karst mountains and winding rivers of Southern China, Guilin has long been a magnet for travelers seeking natural beauty, adventure, and cultural immersion. For backpackers, the city offers an irresistible blend of affordability and awe-inspiring landscapes. But let’s be real: when you’re traveling on a budget, where you stay can make or break your experience. Luckily, Guilin is home to some of the most welcoming, well-located, and cheapest hostels in the region—each with its own unique charm and community vibe.

Backpacking in Guilin isn’t just about checking off Li River cruises or snapping photos of the Longji Rice Terraces. It’s about connecting with fellow travelers, sharing stories over a bottle of local beer, and finding those hidden gems that don’t make it into guidebooks. The hostels here aren’t just a place to sleep—they’re hubs of information, friendship, and adventure.

Why Choose a Hostel in Guilin?

Staying in a hostel is more than a way to save money. It’s a lifestyle. In Guilin, hostels often double as tour operators, cultural exchange centers, and social lounges. Many offer free walking tours, bike rentals, and even cooking classes focused on Guilin’s famous dishes like Guilin mifen (rice noodles). For solo travelers, it’s an instant community. For groups, it’s a chance to meet people from all over the world.

Moreover, Guilin’s hostels are strategically located. Most are within walking distance of the city’s main attractions, such as Elephant Trunk Hill, Seven Star Park, and the bustling Zhengyang Pedestrian Street. Others are nestled in quieter areas like Yangshuo, offering direct access to countryside cycling routes and rock climbing adventures.

Top Budget Hostels in Guilin

Wada Hostel Guilin

Located just a 10-minute walk from Guilin Railway Station, Wada Hostel is a favorite among backpackers for its clean facilities, friendly staff, and social atmosphere. The dormitory rooms are equipped with privacy curtains, personal reading lights, and secure lockers. Common areas include a cozy lounge with books and board games, a shared kitchen, and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the city skyline.

The hostel organizes regular events like group dinners and hiking trips to nearby peaks. Prices start at around $8 per night for a dorm bed, making it one of the most affordable options in the city.

Guilin Central Hostel

Situated in the heart of the city, Guilin Central Hostel is perfect for travelers who want to be close to the action. It’s within walking distance of the Li River and the city’s best night markets. The hostel features a mix of dormitories and private rooms, all decorated with local artwork and traditional Chinese elements.

What sets this place apart is its emphasis on cultural experiences. They offer free calligraphy classes, tea tasting sessions, and guided tours to lesser-known spots like the Reed Flute Cave. Beds in shared rooms start at $10 per night, and the staff are known for going above and beyond to help guests plan their itineraries.

Yangshuo Backpacker Lodge

For those who prefer to base themselves closer to nature, Yangshuo Backpacker Lodge is an ideal choice. Located in the picturesque town of Yangshuo, this hostel is surrounded by limestone peaks and rice paddies. It’s a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with easy access to biking, kayaking, and bamboo rafting.

The lodge offers both dormitory and private accommodations, with a spacious garden and a popular bar area where travelers gather in the evenings. The staff can arrange discounted tickets for the Impression Liu Sanjie light show, a must-see cultural performance directed by Zhang Yimou. Dorm beds are priced from $9 per night.

Riverside Inn Hostel

As the name suggests, Riverside Inn Hostel overlooks the Li River, providing breathtaking views from its balconies and common areas. This hostel is slightly more upscale but still very budget-friendly, with dorm beds starting at $12 per night. It’s located between Guilin and Yangshuo, making it a great base for exploring both areas.

The hostel offers free shuttle services to nearby attractions and has a well-stocked bar serving local craft beers. They also host weekly BBQ parties and movie nights. The rooms are spacious and include air conditioning and hot showers—a welcome luxury after a day of hiking.

Old Street Hostel

For a taste of Guilin’s history, Old Street Hostel is located in a restored traditional building along the ancient streets of Guilin. The architecture blends classic Chinese design with modern amenities, creating a unique and comfortable environment. The hostel is just a short walk from the city’s famous night market and street food stalls.

They offer free walking tours of the old town and have a dedicated travel desk for booking adventures like river cruises or trips to the Longji Rice Terraces. Dormitory beds cost around $11 per night, and the hostel is known for its warm, family-like atmosphere.

What to Expect from Guilin’s Hostels

Community and Social Vibes

One of the biggest draws of hostels is the sense of community. In Guilin, most hostels have common areas designed to encourage interaction. From shared kitchens to rooftop bars, these spaces become the backdrop for friendships and travel partnerships. It’s not uncommon to meet someone at breakfast and end up exploring the city with them all day.

Affordable Tours and Activities

Hostels in Guilin often partner with local tour operators to offer discounted rates on popular activities. For example, a Li River cruise might cost significantly less if booked through your hostel. Many also organize free or low-cost events like group hikes, cooking classes, and language exchanges.

Local Insights

The staff at Guilin’s hostels are usually locals or long-term residents who know the city inside and out. They can recommend the best street food stalls, hidden temples, and scenic spots that aren’t overcrowded with tourists. This insider knowledge can transform your trip from a standard tour to an authentic adventure.

Tips for Booking Hostels in Guilin

  1. Book in Advance: Especially during peak seasons (spring and autumn), hostels fill up quickly. Use platforms like Hostelworld or Booking.com to secure your spot.
  2. Check for Extras: Some hostels include free breakfast, Wi-Fi, or airport shuttle services. These small perks can add up to big savings.
  3. Read Reviews: Pay attention to recent reviews about cleanliness, safety, and staff helpfulness.
  4. Consider Location: If you plan to spend most of your time in Yangshuo, book a hostel there instead of in downtown Guilin to save on transportation time and costs.
  5. Pack Light: Most hostels have shared bathrooms and limited storage space. A lightweight backpack and quick-dry towels are essential.

Beyond the Hostel: Guilin’s Backpacker Hotspots

While your hostel will be your home base, Guilin’s real magic lies outside. Here are some must-do activities for backpackers:

Cycling through Yangshuo’s Countryside

Rent a bike (often available at your hostel for a small fee) and spend a day cycling through rice terraces, past limestone mountains, and alongside the Yulong River. It’s a cheap and unforgettable way to experience the landscape.

Hiking in the Longji Rice Terraces

Take a day trip to the Longji Rice Terraces, where you can hike through stunning layered fields and visit traditional Zhuang and Yao villages. Hostels can help arrange transportation and guides.

Street Food Adventures

Guilin’s night markets are a food lover’s paradise. Don’t miss trying local specialties like beer fish, snail noodles, and sticky rice cakes. Your hostel staff can point you to the best stalls.

Bamboo Rafting on the Yulong River

For a more peaceful alternative to the crowded Li River cruises, head to the Yulong River for a bamboo raft ride. It’s cheaper and offers equally beautiful views.

Making the Most of Your Stay

Guilin’s hostels are more than just accommodation—they’re gateways to experiences. Take advantage of the social events, ask for recommendations, and be open to spontaneous adventures. Whether you’re swapping travel stories in the common room or watching the sunrise over the karst mountains with new friends, these moments will define your trip.

So pack your bag, book your bunk, and get ready to explore one of China’s most beautiful regions without breaking the bank. Guilin is waiting for you.
