How to Plan a Multi-City Trip from Shanghai to Guilin

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Planning a multi-city trip from Shanghai to Guilin is an exciting way to experience China’s diverse landscapes, rich culture, and vibrant urban life. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, this itinerary will help you explore must-see destinations while avoiding common pitfalls. Below is a step-by-step guide to crafting the perfect journey, complete with transportation tips, accommodation recommendations, and hidden gems along the way.

Step 1: Choose Your Route and Destinations

The most popular route from Shanghai to Guilin includes stops in Hangzhou, Huangshan (Yellow Mountain), and Yangshuo. Each city offers unique attractions:

Hangzhou: The Paradise of West Lake

  • West Lake (Xi Hu): Rent a bike or take a boat ride to explore this UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Longjing Tea Plantations: Sip authentic Dragon Well tea while enjoying the serene countryside.
  • Lingyin Temple: A historic Buddhist temple surrounded by lush forests.

Huangshan: Majestic Peaks and Ancient Villages

  • Yellow Mountain (Huangshan): Hike or take a cable car to witness sunrise over sea of clouds.
  • Hongcun and Xidi: Traditional Anhui villages with well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.

Yangshuo: Karst Landscapes and Outdoor Adventures

  • Li River Cruise: The iconic stretch from Guilin to Yangshuo offers breathtaking views.
  • Bamboo Rafting on Yulong River: A quieter alternative to the Li River.
  • Moon Hill: A short hike rewards you with panoramic views of the karst formations.

Step 2: Transportation Options

High-Speed Trains: Fast and Comfortable

  • Shanghai to Hangzhou: 1 hour by train.
  • Hangzhou to Huangshan: 2.5 hours by train.
  • Huangshan to Guilin: Fly or take an overnight train (consider breaking the journey in Nanchang).

Domestic Flights: Save Time

  • Direct flights from Shanghai to Guilin take around 2.5 hours. However, flying may skip scenic train routes.

Local Transport: Buses and Taxis

  • In smaller towns like Yangshuo, renting an e-bike or using ride-hailing apps (Didi) is convenient.

Step 3: Accommodation Picks

Hangzhou: Lakeside Luxury

  • Four Seasons Hangzhou: A lavish retreat near West Lake.
  • Budget Option: Hangzhou Mingtown International Youth Hostel.

Huangshan: Mountain Retreats

  • Banyan Tree Huangshan: A luxury resort with traditional Hui-style architecture.
  • Budget Option: Huangshan International Youth Hostel.

Guilin/Yangshuo: Riverside Stays

  • Alila Yangshuo: A boutique hotel set in a restored sugar mill.
  • Budget Option: Yangshuo Outside Inn.

Step 4: Must-Try Local Dishes

Hangzhou

  • Dongpo Pork: Braised pork belly named after the poet Su Dongpo.
  • West Lake Vinegar Fish: Sweet and sour freshwater fish.

Huangshan

  • Stinky Tofu (Chou Doufu): An acquired taste but a local favorite.
  • Huizhou Smoked Mandarin Fish: A smoky, savory delicacy.

Guilin/Yangshuo

  • Guilin Rice Noodles (Mifen): Served with pickled vegetables and peanuts.
  • Beer Fish (Pijiu Yu): A Yangshuo specialty cooked with local beer.

Step 5: Off-the-Beaten-Path Experiences

Hangzhou

  • Xixi Wetland Park: A peaceful alternative to West Lake’s crowds.

Huangshan

  • Bishan Village: A lesser-known rural escape with artisanal workshops.

Yangshuo

  • Tai Chi or Cooking Classes: Learn from locals in a scenic setting.

Step 6: Packing Tips

  • Layered Clothing: Mountainous areas like Huangshan can be chilly even in summer.
  • Comfortable Shoes: Essential for hiking and exploring ancient villages.
  • Rain Gear: Southern China is prone to sudden showers.

Step 7: Budgeting and Money-Saving Tips

  • Travel During Shoulder Seasons: April-May and September-October offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds.
  • Use Public Transport: High-speed trains are affordable and efficient.
  • Eat Like a Local: Street food and small eateries are budget-friendly and delicious.

Step 8: Cultural Etiquette

  • Temple Visits: Dress modestly and avoid loud behavior.
  • Tipping: Not expected, but appreciated in upscale restaurants.
  • Bargaining: Common in markets, but do so politely.

By following this guide, you’ll create a memorable multi-city adventure that blends urban sophistication, natural wonders, and cultural immersion. Happy travels!
