Best Locations for Capturing Natural Scenery in Guilin

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog

Guilin, renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, offers photographers a paradise of natural beauty. With its iconic karst mountains, serene rivers, and vibrant countryside, the city is a dream destination for nature photography. Here are some of the top spots in Guilin to capture stunning natural vistas.

Li River (Lijiang)

The Li River is perhaps the most iconic location in Guilin. Its picturesque landscapes of karst peaks and tranquil waters have inspired countless artists and photographers.

  • Highlights: The "Nine Horses Fresco Hill" and "Reflection of Yellow Cloth Shoal" are famous stops along the river.
  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning for the ethereal mist rising from the water or sunset for warm, golden hues.
  • Photography Tip: Use a boat to explore various angles, and try shooting during the blue hour for a dramatic effect.

Yangshuo Countryside

The countryside around Yangshuo is a treasure trove of natural beauty. Rice fields, rivers, and dramatic limestone peaks create an idyllic setting.

  • Key Spots: Yulong River and Moon Hill are must-visits.
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and autumn for lush greenery and clear skies.
  • Photography Tip: Rent a bicycle to access hidden spots and get authentic rural shots.

Xianggong Hill

Xianggong Hill provides one of the best panoramic views of the Li River winding through the karst mountains.

  • Highlights: Sunrise is especially magical here, as the valley fills with a soft mist.
  • Best Time to Visit: Arrive early to catch the sunrise and avoid crowds.
  • Photography Tip: Use a wide-angle lens to capture the vast landscape.

Reed Flute Cave

While not an outdoor location, Reed Flute Cave offers a fascinating underground world of stalactites and stalagmites illuminated by colorful lights.

  • Best Features: The lake inside the cave reflects the rock formations beautifully.
  • Best Time to Visit: Late afternoon to combine it with nearby outdoor shoots.
  • Photography Tip: Bring a tripod for longer exposures in the low light.

Longji Rice Terraces (Longsheng)

Longji, or "Dragon's Backbone," Rice Terraces are a marvel of agricultural engineering and a stunning natural attraction.

  • Key Features: The terraces look different in each season, from lush green in summer to golden in autumn.
  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning for sunrise or late afternoon for golden hour.
  • Photography Tip: Include local farmers in your composition for a cultural touch.

Seven Star Park

This large park in Guilin city is a convenient location for nature photography, featuring karst hills, a zoo, and caves.

  • Highlights: The Camel Hill and the Flower Bridge are particularly photogenic.
  • Best Time to Visit: Morning when the light is soft and the park is less crowded.
  • Photography Tip: Capture reflections in the lake for symmetrical compositions.

Elephant Trunk Hill

Elephant Trunk Hill is Guilin’s most recognizable landmark, shaped like an elephant drinking from the Li River.

  • Best Angle: Shoot from the opposite riverbank to capture the hill and its reflection.
  • Best Time to Visit: Evening, as the hill is beautifully lit.
  • Photography Tip: Experiment with long exposures to capture light trails or smooth water.

Daxu Ancient Town

Daxu is a charming riverside town with well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.

  • Key Features: Stone-paved streets, ancient bridges, and traditional wooden houses.
  • Best Time to Visit: Morning for soft light and fewer tourists.
  • Photography Tip: Focus on details, such as intricate wood carvings or local life.

Gudong Waterfall Scenic Area

Gudong Waterfall offers a dynamic setting with cascading waters and lush greenery.

  • Best Features: The multi-level waterfalls are surrounded by dense forest.
  • Best Time to Visit: After rain for fuller waterfalls.
  • Photography Tip: Use a neutral density filter to create silky water effects.

Yao Mountain

Yao Mountain, the highest peak in Guilin, provides panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscapes.

  • Highlights: In winter, the mountain is sometimes covered in snow, a rare sight in southern China.
  • Best Time to Visit: Sunset for a golden glow over the city.
  • Photography Tip: Bring a telephoto lens to capture distant peaks.

Crown Cave (Guanyan)

Crown Cave combines natural beauty with adventure, featuring a subterranean river and dramatic rock formations.

  • Key Attractions: Boat rides inside the cave offer unique photographic opportunities.
  • Best Time to Visit: Any time, as it is an indoor attraction.
  • Photography Tip: Use artificial light carefully to enhance the natural textures.

Xingping Town

Xingping is home to the famous "20-Yuan Note Viewpoint," showcasing a classic Li River scene.

  • Key Spots: Laozhai Hill provides a bird's-eye view of the area.
  • Best Time to Visit: Sunrise for breathtaking colors and morning mist.
  • Photography Tip: Include a traditional bamboo raft in your frame for a quintessential Guilin shot.

Fubo Hill

Fubo Hill, located along the Li River, offers unique rock formations and excellent vantage points.

  • Highlights: The summit provides stunning views of the river and city.
  • Best Time to Visit: Late afternoon for warm light and fewer shadows.
  • Photography Tip: Experiment with drone photography for creative angles.

Caoping Town and Lotus Cave

Caoping Town, near the Li River, is known for its pastoral charm and the stunning Lotus Cave.

  • Key Features: The cave's formations resemble blooming lotuses, adding a poetic touch to your shots.
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and summer for vibrant landscapes.
  • Photography Tip: Focus on macro details like flowers and textures.

Wuzhi Hill

Wuzhi Hill, also known as Five Fingers Hill, provides a unique silhouette against the skyline.

  • Best Time to Visit: Sunset for dramatic backlighting.
  • Photography Tip: Capture the hill from across the Li River for an unobstructed view.

Guilin’s natural beauty is unparalleled, offering photographers endless opportunities to capture its magic. Whether you’re shooting iconic landmarks or hidden gems, every location in this city promises a unique perspective on its timeless landscapes.
