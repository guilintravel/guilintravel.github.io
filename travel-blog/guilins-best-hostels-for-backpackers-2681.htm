Guilin’s Best Hostels for Backpackers

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its surreal karst landscapes, winding Li River, and vibrant local culture, is a dream destination for backpackers. Whether you’re a solo traveler seeking adventure or a budget-conscious explorer, the city offers some of the best hostels in China—each with its own unique charm. From rooftop bars with panoramic views to cozy dorm rooms with a social vibe, here’s your ultimate guide to Guilin’s top hostels for backpackers.

Why Stay in a Hostel in Guilin?

Hostels aren’t just about saving money—they’re about community, local insights, and unforgettable experiences. In Guilin, many hostels organize group tours, bike rentals, and even cooking classes, making it easy to connect with fellow travelers. Plus, with attractions like Yangshuo and Longji Rice Terraces nearby, a good hostel can be your gateway to exploring beyond the city.

Budget-Friendly Travel

Guilin’s hostels offer dorm beds for as low as $5–$10 per night, with private rooms often under $30. Compared to hotels, you’ll save cash for activities like bamboo rafting or street food feasts.

Social Atmosphere

Many hostels have communal areas, game nights, and group outings—perfect for meeting like-minded travelers.

Local Expertise

Staff often speak English and can recommend hidden gems, from secret hiking trails to the best mifen (rice noodle) spots.

Top 5 Hostels in Guilin for Backpackers

1. Wada Hostel Guilin

Location: Near Zhengyang Pedestrian Street
Why Stay Here? Wada is a favorite for its artsy vibe and prime location. The rooftop terrace offers stunning views of the karst mountains, and the bar serves cheap local beers.

Highlights:
- Free walking tours of Guilin’s old town
- On-site bike rentals for exploring the countryside
- Spacious dorms with privacy curtains

Best For: Solo travelers and digital nomads (strong Wi-Fi included).

2. Guilin Backpacker Hostel

Location: Close to Elephant Trunk Hill
Why Stay Here? This hostel feels like a home away from home, with friendly staff who go the extra mile. The common room has board games and a guitar for impromptu jam sessions.

Highlights:
- Organized trips to Yangshuo and the Li River
- Free dumpling-making workshops
- Cozy courtyard for relaxing after a day of sightseeing

Best For: Budget travelers who love a laid-back vibe.

3. Riverside Hostel

Location: Along the Li River, near the night market
Why Stay Here? Wake up to river views and the sound of fishing boats. The hostel’s café serves killer Guilin coffee and homemade banana pancakes.

Highlights:
- Kayak rentals for exploring the river
- Weekly BBQ nights with live music
- Private rooms with balconies overlooking the water

Best For: Couples or small groups who want a mix of adventure and relaxation.

4. Yangshuo Monkey Jane’s Hostel

Location: Yangshuo (a short bus ride from Guilin)
Why Stay Here? While technically in Yangshuo, this hostel is worth the trip. It’s a hub for outdoor enthusiasts, with rock climbing and cycling tours.

Highlights:
- Discounts on Yangshuo’s best activities (caving, rafting, etc.)
- Outdoor movie nights under the stars
- Helpful staff who book buses/trains for you

Best For: Adventure seekers and nature lovers.

5. Ming Palace International Youth Hostel

Location: Inside a historic building near Seven Star Park
Why Stay Here? This hostel blends modern comfort with traditional Chinese architecture. The courtyard is perfect for practicing tai chi in the morning.

Highlights:
- Free calligraphy classes
- Quiet atmosphere, great for writers or artists
- Walking distance to Guilin’s top tea houses

Best For: Culture buffs and those who prefer a quieter stay.

Insider Tips for Backpacking in Guilin

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) offer mild weather and fewer crowds. Summer is hot but great for river activities.

Must-Try Local Dishes

  • Guilin mifen (rice noodles with peanuts and chili)
  • Beer fish (a Yangshuo specialty)
  • Stuffed Li River snails

Off-the-Beaten-Path Adventures

  • Cycle through Yulong River’s bamboo forests
  • Hike to the top of Moon Hill for sunset
  • Visit Xingping for the iconic 20 RMB view

How to Choose the Right Hostel

Prioritize Location

If you want nightlife, stay near Zhengyang Street. For nature, pick a hostel closer to the river or Yangshuo.

Check Reviews for Vibes

Some hostels are party-centric, while others focus on relaxation. Read recent reviews to match your travel style.

Look for Perks

Free breakfast, bike rentals, or tour discounts can save you money in the long run.

Final Thoughts

Guilin’s hostels are more than just a place to sleep—they’re where friendships are forged, adventures begin, and memories are made. Whether you’re gazing at karst peaks from a rooftop bar or swapping travel stories over a bowl of mifen, these hostels ensure your Guilin backpacking trip is nothing short of epic.

Now, pack your bag, grab your camera, and get ready to fall in love with Guilin—one hostel at a time.
