Does Guilin Have a Subway System, and Where Can You Board It?

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a picturesque city in Guangxi known for its karst mountains and serene rivers, is a popular destination for travelers from all over the world. While its natural beauty and cultural heritage are often highlighted, many visitors also wonder about the city’s transportation infrastructure. Specifically, does Guilin have a subway system, and if so, where can you board it? Let’s explore this question in detail.

The Development of Guilin's Public Transportation

Guilin is a mid-sized city by Chinese standards, with a population of approximately five million people. Unlike mega cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, or Guangzhou, Guilin does not have a subway system as of now. Instead, its public transportation primarily relies on buses, taxis, and shared bicycles.

The city’s relatively small size and its focus on preserving the natural environment have limited the need for extensive underground transit systems. However, Guilin's public transportation network is efficient enough to cater to both locals and tourists, making it relatively easy to navigate without a subway.

Alternatives to a Subway System in Guilin

Bus Network

Coverage and Accessibility

Guilin’s bus system is extensive, covering most areas of the city and connecting major tourist attractions such as the Elephant Trunk Hill, Reed Flute Cave, and Seven Star Park. The buses are well-maintained, affordable, and frequent, making them an excellent choice for getting around.

Cost

The average fare for a bus ride in Guilin is around ¥2. For tourists, there are also special sightseeing bus lines that take you directly to popular destinations for a slightly higher fare.

Tips for Tourists

  • Have Small Bills or Coins Ready: Buses in Guilin typically don’t provide change, so it’s best to carry exact fares.
  • Use a Transportation Card: Guilin offers a reloadable bus card that can save you the hassle of carrying cash. These cards are available at major bus stations and convenience stores.

Taxis and Ride-Hailing Services

Availability

Taxis are a convenient way to travel around Guilin, especially for short distances or when public transportation isn’t readily available. They are relatively affordable compared to Western countries, with starting fares ranging from ¥8 to ¥10 depending on the time of day.

Ride-Hailing Apps

For a more modern alternative, you can use ride-hailing apps like Didi. The app offers an English version, making it user-friendly for international travelers. You can select different car options based on your budget and preferences.

Shared Bikes and Walking

Shared Bikes

Guilin is a bike-friendly city, with many shared bike stations scattered across the urban area. Companies like Mobike and Hellobike operate in the city, allowing users to rent bikes via smartphone apps. This is a fantastic option for exploring Guilin’s scenic streets at your own pace.

Walking

Due to its compact size, many of Guilin’s attractions are within walking distance of each other, particularly in the city center. Walking is not only economical but also allows you to soak in the city’s charming atmosphere.

Plans for a Subway in Guilin?

Although Guilin does not currently have a subway system, there have been discussions and feasibility studies about introducing one. As Guilin continues to grow in popularity as a tourist destination and its urban population expands, the need for a more robust public transit system may lead to the construction of a subway in the future.

Cities like Kunming and Nanning, also located in southern China, have successfully implemented subway systems in recent years, suggesting that it’s not out of the question for Guilin to follow suit. However, the environmental considerations and the city’s commitment to preserving its unique landscape could delay such plans.

Key Locations and How to Reach Them Without a Subway

Guilin Liangjiang International Airport

  • Transportation Options: Airport shuttle buses, taxis, or private car services.
  • Travel Time: The shuttle bus to the city center takes approximately 40 minutes, while a taxi ride is around 30 minutes.
  • Cost: Shuttle bus fares are about ¥20, and taxi rides cost around ¥80–¥100.

Guilin Railway Stations

Guilin has three main train stations: Guilin Station, Guilin North Station, and Guilin West Station.

  • Public Transit: All three stations are well-connected by bus routes, with frequent services throughout the day.
  • Taxi or Didi: For faster and more convenient access, taxis and ride-hailing services are readily available.

Popular Tourist Attractions

  1. Elephant Trunk Hill
    Located in the city center, this iconic attraction is easily accessible by bus or on foot if you’re staying nearby.
  2. Reed Flute Cave
    Approximately 5 kilometers from the city center, buses and taxis are the most practical ways to reach this site.
  3. Li River Cruise Dock
    Most cruises depart from Zhujiang Pier, which is about a 40-minute drive from the city center. Tour companies often provide shuttle services.

Yangshuo

Although not part of Guilin city proper, Yangshuo is a must-visit destination for many travelers. To get there:

  • Bus: Regular buses run from Guilin’s main bus station to Yangshuo, taking about 1.5 hours.
  • Private Transfer: For more comfort, consider booking a private car or Didi.

Preparing for Your Trip to Guilin

Apps and Resources

  • Maps: Use Baidu Maps or Google Maps for navigation, but note that Baidu offers better public transportation information for China.
  • Translation: Download a translation app like Google Translate to assist with communication, especially when using public transit or taxis.
  • Weather: Check the local weather forecast, as heavy rains can occasionally disrupt travel plans.

Travel Tips

  • Carry Cash: While digital payments are popular in China, cash is still widely accepted, especially on buses and in smaller shops.
  • Learn Key Phrases: Familiarize yourself with basic Mandarin phrases or use pinyin to communicate with locals.

Guilin’s lack of a subway system doesn’t hinder its charm or accessibility. With a little preparation, you can easily explore the city and its surroundings using the available transportation options. Whether you’re navigating the streets by bus, bike, or foot, Guilin offers a seamless travel experience filled with breathtaking scenery and cultural discoveries.
