Guilin Travel Map: The Most Charming Bridges and Walkways

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a jewel of China’s Guangxi region, is renowned for its surreal karst landscapes, winding rivers, and poetic scenery. But beyond the postcard-perfect views, the city’s bridges and walkways weave an enchanting narrative of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re strolling under moonlit arches or crossing centuries-old stone pathways, Guilin’s bridges and walkways offer a unique way to experience its magic.

The Iconic Bridges of Guilin

Moon Hill Bridge (Yueliang Shan Qiao)

Nestled near the famous Moon Hill, this arched bridge is a photographer’s dream. Its elegant curve mirrors the natural crescent shape of the hill, creating a harmonious blend of man-made and natural beauty. Visitors often pause here to capture the reflection of the karst peaks in the calm waters below.

Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late afternoon when the light casts golden hues over the landscape.

Liberation Bridge (Jiefang Qiao)

As one of Guilin’s oldest bridges, Liberation Bridge connects the bustling city center with the serene Li River. Built during the Ming Dynasty and later reconstructed, it’s a prime spot for watching traditional fishing boats glide by. The bridge’s mix of Chinese and Western architectural influences reflects Guilin’s historical role as a cultural crossroads.

Pro Tip: Visit at night when the bridge is illuminated, offering stunning views of the illuminated Pagoda of Seven Stars.

Dragon Bridge (Long Qiao)

This ornate bridge, adorned with dragon sculptures, pays homage to Guilin’s mythological roots. According to legend, the bridge was built to appease a river dragon that once caused floods. Today, it’s a favorite among locals for evening walks, with street vendors nearby selling youcha (oil tea), a regional specialty.

Scenic Walkways Along the Li River

Li River Promenade

Stretching alongside the iconic Li River, this walkway is a must for any visitor. Lined with banyan trees and dotted with pavilions, it’s perfect for a leisurely stroll or a morning jog. The promenade also offers easy access to boat tours, where you can witness the legendary cormorant fishing—a tradition dating back centuries.

Highlights:
- Elephant Trunk Hill Viewpoint: A short detour leads to Guilin’s most famous landmark.
- Night Markets: Nearby Zhengyang Pedestrian Street comes alive after sunset with local snacks and handicrafts.

Seven-Star Park Walkways

Guilin’s largest park, Seven-Star Park, features a network of winding paths that lead to hidden caves, lush gardens, and quiet ponds. The Flower Bridge, a historic covered walkway, is particularly charming, with its intricate carvings and vibrant floral displays.

Don’t Miss: The Guihai Stele Forest, where ancient calligraphy is etched into stone tablets.

Hidden Gems: Lesser-Known Bridges and Trails

Fubo Hill’s Hidden Footbridge

Tucked behind Fubo Hill, this narrow footbridge offers a secluded escape. The surrounding area is rich in Tang Dynasty relics, and the bridge itself feels like a step back in time. Adventurous travelers can climb the hill for panoramic views of the city.

Yulong River Bamboo Walkway

While the Yulong River is famous for its bamboo rafting, few explore its wooden walkways. These rustic paths wind through rice paddies and small villages, offering an authentic glimpse of rural Guangxi. Rent a bike in Yangshuo and follow the trail for a peaceful afternoon.

Practical Tips for Exploring Guilin’s Bridges and Walkways

  • Footwear Matters: Many paths are uneven or slippery—opt for comfortable, grippy shoes.
  • Timing is Key: Sunrise and sunset provide the best lighting for photography and fewer crowds.
  • Local Guides: Consider hiring a guide for historical insights, especially at Liberation Bridge and Seven-Star Park.

Guilin’s bridges and walkways are more than just pathways—they’re gateways to the soul of the region. Whether you’re crossing a centuries-old arch or wandering a riverside trail, each step reveals a new layer of Guilin’s timeless charm.
