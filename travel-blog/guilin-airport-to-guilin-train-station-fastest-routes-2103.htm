Guilin Airport to Guilin Train Station: Fastest Routes

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Traveling to Guilin is a dream for many tourists, thanks to its breathtaking karst landscapes, serene Li River, and vibrant cultural scene. But before you can fully immerse yourself in the beauty of this region, you’ll need to navigate the journey from Guilin Liangjiang International Airport (KWL) to Guilin Train Station. Whether you're in a hurry to catch a train or just want to minimize travel time, knowing the fastest routes can make your trip smoother.

Why Speed Matters for Travelers in Guilin

Guilin is a major transportation hub in Guangxi, connecting travelers to popular destinations like Yangshuo, Longsheng Rice Terraces, and even neighboring provinces. Many visitors arrive by air and immediately need to transfer to a train for their next adventure. Delays can mean missing a scenic train ride or losing precious exploration time.

Key Factors Affecting Travel Time

  • Traffic Conditions: Guilin’s roads can get congested, especially during peak hours.
  • Transport Mode: Taxis, buses, and private transfers vary in speed and convenience.
  • Time of Day: Early mornings and late nights often mean faster trips.

Fastest Transportation Options

1. Taxi or Ride-Hailing (Didi) – The Quickest Choice

For those prioritizing speed, a taxi or Didi (China’s Uber equivalent) is the best option.

Estimated Time: 30–40 minutes

Cost: ~¥80–120 ($12–18)

Pros:
- Direct route with no stops.
- Available 24/7.
- Comfortable for travelers with luggage.

Cons:
- Slightly more expensive than public transport.
- Possible delays during rush hour (7–9 AM, 5–7 PM).

Tip: Use Didi for fixed pricing to avoid haggling with taxi drivers.

2. Airport Shuttle Bus – Budget-Friendly & Efficient

Guilin Airport offers shuttle buses to the city center, including stops near the train station.

Estimated Time: 45–60 minutes

Cost: ¥20–30 ($3–5)

Route Details:
- Bus Line: Airport Express Line 1 (最快捷的一号线)
- Drop-off Point: Guilin Train Station Square

Pros:
- Affordable.
- Avoids traffic better than taxis in some cases.

Cons:
- Fixed schedule (may require waiting).
- Slower if multiple stops are made.

3. Private Transfer – Luxury & Convenience

For groups or travelers seeking comfort, pre-booking a private car is ideal.

Estimated Time: 30 minutes (traffic permitting)

Cost: ¥150–300 ($22–45)

Pros:
- No waiting time.
- English-speaking drivers available.

Cons:
- Higher cost.

Alternative Routes & Insider Tips

Avoiding Traffic Jams

  • Peak Hours: If arriving between 7–9 AM or 5–7 PM, consider the shuttle bus.
  • Back Routes: Some drivers take minor roads (like Xicheng Road) to bypass congestion.

Combining Transport for Efficiency

If your flight lands late at night and the shuttle isn’t running, a taxi is your only option. Conversely, daytime arrivals can save money with the bus.

Final Thoughts for Travelers

Guilin’s charm lies in its relaxed pace, but getting to your train quickly doesn’t have to be stressful. Whether you choose a taxi, shuttle, or private transfer, planning ahead ensures a seamless transition from airport to train station—leaving you more time to enjoy the stunning landscapes ahead.

Now that you know the fastest routes, all that’s left is to board your train and let Guilin’s magic unfold!
