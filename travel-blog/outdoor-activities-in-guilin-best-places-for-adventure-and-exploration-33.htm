Outdoor Activities in Guilin: Best Places for Adventure and Exploration

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog

Guilin, with its stunning karst mountain landscape, winding rivers, and lush countryside, is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The city and its surrounding areas offer a wide range of outdoor activities that allow visitors to fully immerse themselves in the natural beauty of southern China. From hiking through ancient forests and climbing limestone peaks to river cruises and cycling through scenic villages, Guilin has something for every adventurer. In this article, we will explore some of the best outdoor activity destinations in and around Guilin, highlighting the unique experiences each has to offer.

1. Li River (Li Jiang) – Scenic Cruises and Kayaking

One of the most iconic outdoor experiences in Guilin is a cruise along the Li River. Known for its dramatic karst peaks, tranquil waters, and lush surroundings, the Li River is a must-see destination for anyone visiting the region. The river flows through a landscape that has inspired generations of artists and poets, and today it remains a top attraction for travelers seeking to experience the natural beauty of Guilin.

Li River Cruise

A traditional Li River cruise is one of the best ways to take in the surreal landscape. The cruise typically starts in Guilin and ends in Yangshuo, taking you on a 4-5 hour journey through some of the most picturesque areas in the region. During the trip, you will pass by towering limestone peaks, fishing villages, and farmers working in the fields. The sight of traditional wooden boats and fishermen casting nets from bamboo rafts adds to the charm of the journey.

Kayaking on the Li River

For those who prefer a more active experience, kayaking on the Li River is a fantastic option. Many local companies offer kayak rentals, and paddling down the river gives you a closer look at the stunning karst formations and rural life along the water. Kayaking also offers more flexibility, as you can choose to paddle at your own pace and explore smaller tributaries and hidden coves that are inaccessible by larger boats.

2. Longji Rice Terraces – Hiking and Photography

Located about two hours from Guilin, the Longji Rice Terraces (also known as the Dragon’s Backbone Rice Terraces) are one of the most popular outdoor destinations in the region. These terraces, carved into the mountainside over hundreds of years, offer breathtaking views of lush, green fields and dramatic mountain landscapes. The terraces are especially beautiful during the growing season, when the fields are flooded with water, creating shimmering reflections of the sky.

Hiking in Longji

Hiking is one of the best ways to explore the Longji Rice Terraces. There are several well-marked trails that take you through the villages, allowing you to explore the terraces up close and learn about the traditional farming practices still used by the Zhuang and Yao ethnic minorities. The most popular trail leads from Ping'an Village to Dazhai Village, offering panoramic views of the terraces along the way.

Photography in Longji

The Longji Rice Terraces are a photographer's dream. The combination of terraced fields, dramatic mountain backdrops, and traditional wooden houses creates a unique and picturesque landscape. Whether you're capturing the golden hues of the rice during harvest time or the misty mornings in spring, Longji offers countless opportunities for stunning photos.

3. Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiangbi Shan) – Rock Climbing and Scenic Views

Elephant Trunk Hill is one of the most famous landmarks in Guilin, and it's a great spot for both sightseeing and outdoor activities. The hill, which resembles an elephant drinking water from the Li River, offers a variety of outdoor experiences, from rock climbing to hiking.

Rock Climbing on Elephant Trunk Hill

For outdoor adventurers, Elephant Trunk Hill offers rock climbing opportunities on its towering limestone cliffs. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced climber, the hill provides several routes with varying difficulty levels. The views from the top of the hill are absolutely breathtaking, with panoramic vistas of the city of Guilin, the Li River, and surrounding karst peaks.

Exploring the Hill and Scenic Views

If climbing isn't your thing, you can still enjoy the beauty of Elephant Trunk Hill by hiking to the summit. The hill is equipped with well-maintained paths, and the hike takes you through lush vegetation and rocky terrain. Once at the top, you will be rewarded with stunning views of Guilin’s landscape, as well as a closer look at the famous “elephant trunk” feature.

4. Yangshuo – Cycling and Outdoor Exploration

Located just an hour from Guilin, Yangshuo is a popular destination for outdoor activities, offering a wide range of options for adventure lovers. The town is surrounded by dramatic karst mountains, rice fields, and winding rivers, making it a perfect base for outdoor exploration.

Cycling in Yangshuo

Cycling is one of the most popular outdoor activities in Yangshuo, and it's easy to see why. The surrounding countryside is dotted with picturesque villages, rice paddies, and rivers, making for an idyllic setting for a bike ride. Several local companies offer bike rentals, and there are well-marked cycling routes that take you through the countryside. The Yulong River route is particularly popular, as it allows you to ride along the river while enjoying stunning views of the surrounding karst peaks.

Rock Climbing in Yangshuo

Yangshuo is also famous for its rock climbing opportunities. The region is home to some of the best limestone climbing in China, with hundreds of routes on the steep karst cliffs that surround the town. Whether you're a beginner or an advanced climber, there are routes for all skill levels, and the views from the top of the cliffs are incredible. Yangshuo also hosts several international rock climbing competitions, making it a hub for climbing enthusiasts.

5. Reed Flute Cave (Ludi Yan) – Exploring Limestone Caves

For those looking to explore the underground world of Guilin, Reed Flute Cave is a must-see destination. Known as the “natural art gallery,” Reed Flute Cave is a limestone cave that features stunning stalactites and stalagmites, many of which have been shaped over millions of years by the erosion of the surrounding rock.

Exploring the Cave

A guided tour of Reed Flute Cave takes you through a series of chambers filled with colorful mineral formations, underground rivers, and sparkling stalactites and stalagmites. The cave's unique lighting system highlights the beauty of these natural features, creating an almost otherworldly experience. The tour is suitable for people of all ages, and the cave’s cool temperatures provide a welcome respite from the heat of the outdoors.

Photography in Reed Flute Cave

For photographers, Reed Flute Cave offers a unique opportunity to capture the beauty of the underground world. The natural formations, illuminated by the cave's vibrant lights, create a surreal atmosphere that is perfect for creative photography.

6. Seven Star Park (Qixing Gongyuan) – Hiking and Leisure Activities

Seven Star Park, located near the center of Guilin, is another popular outdoor destination that combines natural beauty with recreational activities. The park is home to lush greenery, beautiful lakes, and several scenic spots, making it a great place for both active and relaxing outdoor activities.

Hiking in Seven Star Park

Seven Star Park is named after the seven peaks that are shaped like the Big Dipper constellation. The park offers several hiking trails that take visitors up the peaks, where they can enjoy panoramic views of Guilin’s landscape. The hike to the top of the Seven Star Peaks offers some of the best views of the city and surrounding karst mountains.

Leisure Activities in Seven Star Park

Aside from hiking, Seven Star Park also offers opportunities for boating on its serene lakes, as well as picnic spots, making it an excellent location for a relaxing day outdoors. Visitors can also explore the park's cultural sites, such as the Seven Star Cave and the Flower Bridge.

7. Mount Mao'er (Mao'er Shan) – Summit Hiking

Located about an hour's drive from Guilin, Mount Mao'er is the highest peak in Guilin, offering a challenging yet rewarding hiking experience. The peak stands at 2,141 meters and provides stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking to the Summit

The hike to the summit of Mount Mao'er is demanding, but for experienced hikers, it’s a thrilling challenge. The trail takes you through dense forests, rocky terrain, and alpine meadows, with the final push to the summit offering spectacular views of the surrounding karst mountains and valleys.

Camping at Mao'er Shan

For those who want to extend their outdoor adventure, camping at the summit of Mount Mao'er is a popular option. The mountain has designated camping areas, and visitors can enjoy a night under the stars with breathtaking views of the karst landscape.

Guilin offers a wide range of outdoor activities for every type of adventurer. Whether you're interested in hiking, cycling, rock climbing, or simply exploring the natural beauty of the region, the city and its surrounding areas provide endless opportunities for outdoor fun. The combination of dramatic landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and diverse activities makes Guilin a truly exceptional destination for outdoor exploration.
