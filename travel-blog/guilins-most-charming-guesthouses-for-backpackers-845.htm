Guilin’s Most Charming Guesthouses for Backpackers

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Nestled among the karst mountains and winding rivers of southern China, Guilin is a dream destination for backpackers seeking adventure, culture, and breathtaking landscapes. While luxury hotels have their appeal, the city’s guesthouses offer an intimate, budget-friendly way to experience Guilin’s charm. From rustic riverside retreats to lively social hubs, here are the most enchanting guesthouses for backpackers exploring this iconic region.

Riverside Retreats: Where Nature Meets Comfort

Yangshuo Secret Garden

Tucked away in the countryside just outside Yangshuo, the Yangshuo Secret Garden is a haven for travelers craving tranquility. This restored 19th-century farmhouse blends traditional Chinese architecture with modern comforts. Backpackers love the lush gardens, open-air courtyards, and stunning views of the limestone peaks. The owner, an avid traveler himself, often organizes bike tours to hidden villages and sunset viewpoints.

Li River Lodge

For those who want to wake up to the sound of water, Li River Lodge is a top pick. Located in Xingping, this cozy guesthouse sits right on the banks of the Li River, offering front-row seats to the famous bamboo raft scenes. The communal terrace is perfect for swapping travel stories over a beer, and the staff can arrange hiking trips to nearby Laozhai Mountain for panoramic views.

Social Hotspots: Where Backpackers Connect

Guilin Downtown Backpacker Hostel

If you’re looking to meet fellow travelers, Guilin Downtown Backpacker Hostel is the place to be. Situated in the heart of the city, this lively spot features dorm rooms, a rooftop bar, and nightly events like dumpling-making classes and pub crawls. The staff are experts at helping guests plan trips to the Longji Rice Terraces or securing last-minute tickets to the Impression Sanjie Liu light show.

Wada Hostel

A favorite among digital nomads, Wada Hostel combines work and play seamlessly. With fast Wi-Fi, a co-working space, and a café serving strong coffee, it’s ideal for those who need to stay connected. By night, the courtyard transforms into a social hub with live music and BBQ parties. The hostel also offers free walking tours of Guilin’s hidden alleyways.

Off-the-Grid Gems: For the Intrepid Explorer

Dragon’s Nest Eco-Lodge

For backpackers willing to venture off the beaten path, Dragon’s Nest Eco-Lodge is a hidden treasure. Accessible only by a short boat ride, this eco-friendly retreat is surrounded by rice paddies and bamboo forests. Guests can kayak along the Yulong River, learn to cook local dishes with a village family, or simply unwind in a hammock with a book.

Moon Hill Village Homestay

Immerse yourself in rural life at Moon Hill Village Homestay, where you’ll stay with a local family in a traditional farmhouse. The hosts are eager to share their culture, whether it’s teaching you to harvest tea or guiding you on a sunrise hike to Moon Hill’s iconic arch. It’s a raw, authentic experience far from the tourist crowds.

Budget-Friendly Luxe: Affordable Indulgences

The Giggling Tree

Don’t let the name fool you—The Giggling Tree is a slice of rustic elegance. This Dutch-owned guesthouse near Yangshuo offers private rooms with hand-carved furniture and mountain views. The on-site restaurant serves Western and Chinese fusion dishes, and the infinity pool is a rare luxury for budget-conscious travelers.

Bamboo Leaf Inn

A stylish yet affordable option, Bamboo Leaf Inn in Guilin’s old town features minimalist décor with bamboo accents. The rooftop terrace is perfect for sunset cocktails, and the staff can arrange private tours to lesser-known caves and waterfalls.

Tips for Booking Your Stay

  • Book Early in Peak Season: Guilin’s guesthouses fill up quickly from April to October.
  • Pack Light: Many guesthouses have narrow staircases or rural pathways.
  • Embrace Shared Spaces: The best connections happen in communal areas—don’t hide in your room!
  • Ask Locals for Recommendations: Some hidden gems aren’t listed online.

Whether you’re chasing waterfalls, craving cultural exchanges, or just need a cozy bed after a day of exploring, Guilin’s guesthouses offer something for every backpacker. The real magic lies in the stories you’ll collect—and the friends you’ll make—along the way.
