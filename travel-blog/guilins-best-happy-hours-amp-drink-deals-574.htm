Guilin’s Best Happy Hours & Drink Deals

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its breathtaking karst landscapes and serene Li River, is a dream destination for travelers. But beyond the postcard-perfect views, the city boasts a vibrant nightlife scene. Whether you’re sipping cocktails by the river or enjoying craft beers in a hidden alley bar, Guilin’s happy hours and drink deals are a must-experience. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best spots for cheap drinks, lively atmospheres, and unforgettable evenings.

The Best Bars for Happy Hour in Guilin

1. The Rooftop Bar at Hotel Éclat

Location: Near Elephant Trunk Hill
Happy Hour: 5 PM – 7 PM

Perched atop one of Guilin’s most luxurious hotels, this bar offers panoramic views of the city and the iconic Elephant Trunk Hill. During happy hour, enjoy 50% off signature cocktails like the Lychee Martini and Guilin Mule. Pair your drinks with tapas-style snacks while watching the sunset paint the sky in hues of orange and pink.

2. Backstreet Brews

Location: Zhengyang Pedestrian Street
Happy Hour: 4 PM – 8 PM

A haven for craft beer lovers, Backstreet Brews serves locally brewed IPAs, stouts, and lagers at half price during their extended happy hour. Try their Karst IPA—a hoppy tribute to Guilin’s famous limestone peaks. The industrial-chic decor and live acoustic music make this spot a favorite among expats and backpackers.

3. Bamboo Lounge

Location: West Street, Yangshuo
Happy Hour: 6 PM – 8 PM

Just a short drive from Guilin, Yangshuo’s West Street is packed with bars, but Bamboo Lounge stands out. Their happy hour features buy-one-get-one-free mojitos and margaritas. The bamboo-themed interior and laid-back vibe make it perfect for unwinding after a day of biking through rice terraces.

Cheap Drinks & Local Favorites

1. Sanhua Liquor Tasting at Zhengyang Street

For a taste of local culture, head to one of the traditional baijiu (Chinese liquor) shops along Zhengyang Street. Many offer free tastings of Guilin Sanhua Jiu, a fiery rice wine with a 55% ABV. It’s not for the faint-hearted, but it’s a must-try for adventurous drinkers.

2. Two Rivers & Four Lakes Beer Garden

Location: Near Sun & Moon Pagodas
Deal: 20 RMB draft beers all night

This open-air beer garden is ideal for budget travelers. Sip on cheap local brews like LiQuan Beer while admiring the illuminated pagodas reflecting on the water. The lively crowd and occasional live bands create a festive atmosphere.

3. The Hidden Alley Whiskey Bar

Location: Secret spot near Solitary Beauty Peak
Deal: Whiskey flights at 30% off before 9 PM

Tucked away in a quiet alley, this speakeasy-style bar offers an impressive selection of single malts and bourbons. The knowledgeable bartenders will guide you through a tasting flight, making it a great spot for whiskey enthusiasts.

Unique Themed Bars & Late-Night Spots

1. The Cormorant Fisherman Pub

Location: Binjiang Road
Theme: Traditional fishing culture

Named after Guilin’s iconic cormorant fishermen, this quirky bar serves cocktails in bamboo cups. Try their Cormorant’s Delight—a mix of lychee liqueur, vodka, and lime. The walls are adorned with vintage fishing gear, adding to the rustic charm.

2. Moonlight Cave Club

Location: Underground near Reed Flute Cave
Vibe: Electronic & hip-hop

For those who want to dance the night away, Moonlight Cave Club is Guilin’s top late-night destination. The cave-like interior and laser lights create a surreal party atmosphere. Arrive before midnight for discounted entry and drink specials.

3. The Tea & Cocktail Fusion Bar

Location: Inside Seven Star Park
Specialty: Tea-infused cocktails

This bar blends Guilin’s tea culture with modern mixology. Sip on a Jasmine Gin Fizz or Oolong Old Fashioned while lounging in a bamboo chair. Happy hour (3 PM – 6 PM) offers 40% off all tea cocktails.

Tips for Enjoying Guilin’s Nightlife

  • Timing is Key: Most happy hours end by 8 PM, so plan accordingly.
  • Cash vs. Card: Smaller bars may only accept cash, especially in Yangshuo.
  • Local Etiquette: Toasting with "Ganbei!" (cheers) is common—just be prepared to down your drink in one go.
  • Safety First: Stick to well-lit areas and use ride-hailing apps like Didi for late-night transport.

From rooftop cocktails to underground clubs, Guilin’s drink scene is as diverse as its landscapes. Whether you’re a budget traveler or a luxury seeker, there’s a happy hour waiting for you in this enchanting city.
