Guilin’s Top Waterfalls and Swimming Holes

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

The postcard-perfect image of Guilin is undeniable: the serene Li River, those iconic karst peaks piercing the mist, and bamboo rafts drifting peacefully. But to experience the true, untamed soul of this landscape, you must venture beyond the riverbanks and into the emerald heart of the hills. Here, where the limestone mountains catch the clouds, a network of crystalline water carves its way through ancient rock, creating some of China’s most spectacular waterfalls and secret swimming holes. This is Guilin beyond the cruise, a world of cooling mists, hidden grottoes, and natural infinity pools that offer the ultimate respite from the subtropical heat. Forget just sightseeing; it’s time to dive into an adventure.

Chasing the Cascades: Guilin’s Most Famous Falls

While Guilin’s beauty is often painted in strokes of green and grey, its waterfalls add a dynamic, roaring silver. These are not just side attractions; they are destinations that define the raw power and beauty of the Guangxi countryside.

Detian Transnational Waterfall: A Borderless Spectacle

Located about a three-hour drive from Guilin city, near the border with Vietnam, this is Asia’s largest transnational waterfall and an absolute masterpiece of nature. The Detian Falls are a sprawling, multi-tiered cascade where the Guichun River plummets over 70 meters in a thunderous display. The best part? You can experience it from both sides. Take a bamboo raft that gets you so close to the main cascade that the spray soaks you through—a welcome free shower. The surrounding karst forest, often shrouded in rainbows on sunny days, makes the journey worth every minute. It’s a full-day trip, but the sheer scale and power are unparalleled.

Xianggong Mountain’s Hidden Veil: The Waterfalls of Yangshuo’s Backyard

While Xianggong Mountain is famed for its sunrise view over a sea of peaks, the trails on its reverse side lead to seasonal wonders. After substantial rain, numerous slender waterfalls emerge from the cliff faces, streaming down like liquid threads against the green moss. They may not be permanent giants, but finding these ephemeral cascades during a summer hike feels like discovering a secret the mountains only tell when they choose to.

Dive In: The Ultimate Natural Swimming Holes

The real magic of Guilin’s water world lies in its swimmable oases. These are places where the journey—a hike, a scooter ride, a local boat trip—is part of the reward, culminating in a plunge into nature’s own perfectly tempered pools.

Moon Hill’s Secret Pool: A Local Favorite

Just a short bicycle or scooter ride from Yangshuo’s West Street, past the famous lunar-arched hill, lies a network of villages and fields. Ask a local to point you toward “Shuilong Tan” or simply follow the sound of happy splashing. Here, a wide, calm section of the Yulong River forms a perfect swimming hole. The water is jade-green and refreshing, with a soft, sandy bottom. The view is the classic Guilin panorama: water buffalo grazing, bamboo groves, and those majestic peaks all around. It’s easily accessible yet feels a world away from the tourist bustle.

The Blue Pools of Longji Rice Terraces

A visit to the magnificent Longji (Dragon’s Backbone) Rice Terraces isn’t just about panoramic views. The mountain streams that feed these ancient agricultural wonders have carved out deep, stunningly blue pools in the valleys below. Near the Ping’an and Dazhai villages, you can find spots where the water collects in clear, cold basins perfect for a post-hike cool-down. Swimming here, surrounded by the towering, manicured terraces, is a unique experience that connects you to the lifeblood of this entire ecosystem.

Underground Serenity: The Cave Pools of Lipu

For the truly adventurous, the county of Lipu, within Guilin’s administrative area, offers otherworldly experiences. Places like the “Water Cave” involve guided tours where you don a helmet and headlamp to wade and swim through illuminated underground rivers. The water is cool and eerily clear, flowing through caverns adorned with stalactites and stalagmites. It’s less a traditional swim and more a spelunking aquatic adventure, highlighting the incredible geological forces that shaped this region.

Beyond the Swim: Crafting Your Water-Focused Guilin Adventure

To make the most of these aquatic treasures, a little planning goes a long way. This isn’t just about finding a spot on a map; it’s about embracing a style of travel.

Timing is Everything: Seasons and Safety

The ideal season for waterfall power and warm swims is late spring through early autumn (May to October). Summer brings the heaviest water flow and the most reliable swimming temperatures. However, always be cautious after heavy rains, as water levels can rise rapidly and currents strengthen. The dry season (winter) reveals different beauty, but swimming becomes chilly and some waterfalls reduce to a trickle. Always check local conditions, never swim alone in remote areas, and respect all safety signs and barriers.

Getting There: The Journey as Part of the Fun

Renting an electric scooter or bicycle in Yangshuo is the ultimate freedom for exploring nearby holes like those near Moon Hill. For farther-flung destinations like Detian or the rice terraces, hiring a private driver for the day is the most efficient and comfortable option. Many local tour operators in Guilin and Yangshuo now offer specific “waterfall and swimming” day trips, which take the logistics out of your hands. For the truly intrepid, linking several spots along the Yulong River via a combination of biking, bamboo rafting, and hiking makes for an unforgettable multi-activity day.

Embracing the "Cool" Culture

This pursuit of natural water fun taps directly into a growing global travel trend: the desire for active, immersive, and Instagram-worthy experiences that also provide relief from the heat. Pack a waterproof phone case, a quick-dry towel, and sturdy water shoes (essential for rocky entries). Support the local economy by buying fresh fruit like lychee or mango from village vendors on your way, and always practice Leave No Trace principles—take all your trash out with you to keep these paradises pristine.

The soul of Guilin has always been defined by water. It’s the gentle force that shaped the peaks and the serene canvas of its most famous paintings. But by seeking out its waterfalls and swimming holes, you engage with that element in its most joyful and dynamic form. You trade passive observation for active participation, feeling the cool spray on your face and the embrace of its ancient pools. So, after you’ve taken that iconic Li River photo, grab your swimsuit, chart a course inland, and dive headfirst into the refreshing, hidden heart of Guilin. The mountains aren’t just to be looked at; here, they’re the backdrop for your own perfect, watery adventure.
