The Most Scenic Hikes in Longji Rice Terraces

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

The first glimpse is the one that steals your breath. As your vehicle winds up the serpentine mountain road in Guangxi, a sea of green and gold curves suddenly erupts into view, scaling the slopes like colossal steps for giants. This is the Longji (Dragon’s Backbone) Rice Terraces, a 700-year-old masterpiece of agricultural engineering and sheer human perseverance carved into the mountains by the Zhuang and Yao peoples. While the panoramic viewpoint photos are iconic, the true soul of Longji is not found in a single snapshot, but in the rhythm of your footsteps on its ancient stone paths. This is a landscape meant to be walked, where every turn reveals a new perspective, a hidden village, and a deeper connection to a living cultural heritage. Forget the rushed tour groups; lace up your hiking boots. We’re mapping out the most scenic hikes that will take you from famous vistas into the heart of the terraces themselves.

Choosing Your Gateway: Ping’an vs. Dazhai

Your hiking adventure begins with a choice of two primary entry points, each offering a distinct flavor and trail network. Understanding this is key to planning your scenic journey.

Ping’an Zhuang Village: The Classic Introduction

Ping’an is the more developed and accessible of the two, famous for its quintessential, picture-perfect terrace views. The hikes here are well-trodden and clearly marked, making it ideal for first-time visitors or those with limited time. The village itself, with its traditional stilted wooden houses, clings to the mountainside. The trails here are a series of interconnected stone paths leading to the legendary viewpoints.

  • The "Seven Stars Around the Moon" Trail: This is the premier short hike in Ping’an. It connects the two most famous vistas: "Nine Dragons and Five Tigers" and "Seven Stars Around the Moon." The hike itself is a moderate climb on stone steps, taking you through terraces, past small streams, and alongside working farmers. From "Nine Dragons and Five Tigers," the terraces resemble the sinuous bodies of dragons descending the slopes. The "Seven Stars" view features seven small, rounded hilltops surrounded by terraced water, resembling stars hugging a moon. The hike between them offers constantly shifting, intimate views of the zigzagging fields.
  • Scenic Profile: Polished, iconic, panoramic. Perfect for sunrise and sunset photography. Expect more visitors, especially during peak seasons.

Dazhai & Jinkeng Red Yao Terraces: The Grand & Untamed

For the serious hiker and landscape enthusiast, Dazhai is the undisputed champion. The Jinkeng Terraces here are larger, steeper, and often considered more spectacular. This area is home to the Red Yao people, recognizable by the women’s vibrant pink and embroidered traditional dress and famously long hair. The hiking here is more extensive and rewarding.

  • The Cable Car & Summit Circuit: A cable car ascends from Dazhai village to Golden Buddha Peak (Jinfo Ding), the highest viewpoint. The most scenic strategy is to hike up and cable car down (or vice-versa). The hiking trail to the summit is a strenuous but breathtaking climb through dense forests and alongside towering terraces. From the top, on a clear day, the view is staggering—layer upon layer of terraces fan out across multiple valleys, a true "dragon’s backbone" spectacle.
  • The "Thousand-Layer Steps to the Clouds": This network of trails connects Dazhai to its smaller, more authentic satellite villages like Tiantouzhai and Zhuangjie. This hike is an immersion. You’ll pass through quiet hamlets, over ancient stone bridges, and across terraces where the only sound is the rushing water of the irrigation system. It’s less about a single viewpoint and more about the continuous, awe-inspiring scenery around every corner.

Crafting Your Perfect Scenic Hike Itinerary

The Epic Cross-Terraces Trek: Ping’an to Dazhai

For the ultimate Longji experience, the 4-5 hour hike between Ping’an and Dazhai is legendary. This full-day trek traverses the mountain ridges, connecting the two terrace systems. You’ll walk through dense bamboo forests, descend into quiet valleys, pass by remote Yao settlements where time seems to stand still, and enjoy unparalleled vistas few tourists see. It’s moderately challenging but incredibly rewarding. You’ll need to arrange transport for your luggage to meet you at your destination village. This hike embodies the complete Longji narrative—from the classic to the grand, all on foot.

Chasing the Light: Sunrise & Sunset Hikes

The terraces transform with the light. Planning your hikes around dawn and dusk is non-negotiable for photographers and romantics.

  • Sunrise in Dazhai: Head to No.1 or No.2 Sunrise Viewing Platform in Tiantouzhai (Dazhai area). Watching the first rays of sun ignite the morning mist in the valley, slowly illuminating the water-filled terraces, is a mystical experience. The layers of mountains fade into a dreamlike blue background.
  • Sunset in Ping’an: The "Nine Dragons and Five Tigers" viewpoint faces west, making it ideal for sunset. As the sun dips, it casts long, dramatic shadows across the curves, turning the paddies into a patchwork of gold, amber, and deep green.

The Living Culture: More Than Just a Landscape

The scenic beauty is inextricably linked to the people who maintain it. Your hike is a journey through a living museum. You might encounter Yao women weaving by the path, their hair coiled with remarkable length, or Zhuang farmers tending to the water buffalo. The intricate irrigation system, a marvel of sustainable engineering, is a constant companion on your walk. Time your visit with the agricultural cycle for a different kind of scenery: water-filled terraces like mirrors in April/May, vibrant green shoots in June/July, golden ripe rice in September/October, and the stark, frost-etched geometric patterns in winter. Each season offers a radically different, equally scenic hike.

Travel Smart: Hiking the Dragon's Backbone

  • Footwear is King: Sturdy, broken-in hiking shoes with good grip are essential. Paths are often steep, uneven stone steps that can be slippery, especially after rain.
  • Pack Layers: Mountain weather is fickle. Mornings are cool, midday can be warm and humid, and temperatures drop quickly. A rain jacket is always a wise inclusion.
  • Hydration & Fuel: Carry ample water and snacks. While small vendors exist in the villages, they are sparse on the trails between them.
  • Respect the Landscape: Stay on marked paths to avoid damaging the delicate terrace edges. This is both a scenic treasure and a vital food source for the community.
  • Consider a Guide: For the cross-terraces trek or to delve deeper into the cultural stories, a local guide from one of the villages is invaluable. They’ll share insights you’d never find in a guidebook and ensure you don’t miss hidden trails.

The Longji Rice Terraces ask for your engagement. They reward the traveler who is willing to trade the comfort of a bus seat for the burn in their calves, who seeks not just a view but a journey. As you stand on a quiet path, the only sound the wind rustling through rice stalks and the distant call of a farmer, you’ll understand. This is not a static painting. It’s a breathing, growing, monumental testament to harmony between humans and nature, and every step you take is a line in its ongoing story. So, load your camera, but more importantly, prepare your legs and your heart for a walk through one of China’s most profoundly scenic wonders.
