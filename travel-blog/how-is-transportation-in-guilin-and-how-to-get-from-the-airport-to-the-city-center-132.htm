How Is Transportation in Guilin and How to Get from the Airport to the City Center

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, located in southern China’s Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, is known for its stunning karst mountain landscapes and beautiful rivers. It is a popular travel destination, drawing visitors from around the world who are eager to explore its scenic spots, such as the Li River, Reed Flute Cave, and Elephant Trunk Hill. While the city is relatively small compared to megacities like Shanghai or Beijing, its transportation infrastructure is well-developed and efficient, making it easy for travelers to navigate the area.

A key consideration for visitors arriving in Guilin is how to get from the airport to the city center and what transportation options are available within the city itself. Whether you are taking a taxi, a shuttle bus, or using public transport, Guilin offers a range of convenient and affordable ways to explore the city.

Guilin Liangjiang International Airport

Guilin Liangjiang International Airport (IATA: KWL) is the main gateway for travelers arriving by air. Located approximately 28 kilometers (17 miles) southwest of the city center, it connects Guilin with major domestic cities like Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and international hubs like Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, and Seoul.

Facilities at the Airport

The airport is modern and easy to navigate, with facilities including restaurants, duty-free shops, and currency exchange counters. English signs are present throughout the airport, making it accessible for international visitors. Upon arrival, you’ll find multiple transportation options to reach the city center.

Options to Get from the Airport to the City Center

1. Airport Shuttle Buses

The most popular and budget-friendly way to travel from Guilin Liangjiang International Airport to the city center is via the airport shuttle bus. These buses are reliable, spacious, and ideal for travelers who are carrying luggage.

  • Route and Stops:
    The shuttle bus service typically runs from the airport to Guilin’s main landmarks and transportation hubs, such as the Guilin Railway Station and major hotels. Depending on the route, you may find multiple stops along the way.
  • Schedule:
    Shuttle buses operate frequently, with departures every 30 to 40 minutes during peak hours. The service runs from early morning until the arrival of the last flight at night.
  • Duration:
    The journey to the city center takes approximately 45 to 60 minutes, depending on traffic conditions.
  • Cost:
    A one-way ticket costs around 20 RMB (approximately $3 USD). You can purchase tickets at the airport or directly from the bus driver.

Tip: If you’re staying at a specific hotel in Guilin, check if they offer free or private shuttle bus services from the airport. Many hotels, particularly those catering to tourists, provide airport transfer options.

2. Taxis and Ride-Hailing Services

For travelers who prefer a faster and more direct option, taxis are widely available outside the terminal. Taxis in Guilin are metered, and the fare to the city center is generally reasonable.

  • Cost:
    A taxi ride from the airport to the city center costs between 80 to 120 RMB (approximately $12 to $18 USD), depending on your destination and traffic conditions. Tolls and airport surcharges may apply, but they are minimal.
  • Duration:
    A taxi ride to downtown Guilin typically takes around 30 to 45 minutes.
  • How to Get a Taxi:
    Official taxis are located at designated taxi stands outside the terminal. Avoid unofficial drivers offering rides inside the terminal, as their rates may be unregulated and significantly higher.

Ride-Hailing Apps:
If you’re comfortable using apps, platforms like DiDi (similar to Uber) are a popular and affordable alternative to taxis. You can easily book a ride through the app, and some versions even offer an English interface for international users. Ride-hailing fares are similar to taxi rates and can often be slightly cheaper during non-peak hours.

3. Private Car Services

For a stress-free and luxurious arrival, you can pre-arrange a private car service. This option is ideal for families, groups, or travelers with significant luggage. Many travel agencies and hotels offer private airport transfers that include an English-speaking driver and assistance with luggage.

  • Cost:
    Private car services typically range from 150 to 300 RMB ($22 to $45 USD) depending on the type of vehicle and the provider.
  • Benefits:
    You get the convenience of a direct transfer, guaranteed availability, and personalized service.

4. Public Buses

While public buses are not a common option for travelers arriving at the airport, there are a few city buses that connect the airport with various districts in Guilin. This option is not recommended for first-time visitors or those with heavy luggage, as the bus stops can be less convenient and schedules less reliable.

Getting Around Guilin

Once you’ve arrived in the city center, Guilin offers an array of transportation options to help you explore its attractions with ease.

1. Taxis

Taxis are plentiful and affordable in Guilin. The starting fare is 9 RMB (about $1.30 USD), and fares increase based on distance. For short trips around the city, taxis are a convenient option. Drivers generally use meters, but it’s always a good idea to confirm before starting your ride.

2. Public Buses

Guilin’s bus network is extensive, connecting key attractions, neighborhoods, and nearby towns. Buses are very affordable, with fares typically ranging from 1 to 2 RMB per ride. While English signage is limited on most buses, routes and stops are often displayed clearly, making it easy for visitors to navigate.

3. Bicycles and Bike-Sharing Services

Guilin’s flat terrain and scenic roads make it an ideal city for cycling. Bike-sharing services like Mobike and HelloBike are widely available, allowing visitors to rent bikes through smartphone apps. This is a great option for exploring parks, lakes, and the city’s quieter streets at your own pace.

4. Walking

Downtown Guilin is compact and pedestrian-friendly, making it easy to explore on foot. Many attractions, such as the Two Rivers and Four Lakes Scenic Area, are located close to each other. Walking along the Li River and through the city’s parks provides an excellent opportunity to enjoy Guilin’s natural beauty.

5. River Cruises

For travelers looking to visit nearby towns like Yangshuo, river cruises along the Li River are a popular and scenic option. Boats depart from Guilin’s docks and offer a relaxing way to experience the stunning karst landscapes that the region is famous for.

Traveling Between Guilin and Nearby Areas

Guilin serves as a transportation hub for exploring nearby destinations like Yangshuo, Longji Rice Terraces, and Xingping Ancient Town.

High-Speed Trains

Guilin’s high-speed railway stations (Guilin Railway Station and Guilin North Railway Station) connect the city to other major cities in China. High-speed trains are modern, comfortable, and ideal for longer journeys.

Long-Distance Buses

Long-distance buses operate from Guilin Bus Station, offering affordable and convenient travel to nearby attractions. This is a good option for reaching scenic spots like the Longji Rice Terraces.

Tours and Private Drivers

For a hassle-free experience, consider booking organized tours or hiring a private driver. This allows you to explore multiple attractions at your own pace and eliminates the stress of navigating local transportation.

Guilin’s transportation system, combined with its natural beauty and cultural richness, makes it a delightful destination for travelers. Whether you’re arriving from the airport, exploring the city center, or venturing out to nearby areas, you’ll find plenty of options to suit your needs and budget. From modern airport shuttle buses to traditional bike rides along the Li River, getting around Guilin is both convenient and enjoyable.
