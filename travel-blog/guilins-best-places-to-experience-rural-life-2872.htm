Guilin’s Best Places to Experience Rural Life

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its iconic karst mountains and winding rivers, is often celebrated for its postcard-perfect landscapes. But beyond the well-trodden tourist paths lies a quieter, equally enchanting side of the region—its rural villages. Here, time moves slower, traditions thrive, and nature takes center stage. If you’re looking to escape the crowds and immerse yourself in authentic countryside living, these are the best places in Guilin to experience rural life.

The Charm of Guilin’s Countryside

Guilin’s rural areas offer a stark contrast to the bustling city center. Rolling rice terraces, ancient wooden houses, and mist-covered hills create a scene straight out of a traditional Chinese painting. The locals, mostly belonging to ethnic minority groups like the Zhuang and Yao, maintain centuries-old customs, from hand-weaving textiles to farming with water buffalo.

For travelers seeking a deeper connection with nature and culture, these villages provide an unfiltered glimpse into a way of life that has remained largely unchanged for generations.

Top Villages for an Authentic Rural Experience

1. Longji Rice Terraces (Longsheng)

No visit to Guilin’s countryside is complete without witnessing the breathtaking Longji Rice Terraces. Carved into the mountains over 600 years ago, these cascading fields resemble dragon scales—hence the name "Dragon’s Backbone."

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (water-filled terraces reflect the sky) and autumn (golden harvest season).
  • Must-Do Activities:
    • Hike through Ping’an Village and Dazhai Village for different terrace perspectives.
    • Stay overnight in a traditional wooden guesthouse.
    • Try local dishes like bamboo rice and sticky rice wine.

2. Xingping Ancient Town

While not entirely rural, Xingping offers a perfect blend of history and countryside charm. This 1,500-year-old fishing village sits along the Li River and is famous for its well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.

  • Why Visit?
    • The backdrop of the 20 RMB note’s scenery.
    • Less commercialized than Yangshuo.
    • Easy access to rural bike trails and bamboo rafting.

3. Yangshuo’s Yulong River Valley

Yangshuo is a hotspot for tourists, but venture just outside the town, and you’ll find serene villages along the Yulong River. This area is ideal for cycling, farming experiences, and slow-paced exploration.

  • Highlights:
    • Bike through Jiuxian Village and stop at hidden cafes.
    • Try your hand at farming with local families.
    • Take a bamboo raft down the Yulong River for a peaceful alternative to the Li River.

4. Daxu Ancient Town

One of Guilin’s best-kept secrets, Daxu is a riverside village frozen in time. Cobblestone streets, old tea houses, and artisan workshops make it a haven for culture lovers.

  • Unique Experiences:
    • Watch traditional bean curd making.
    • Visit the 400-year-old Gaozhai Residence.
    • Stroll along the river at sunset.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Gems

1. Huangluo Yao Village

Home to the Red Yao women, known for their extraordinarily long hair (often over 6 feet!), this village offers a rare cultural immersion.

  • What to Expect:
    • Hair-washing ceremonies using fermented rice water.
    • Handmade embroidery workshops.
    • Homestays with Yao families.

2. Fuli Town

Famous for its hand-painted fans and lanterns, Fuli is an artisan’s paradise. The town’s riverside setting adds to its rustic appeal.

  • Don’t Miss:
    • Learning fan-painting from local craftsmen.
    • The weekly market where farmers trade fresh produce.

Tips for Experiencing Rural Guilin

  • Respect Local Customs: Always ask before taking photos of villagers.
  • Travel Slow: Rent a bike or walk to fully absorb the scenery.
  • Seasonal Awareness: Some villages are more vibrant during harvest festivals.
  • Pack Light: Many rural guesthouses have basic amenities.

Whether you’re hiking through terraced fields, learning ancient crafts, or simply sipping tea by a quiet river, Guilin’s countryside promises an unforgettable escape from modern life.
