How Are Hygiene Conditions in Guilin, and Should You Bring Personal Toiletries?

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog

Guilin, a city known for its stunning karst landscapes and rich cultural heritage, is a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists. However, when planning your trip, questions about hygiene and the need for personal toiletries often arise. Here’s a detailed guide to help you understand Guilin’s hygiene standards and determine whether packing personal toiletries is necessary.

General Hygiene Conditions in Guilin

Public Spaces

In general, Guilin’s public spaces, especially tourist areas, maintain a decent level of cleanliness. Municipal cleaning teams regularly maintain streets, parks, and popular attractions like Elephant Trunk Hill and the Reed Flute Cave. However, the cleanliness of public spaces in less tourist-heavy areas may vary.

Restaurants and Street Food Stalls

  • Restaurants: Most mid-range and high-end restaurants in Guilin adhere to good hygiene practices. Kitchens are typically visible in some establishments, allowing customers to assess the cleanliness.
  • Street Food: Guilin’s street food scene is vibrant and flavorful, but hygiene standards can vary widely. To minimize risks, choose stalls with a high turnover of customers, as this often indicates fresh ingredients.

Hotels and Accommodation

Guilin offers a wide range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget guesthouses.

  • High-End Hotels: International and high-end local hotels usually meet or exceed global hygiene standards. Rooms are cleaned daily, and bathrooms are well-maintained.
  • Mid-Range Hotels: These establishments are generally clean, though amenities may be simpler.
  • Budget Guesthouses: Hygiene can be more variable in budget options. Checking reviews online is recommended before booking.

Toiletries in Guilin

What is Typically Provided?

Hotels in Guilin usually offer basic toiletries such as shampoo, conditioner, soap, and toothbrushes. However, the quality of these items can vary depending on the level of the accommodation. Luxury hotels tend to provide high-quality branded toiletries, while budget options might only supply basic items.

Should You Bring Your Own Toiletries?

  • Shampoo and Conditioner: If you are particular about your hair care products, bringing your own is a good idea. Hotel-provided shampoo and conditioner are often basic and may not suit everyone.
  • Toothbrush and Toothpaste: While these are typically provided, they are often of lower quality. Packing your own ensures comfort and reliability.
  • Body Wash and Lotion: These items may not always be included, especially in budget accommodations. Travelers with sensitive skin or specific preferences should bring their own.
  • Razor and Shaving Cream: These are rarely provided, so it’s advisable to pack them if needed.
  • Feminine Hygiene Products: These are not typically offered in accommodations. Be sure to bring your own supplies.

Travel-Sized Toiletries

If you’re traveling light or planning to stay in budget accommodations, carrying travel-sized toiletries can be a convenient solution. Stores in Guilin also sell basic toiletry items, but availability of specific brands may be limited.

Public Restrooms

Cleanliness

Public restrooms in tourist areas of Guilin, such as scenic spots and transportation hubs, are usually well-maintained. However, restrooms in less touristy areas may be less clean and lack essential supplies.

What to Bring

  • Tissue Paper: It’s common for public restrooms in China, including Guilin, not to provide toilet paper. Carrying a small pack of tissues is highly recommended.
  • Hand Sanitizer: While some restrooms offer soap, it’s not guaranteed. A pocket-sized hand sanitizer is a useful backup.

Drinking Water and Food Safety

Drinking Water

Tap water in Guilin is not potable. Most hotels provide bottled water, and it’s also widely available for purchase in convenience stores. Boiled water is commonly used for tea and cooking, and electric kettles are standard in hotel rooms.

Food Safety

To enjoy Guilin’s cuisine safely:

  • Choose restaurants and street food stalls with a high volume of customers.
  • Avoid raw or undercooked foods unless dining at a reputable establishment.
  • Use bottled or boiled water for brushing teeth and making tea.

Seasonal Considerations

Summer

High temperatures and humidity during summer months can lead to faster food spoilage. Be cautious when consuming street food or perishable items during this time.

Winter

Winter in Guilin is relatively mild but can be damp. Pack a small towel or wipes to keep hands and face dry in humid conditions.

Staying Healthy While Traveling

Vaccinations

Ensure your routine vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling to Guilin. Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for those planning to eat street food or visit rural areas.

Travel Insurance

Comprehensive travel insurance that includes medical coverage is a smart choice for any trip. This provides peace of mind in case of illness or accidents.

First Aid Kit

Consider carrying a small first aid kit with essentials like band-aids, antiseptic wipes, and medication for common ailments such as diarrhea or headaches.

Eco-Friendly Tips

To reduce waste, consider packing reusable travel bottles for toiletries and a refillable water bottle with a built-in filter. Many hotels in Guilin support eco-friendly practices, and using your own toiletries can align with these efforts.

Recommendations for Travelers

  • Research Your Accommodation: Check online reviews for feedback on hygiene and amenities.
  • Pack Essentials: Bring a few key toiletries and hygiene products to ensure comfort and peace of mind.
  • Be Prepared for Public Restrooms: Carry tissues, hand sanitizer, and possibly a small bar of soap.
  • Stay Hydrated Safely: Use bottled or boiled water for drinking and oral hygiene.

Guilin’s hygiene standards are generally adequate for travelers, especially in tourist-friendly areas. By being prepared and taking a few precautions, you can enjoy your trip to this picturesque destination without any hygiene-related concerns.
