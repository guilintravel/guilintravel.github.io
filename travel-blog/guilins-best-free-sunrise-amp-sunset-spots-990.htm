Guilin’s Best Free Sunrise & Sunset Spots

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its iconic karst landscapes and winding rivers, is a dream destination for photographers and nature lovers. While popular spots like Elephant Trunk Hill and Reed Flute Cave charge admission fees, there are plenty of free vantage points to witness breathtaking sunrises and sunsets. Whether you're a budget traveler or simply seeking hidden gems, here’s your guide to Guilin’s most stunning free sunrise and sunset spots.

Sunrise Spots in Guilin

1. Diecai Hill (叠彩山)

Diecai Hill offers one of the best panoramic views of Guilin’s skyline at sunrise. The hike is relatively short (about 20 minutes), making it accessible even for casual travelers. From the top, you’ll see the sun rising behind the mist-covered karst peaks, casting golden hues over the Li River.

Pro Tip: Arrive at least 30 minutes before sunrise to secure a good spot, especially during peak seasons.

2. Fubo Hill (伏波山)

Fubo Hill is another excellent free sunrise spot, though it’s technically inside a paid park. However, the surrounding riverside area provides unobstructed views of the morning light reflecting off the water. Early risers can also catch local fishermen setting out on their bamboo rafts—a perfect photo op.

3. Seven Star Park’s Outer Trails (七星公园外围)

While Seven Star Park itself requires an entrance fee, the outer trails along the riverbank are free and offer stunning sunrise views. The combination of water, mountains, and morning mist creates an ethereal atmosphere.

Sunset Spots in Guilin

1. Xianggong Mountain (相公山)

Though technically in Yangshuo (a short trip from Guilin), Xianggong Mountain is worth mentioning for its legendary sunset views. The climb is steep but short (15-20 minutes), and the reward is a sweeping vista of the Li River winding through karst peaks bathed in golden light.

Pro Tip: Visit on a weekday to avoid crowds—this spot has gained popularity among photographers.

2. Moon Hill Viewpoint (月亮山观景台)

Another Yangshuo gem, Moon Hill’s natural arch frames the sunset beautifully. The best free viewpoint is along the road leading to the hill, where you can capture the sun dipping behind the iconic crescent-shaped rock.

3. Yulong River Banks (遇龙河畔)

For a more serene sunset experience, head to the Yulong River. Unlike the bustling Li River, this area is quieter, with fewer tourists. Bamboo rafts drifting along the water add to the picturesque scene as the sky turns pink and orange.

Hidden Gems & Local Secrets

1. Longji Rice Terraces (龙脊梯田) – Free Entry Zones

While parts of Longji require tickets, some lesser-known trails outside the official gates offer equally stunning sunrise views over the terraced fields. Ask locals for directions to free-access viewpoints.

2. Guilin’s Riverside Walkways

The city’s riverside promenades, especially near Liberation Bridge, provide unobstructed sunset views without the crowds. It’s a great place to relax with street food from nearby vendors.

3. Local Cafés with Rooftop Views

Some cafés and hostels in Guilin have rooftop terraces open to the public. While not entirely free, buying a coffee grants you access to prime sunset-watching spots without paying for a tourist attraction.

Best Times to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Mild weather with occasional mist, adding drama to sunrise shots.
  • Autumn (September-November): Clear skies and vibrant colors at sunset.
  • Avoid Summer Midday Heat: Early mornings and late afternoons are ideal.

Photography Tips

  • Use a polarizing filter to enhance reflections on the water.
  • Arrive early to scout compositions before the light changes.
  • Experiment with long exposures for silky river effects.

Guilin’s natural beauty doesn’t have to come with a price tag. With these free sunrise and sunset spots, you can experience the magic of this landscape without spending a dime.
