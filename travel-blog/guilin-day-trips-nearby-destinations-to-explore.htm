Guilin Day Trips: Nearby Destinations to Explore

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin’s fairy-tale landscape of karst peaks and the serene Li River is the stuff of travel legend. But to experience the true soul of Guangxi, you must venture beyond the city limits. The region surrounding Guilin is a treasure trove of ancient towns, cascading terraces, and cultural encounters that are easily accessible within a day. These journeys don’t just show you more scenery; they immerse you in the rhythms of local life, ethnic heritage, and breathtaking agricultural artistry. Here is your guide to the essential day trips that will transform your Guilin visit from a postcard moment into a profound adventure.

The Iconic Escape: Yangshuo and the Yulong River

No list of Guilin day trips is complete without Yangshuo. While some choose to overnight, its proximity (about a 1.5-hour drive) makes it a perfect full-day immersion.

Cycling Through a Painting

The true magic of Yangshuo isn't just the view from West Street; it's found on the paths that snake through the countryside. Rent a bicycle or an electric scooter and set out towards the Yulong River. This is the Li River’s quieter, more intimate cousin. You’ll pedal past water buffalo grazing in fields, through small villages where farmers dry chili peppers on their doorsteps, and under the shade of ancient banyan trees. The iconic scene of the Yulong River Bridge with karst mountains in the background is a cyclist’s reward.

Bamboo Rafting on the Yulong

For a different perspective, take a bamboo raft ride on the Yulong. Unlike the larger motorized boats on the Li, these hand-poled rafts offer a serene, up-close experience with the river. Some passes include small, thrilling rapids, adding a dash of excitement. It’s a timeless way to connect with the landscape, listening to the water and the songs of local rafters.

Impression Sanjie Liu: A Nighttime Spectacle

If your day trip extends into the evening, do not miss Impression Sanjie Liu. Directed by Zhang Yimou, this outdoor performance uses the Li River itself as a stage, with the karst mountains as a natural backdrop. Hundreds of performers from local ethnic villages bring the area’s folklore to life with song, light, and movement. It’s a theatrical experience unlike any other in the world.

The Cultural Time Capsule: Ancient Towns of Daxu and Xingping

For those seeking history and authentic atmosphere, the ancient towns along the Li River provide a stark and beautiful contrast to Guilin’s urban energy.

Daxu Ancient Town: A Stroll Through Stone Slabs

Just a 40-minute drive from Guilin, Daxu offers a glimpse into a prosperous past as a bustling river port. Its charm lies in its well-preserved Qing and Ming dynasty architecture and the 2.5-kilometer-long stone slab road that runs through its center. Wander past old-time barber shops, traditional medicine halls, and homes with carved wooden doors. The pace here is slow, defined by the elderly residents chatting in the shade. Climb up to the Longevity Temple for a panoramic view of the town roofs against a riverine backdrop.

Xingping: The Home of the 20-Yuan Note View

Famous for being featured on the back of China’s 20-yuan banknote, Xingping is where the Li River’s scenery reaches its dramatic peak. The town itself is a charming maze of cobbled lanes and old buildings. The main activity, besides capturing that iconic photo, is a short boat ride or a hike to Laozhai Mountain (also called Xianggong Mountain). The climb is steep but short, leading to arguably the most spectacular viewpoint in all of Guilin—a 180-degree panorama of the Li River winding like a jade ribbon through a forest of pinnacles.

The Agricultural Marvel: Longji Rice Terraces

This day trip requires an early start (about a 2.5-hour drive each way) but promises one of the most awe-inspiring sights in China. The Longji (Dragon’s Backbone) Rice Terraces are a testament to human ingenuity and harmony with nature, built over 500 years ago by the Zhuang and Yao people.

Choosing Your Village: Ping'an vs. Jinkeng

The two main viewing areas are Ping’an Zhuang Village and the larger Jinkeng Red Yao Terraces. Ping’an is more developed and accessible, famous for its "Seven Stars Around the Moon" and "Nine Dragons and Five Tigers" terrace formations. Jinkeng is more expansive and offers cable car access to its "Golden Buddha Peak" viewpoint, which is particularly stunning at sunrise. Both offer homestays, but for a day trip, the key is to hike between the viewpoints. The sight of these colossal terraces—emerald green in spring and summer, golden in autumn, and often frosted white in winter—cascading down mountain slopes is utterly unforgettable.

Encounter with the Red Yao

In Jinkeng, you’ll likely see women of the Red Yao ethnic group with their signature long hair, sometimes worn in elaborate coifs. Their traditional dress is a vibrant red, and they are skilled in embroidery and silverwork. Engaging respectfully with the local culture adds a rich layer to the visual spectacle of the terraces.

The Underground Fantasy: Reed Flute Cave and Silver Cave

While Reed Flute Cave (Ludi Yan) is practically within Guilin city, it deserves a half-day excursion. This "Palace of Natural Arts" is a surreal underground world of stalactites, stalagmites, stone curtains, and pillars illuminated by multicolored lights. The formations have fanciful names like "Crystal Palace" and "Pine in the Snow," sparking the imagination.

For a more extensive subterranean adventure, head to Silver Cave (Yinzi Yan) near Yangshuo. It’s one of the largest and most stunning caves in the region, featuring a multi-level journey past a "music stone" that rings when tapped, a majestic "Misty Waterfall," and a breathtaking "Snowy Peak" formation. The sheer scale and variety here are phenomenal.

The Adventurer's Path: Hiking and Rock Climbing in Yangshuo

Guilin’s landscape is a playground for active travelers.

Summiting a Karst Peak

For the fit and fearless, hiking up one of the karst peaks offers the ultimate reward. Moon Hill, with its natural arch, is the classic hike, with steep steps leading to stunning views. For a less crowded but more challenging adventure, local guides can lead you up peaks like TV Tower Mountain for a sunrise or sunset that will forever be etched in your memory.

Rock Climbing Mecca

Yangshuo has gained an international reputation as a world-class rock climbing destination. With over 400 bolted routes on limestone crags of various difficulties, it caters to beginners and experts alike. Numerous local outfitters offer half-day taster sessions or full-day guiding services. There’s no feeling quite like clinging to the face of a karst pinnacle, looking out over a sea of green.

Practical Tips for Your Guilin Day Trips

  • Transport: For flexibility, especially to sites like the Rice Terraces, hiring a private car and driver for the day is cost-effective for small groups. Public buses are available to Yangshuo and some towns but can be time-consuming. For river-based trips, organized tours from Guilin’s pier are straightforward.
  • Timing: Start early to beat the crowds, particularly for the Rice Terraces and popular caves. Weekdays are always quieter than weekends.
  • Footwear: Comfortable, sturdy walking shoes are non-negotiable. The terrain at the terraces and on hikes is often steep and can be slippery.
  • Respect: When visiting ethnic villages or ancient towns, be a mindful observer. Ask for permission before taking portraits of locals and support the community by purchasing handicrafts directly from artisans.

The landscape around Guilin is a gift that keeps on giving. Each day trip peels back a different layer, revealing not just more beautiful scenery, but the enduring spirit, history, and creativity of the people who call this magical corner of China home. Your journey in Guilin truly begins the moment you step outside the city.
