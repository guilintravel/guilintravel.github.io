Sunrise and Sunset Photography in Guilin: Top Locations

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its iconic karst landscapes, misty rivers, and lush greenery, is a paradise for photographers. The interplay of light and shadow during sunrise and sunset transforms this region into a dreamlike canvas. Whether you’re a professional photographer or an enthusiastic traveler with a smartphone, capturing Guilin’s golden hours is an unforgettable experience. Here are the top locations to photograph sunrise and sunset in Guilin.

The Best Spots for Sunrise Photography

1. Xianggong Mountain (Xiānggōng Shān)

Perched above the Li River, Xianggong Mountain offers one of the most breathtaking sunrise views in Guilin. The vantage point overlooks a dramatic bend in the river, framed by towering karst peaks. Arrive early to secure a spot, as this location is popular among photographers. The first light of dawn paints the misty river in hues of pink and gold, creating a surreal atmosphere.

Pro Tip: Use a telephoto lens to compress the layers of mountains and enhance the depth of your shot.

2. Longji Rice Terraces (Lóngjǐ Tītián)

The Longji Rice Terraces, especially the "Dragon’s Backbone" area, are a spectacular sunrise destination. During the golden hour, the sun casts long shadows across the terraced fields, highlighting their intricate patterns. In spring and autumn, the terraces are often shrouded in mist, adding a mystical quality to your photos.

Best Time to Visit: Early October, when the rice turns golden, or late April, when the paddies are filled with water, reflecting the sky.

3. Fubo Hill (Fúbō Shān)

For a sunrise shot within the city, Fubo Hill is an excellent choice. The hill provides a panoramic view of Guilin’s skyline against a backdrop of karst formations. As the sun rises, the city slowly awakens, and the river glows with soft light.

Bonus: Climbing Fubo Hill is relatively easy, making it accessible for all skill levels.

The Best Spots for Sunset Photography

1. Yangshuo’s West Street & Yulong River (Yángshuò Xījiē & Yùlóng Hé)

Yangshuo is the heart of Guilin’s countryside, and its sunsets are legendary. The Yulong River, with its bamboo rafts and serene waters, is perfect for capturing reflections of the setting sun. Meanwhile, West Street offers a lively contrast, where the warm light bathes the cobblestone streets and traditional architecture.

Pro Tip: Rent a bike and explore small villages along the river for hidden sunset spots.

2. Moon Hill (Yuèliàng Shān)

Moon Hill, named for its natural moon-shaped arch, is a unique spot for sunset photography. The fading light creates dramatic silhouettes against the arch, while the surrounding countryside glows in warm tones.

Best Angle: Shoot from the base of the hill to emphasize the arch’s grandeur.

3. Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiàngbí Shān)

This iconic Guilin landmark is magical at sunset. The hill’s namesake “trunk” appears to drink from the Li River, and the setting sun casts a golden reflection on the water. For a creative twist, try long-exposure shots to smooth the river’s surface.

Ideal Season: Visit in autumn when the air is crisp and the skies are clear.

Essential Photography Tips for Guilin

1. Timing is Everything

Arrive at least 30 minutes before sunrise or sunset to scout your composition. The best light often occurs just before the sun dips below the horizon.

2. Use a Tripod

Low-light conditions require stability. A tripod ensures sharp images, especially for long exposures.

3. Experiment with Filters

A polarizing filter can enhance reflections and reduce glare, while a neutral density (ND) filter helps balance exposure in high-contrast scenes.

4. Capture the Mist

Guilin’s morning mist is legendary. Use it to add depth and mystery to your sunrise shots.

5. Respect the Environment

Many photography spots are in rural or protected areas. Follow local guidelines and avoid disturbing wildlife or farmers working in the fields.

Hidden Gems for Adventurous Photographers

1. Xingping Ancient Town (Xīngpíng Gǔzhèn)

This lesser-known town offers a quieter alternative to Yangshuo. The Li River here is particularly photogenic, with classic karst views that appear on the 20 RMB banknote.

2. Daxu Ancient Town (Dàxū Gǔzhèn)

For a rustic sunset, Daxu’s old streets and riverside provide a nostalgic charm. The fading light on the ancient buildings creates a timeless atmosphere.

3. Reed Flute Cave (Lúdí Yán)

While primarily known for its illuminated stalactites, the area around the cave also offers unique sunset opportunities, especially with the surrounding hills and lakes.

Final Thoughts on Gear and Preparation

  • Lenses: A wide-angle (16-35mm) for landscapes and a telephoto (70-200mm) for compressing distant peaks.
  • Clothing: Dress in layers—mornings can be chilly, even in summer.
  • Local Guides: Consider hiring a local guide who knows the best-hidden viewpoints and optimal shooting conditions.

Guilin’s sunrises and sunsets are more than just photographic moments—they’re experiences that stay with you long after the trip ends. Whether you’re framing the perfect shot or simply soaking in the beauty, these locations promise unforgettable memories.
