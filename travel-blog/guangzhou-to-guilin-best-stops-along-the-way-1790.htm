Guangzhou to Guilin: Best Stops Along the Way

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Road trips are one of the best ways to explore China’s stunning landscapes, and the journey from Guangzhou to Guilin is no exception. While Guilin’s karst mountains and Li River are world-famous, the route between these two cities is packed with hidden gems, cultural landmarks, and breathtaking scenery. Whether you’re traveling by car, train, or bus, these stops will make your trip unforgettable.

Why Take the Scenic Route?

Many travelers rush straight to Guilin, but slowing down and exploring the lesser-known spots along the way offers a richer experience. From ancient villages to hot springs and dramatic mountain passes, this route showcases the diversity of southern China.

Best Time to Travel

The ideal time for this journey is spring (March-May) or autumn (September-November) when temperatures are mild, and the landscapes are at their most vibrant. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter brings cooler weather—though some travelers enjoy the misty, atmospheric views during this season.

Top Stops Between Guangzhou and Guilin

1. Qingyuan: The Hot Spring Paradise

Just an hour from Guangzhou, Qingyuan is famous for its natural hot springs and lush forests.

What to Do Here:

  • Relax in Thermal Baths: Resorts like Guangdong First Peak Hot Spring offer private and public pools with mineral-rich waters.
  • Hike the Nanling Mountains: A great spot for nature lovers, with trails leading to waterfalls and scenic viewpoints.
  • Visit Feilai Gorge: A stunning canyon with boat tours and cliffside temples.

Travel Tip: Spend a night here to fully unwind before continuing your journey.

2. Yangshuo’s Little Sister: Xingping

Before reaching Guilin, make a detour to Xingping, a charming riverside town that feels like a quieter version of Yangshuo.

Highlights:

  • 20 RMB Viewpoint: The iconic scenery featured on China’s 20 RMB note is best seen here.
  • Bamboo Rafting: Drift along the Li River for a peaceful escape.
  • Ancient Fishing Village: Explore well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.

Insider Tip: Sunrise and sunset are magical times for photography.

3. Hezhou: The Hidden Gem

Often overlooked, Hezhou offers a mix of history and natural beauty.

Must-See Attractions:

  • Huangyao Ancient Town: Cobblestone streets, old bridges, and lantern-lit alleys make this a photographer’s dream.
  • Gupo Mountain: A sacred site with temples and panoramic views.
  • Local Cuisine: Try Hezhou oil tea, a savory traditional drink.

Best For: Travelers who love off-the-beaten-path destinations.

4. Guilin’s Backyard: Longsheng Rice Terraces

Though technically closer to Guilin, the Longsheng Rice Terraces are worth a stop before arriving in the city.

Why Visit?

  • Dragon’s Backbone Terraces: These cascading fields are especially stunning in spring (water-filled) and autumn (golden harvest).
  • Minority Culture: The area is home to the Zhuang and Yao ethnic groups—visit a local village for handmade crafts and traditional performances.

Pro Tip: Stay overnight in a guesthouse to catch both sunrise and sunset over the terraces.

Alternative Routes & Transportation Tips

By Train

The high-speed rail from Guangzhou to Guilin takes about 2.5 hours, but if you have time, consider breaking up the trip with stops in Zhaoqing or Wuzhou.

By Car

Renting a car gives the most flexibility. The drive takes roughly 6-7 hours without stops, but spreading it over 2-3 days allows for deeper exploration.

By Bus

Budget travelers can take buses between major stops, though this requires more planning.

Final Travel Essentials

  • Pack light but smart: Comfortable shoes, layers for changing weather, and a good camera.
  • Cash in rural areas: Some smaller towns may not accept digital payments.
  • Local SIM card: For navigation and translation apps.

This journey isn’t just about reaching Guilin—it’s about discovering the soul of southern China along the way.
