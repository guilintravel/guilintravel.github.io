Guilin Nightlife: Best Bars, Clubs & Local Experiences

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, renowned for its breathtaking karst landscapes and serene Li River, transforms into a vibrant playground after dark. While the city is famous for its daytime adventures, the nightlife scene offers a unique blend of modern entertainment and traditional charm. Whether you’re looking for craft cocktails, live music, or authentic local experiences, Guilin’s after-hours offerings won’t disappoint.

The Best Bars in Guilin

1. The Rooftop Bar at The Secret Garden Hotel

Perched atop a boutique hotel, this bar offers panoramic views of Guilin’s skyline and the surrounding limestone peaks. Sip on a signature lychee martini while watching the sunset—it’s the perfect way to unwind after a day of exploring. The ambiance is chic yet relaxed, with a mix of expats and travelers mingling over creative cocktails.

2. Mojo Bar

A favorite among locals and backpackers, Mojo Bar is known for its lively atmosphere and strong drinks. The bar features a pool table, dartboard, and regular live music performances. Try their "Guilin Mule," a twist on the Moscow Mule, made with local ginger beer.

3. Whiskey & Words

For a more sophisticated vibe, head to Whiskey & Words. This cozy speakeasy-style bar boasts an impressive selection of single malts and craft cocktails. The dim lighting and vintage decor make it an ideal spot for a quiet evening with friends or a date night.

Top Nightclubs for Dancing the Night Away

1. M2 Club

Guilin’s premier nightclub, M2, attracts party-goers with its high-energy DJ sets and laser light shows. The club often hosts international DJs and themed parties, drawing a young, fashionable crowd. If you’re looking to dance until dawn, this is the place to be.

2. SOHO Bar

A mix of bar and club, SOHO offers a more laid-back clubbing experience. The music ranges from hip-hop to electronic, and the outdoor terrace is perfect for cooling off between dance sessions. Don’t miss their "SOHO Special," a fruity cocktail that packs a punch.

3. 88 Club

A long-standing favorite, 88 Club is known for its vibrant atmosphere and affordable drinks. The club features multiple floors with different music genres, so there’s something for everyone. It’s a great spot to experience Guilin’s local party scene.

Unique Local Nighttime Experiences

1. Night Cruise on the Li River

While most tourists take daytime boat rides, a nighttime cruise offers a magical perspective of Guilin’s landscapes. The illuminated karst mountains and reflections on the water create a surreal ambiance. Some cruises even include traditional Chinese performances and dinner.

2. Zhengyang Pedestrian Street Night Market

This bustling street comes alive at night with food stalls, souvenir shops, and street performers. Sample local delicacies like luosifen (spicy snail rice noodles) or bbq skewers while soaking in the lively atmosphere. It’s a must-visit for foodies and culture enthusiasts.

3. Impression Sanjie Liu Light Show

Directed by Zhang Yimou, this outdoor spectacle combines music, dance, and stunning visuals set against the natural backdrop of the Li River. Hundreds of performers showcase local ethnic culture in a mesmerizing display of light and sound.

Hidden Gems & Off-the-Beaten-Path Spots

1. The Hideaway

Tucked in an alley near West Street, this intimate bar is a well-kept secret among expats. The owner, a former musician, often hosts impromptu jam sessions. Their homemade infused liquors are a highlight—try the osmanthus gin.

2. Seven Stars Park Night Walk

For a quieter evening, take a moonlit stroll through Seven Stars Park. The park is beautifully lit at night, and the cooler temperatures make it a pleasant escape from the city’s hustle.

3. Local Tea Houses

While not a typical nightlife spot, Guilin’s tea houses offer a serene way to end the day. Sip on locally grown osmanthus tea while enjoying traditional Chinese music—a perfect way to unwind.

Tips for Enjoying Guilin’s Nightlife

  • Dress Code: Most bars and clubs are casual, but upscale venues may require smart attire.
  • Transport: Taxis and ride-hailing apps like Didi are widely available, but walking is often the best way to explore the city center.
  • Safety: Guilin is generally safe at night, but always keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Local Etiquette: Tipping isn’t customary, but leaving small change is appreciated in bars and clubs.

From rooftop cocktails to riverside light shows, Guilin’s nightlife offers something for every traveler. Whether you’re a party animal or a culture seeker, the city’s after-dark scene is sure to leave a lasting impression.
