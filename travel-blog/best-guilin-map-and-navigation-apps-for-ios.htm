Best Guilin Map and Navigation Apps for iOS

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a city etched into the collective imagination of travelers worldwide, is famous for its karst mountains, winding Li River, and ethereal rice terraces. But navigating this sprawling region—from the bustling city center to remote Yangshuo villages and the misty Longji rice terraces—can be a challenge, especially for international visitors. While Guilin is more tourist-friendly than many Chinese cities, the lack of English signage in rural areas, the complexity of public transport, and the sheer scale of the landscape mean that a reliable iOS navigation app is not a luxury—it’s a necessity.

In this guide, we dive deep into the best map and navigation apps for iPhone users exploring Guilin and its surrounding hotspots. We’ll cover everything from mainstream giants like Gaode Maps to niche apps designed for hiking the Dragon’s Backbone Rice Terraces. Whether you’re a solo backpacker, a family on a guided tour, or a digital nomad looking to escape the crowds, these apps will ensure you never lose your way—or your sense of wonder.

Why You Can’t Rely on Apple Maps or Google Maps in Guilin

Before we get into the best options, let’s address the elephant in the room: why not just use the pre-installed Apple Maps or the ever-popular Google Maps? The short answer is that both are severely limited in China due to government restrictions on foreign mapping services. Apple Maps in China uses data from AutoNavi (Gaode), which is actually quite good, but the interface is still clunky for non-Chinese speakers. Google Maps, on the other hand, is essentially unusable without a VPN, and even then, its data for Guilin’s rural areas is outdated or missing entirely.

For example, if you try to find a bus from Guilin to Yangshuo using Google Maps, you might be directed to a route that no longer exists. Similarly, Apple Maps often fails to recognize the names of smaller villages like Dazhai or Ping’an in the Longji Terraces. This is where specialized, China-native apps come into play.

The Top iOS Navigation Apps for Guilin Travel

1. Gaode Maps (高德地图) – The Gold Standard for China Travel

Gaode Maps, also known as AutoNavi, is the most widely used navigation app in China, and for good reason. It’s the backbone of most third-party apps and offers unparalleled detail for Guilin. The iOS version is fully functional, though the English interface is limited.

Key Features for Guilin:

  • Real-time public transport data: Guilin’s bus system can be confusing, especially the routes that connect the city to Yangshuo and Longsheng. Gaode provides live bus tracking, including the popular Guilin–Yangshuo express bus (which departs from the Guilin Bus Station every 30 minutes). You can see exactly where the bus is on a map, how crowded it is, and the estimated arrival time at your stop.

  • Walking and hiking routes: For the Longji Rice Terraces, Gaode includes detailed walking paths that connect the main villages. Many tourists miss the hidden trails between Ping’an and Dazhai because they rely on tourist maps that only show the main road. Gaode’s satellite view can reveal these shortcuts, though you’ll need to download the offline map beforehand since mobile signal is spotty in the mountains.

  • Taxi and ride-hailing integration: Gaode is deeply integrated with Didi, China’s equivalent of Uber. You can book a Didi directly from the app, which is a lifesaver when you’re in a remote area like the Xianggong Mountain viewpoint in Yangshuo. The app also shows estimated fares, so you won’t get ripped off by taxi drivers who quote inflated prices for tourists.

Pro Tip: Switch the app to “English mode” by going to Settings > Language. While not all features are translated, the basic navigation prompts (turn left, turn right, arrive at destination) are in English. For text-based searches, use the Chinese name of the place (e.g., “阳朔” for Yangshuo). Save these names in your notes app beforehand.

2. Baidu Maps (百度地图) – The Best for Detailed POIs and Reviews

Baidu Maps is Gaode’s main competitor, and while it’s slightly less popular for navigation, it excels in points of interest (POIs) and user reviews. For Guilin, this is invaluable because many smaller attractions, like the Xiangbi Hill (Elephant Trunk Hill) or the Fubo Hill, have specific opening hours, ticket prices, and crowd levels that Baidu aggregates from local users.

Why Choose Baidu Maps for Guilin:

  • Comprehensive POI database: Baidu has the most exhaustive list of restaurants, hotels, and scenic spots in Guilin. For example, if you’re looking for a specific rice noodle shop (like the famous “老东江米粉” in the city center), Baidu will show you the exact location, customer ratings, and even photos of the menu. This is far more reliable than TripAdvisor, which often has outdated listings for smaller Chinese eateries.

  • Indoor navigation: Some of Guilin’s larger attractions, like the Reed Flute Cave or the Jingjiang Prince’s City, have complex indoor layouts. Baidu Maps offers indoor floor plans for these sites, which helps you navigate through the caves without missing the most photogenic stalactites.

  • Voice navigation in English: Baidu’s voice prompts are clearer and more natural in English compared to Gaode. If you’re driving a rental car (which is increasingly popular for Guilin’s countryside), Baidu’s lane guidance and speed camera alerts are superior.

Limitation: Baidu Maps can be heavy on data usage and battery drain. Make sure to download offline maps for Guilin city, Yangshuo, and Longsheng before you arrive. The offline maps are detailed enough for walking and driving, but they don’t include real-time traffic or public transport updates.

3. Apple Maps (with China Data) – The Surprising Underdog

Many travelers dismiss Apple Maps, but in China, it’s actually a solid choice because it uses Gaode’s data behind the scenes. The interface is cleaner and more familiar for iOS users, and it integrates seamlessly with Siri and CarPlay.

How to Make Apple Maps Work in Guilin:

  • Search in Chinese: Apple Maps doesn’t recognize English names for most Guilin attractions. For example, searching “Elephant Trunk Hill” will return nothing, but searching “象鼻山” will give you the exact location. Save a list of Chinese names for your must-visit spots.

  • Use Collections: Apple Maps allows you to create collections of saved places, which is perfect for planning a Guilin itinerary. You can group all your Yangshuo spots (Moon Hill, Yulong River, West Street) into one collection and then navigate between them sequentially.

  • Integration with Calendar: If you have your flight or train tickets in your iOS Calendar, Apple Maps can automatically suggest routes to the airport or Guilin West Railway Station. This is a small but helpful feature when you’re rushing to catch the high-speed train to Guangzhou or Hong Kong.

When to Use It: Apple Maps is best for urban navigation within Guilin city and for driving between major towns. For remote hiking or bus routes, stick with Gaode or Baidu.

4. Maps.Me – The Offline King for Hiking and Rural Areas

Maps.Me is a global favorite among backpackers, and its offline capabilities are unmatched. For Guilin’s rural areas, where internet access is unreliable (especially in the Longji Terraces and the remote villages near Xingping), Maps.Me is a lifesaver.

Why Maps.Me Shines in Guilin:

  • Full offline functionality: Download the entire Guilin region (including Yangshuo and Longsheng) before your trip. The offline map includes hiking trails, small footpaths, and even contour lines for elevation, which is crucial for navigating the steep steps of the Longji Rice Terraces.

  • OpenStreetMap data: Maps.Me uses OpenStreetMap, which is community-edited and often more detailed for hiking routes than commercial apps. For example, the trail from Dazhai to the No. 1 viewpoint (七星伴月) is clearly marked on Maps.Me, while Gaode might show it as a faint line.

  • Bookmarks and pins: You can drop pins for specific viewpoints, like the “Nine Horses Wall” on the Li River cruise, and then navigate to them later. This is useful if you’re planning a self-guided bike tour of Yangshuo’s countryside.

Limitation: Maps.Me doesn’t have real-time traffic or public transport data. It’s purely a static map. Use it in combination with Gaode for the best results.

5. Didi Chuxing (滴滴出行) – The Essential Ride-Hailing Companion

While not strictly a map app, Didi is the most important navigation tool for getting around Guilin without a car. The iOS app is fully functional in English, and it’s the only safe and reliable way to hail a taxi in Guilin, especially from the airport or train station.

How Didi Works in Guilin:

  • Price transparency: Didi shows the estimated fare before you book, so you won’t be subject to the inflated prices that taxi drivers often charge at Guilin Liangjiang International Airport. A ride from the airport to the city center should cost around 100–120 RMB, but taxis often ask for 150–200 RMB. Didi ensures you pay the market rate.

  • Multiple vehicle types: In Guilin, you can choose between “Express” (standard car), “Taxi” (licensed taxis), and “Hitch” (carpooling, which is cheaper but less reliable). For groups of 3–4, the “Express” option is the most comfortable and affordable.

  • Driver communication: The app has a built-in translator for messages, so you can tell your driver “Please take me to the Yangshuo Bus Station” in Chinese without speaking a word. However, it’s still a good idea to have the address written in Chinese on your phone screen.

Pro Tip: Didi can also be used to book intercity rides, such as from Guilin to Yangshuo or from Yangshuo to the Longji Terraces. The fare is usually cheaper than a private car hire, and you can split the cost with fellow travelers.

Navigating Guilin’s Hotspots with These Apps

The Li River Cruise: From Guilin to Yangshuo

The Li River cruise is the most iconic experience in Guilin, but the logistics can be confusing. Most cruises depart from the Mopanshan Dock (磨盘山码头) about 30 minutes from Guilin city center. Use Gaode Maps to navigate to the dock, as it shows the exact bus routes (Bus 4 or 9) and the Didi pickup points.

Once on the boat, you’ll pass by famous sights like the “Nine Horses Wall” and “Yellow Cloth Reflection.” Instead of relying on the boat’s audio guide (which is often in Chinese only), use Maps.Me with the offline map of the Li River. The map marks each viewpoint with notes about the best photo angles. You can also use Baidu Maps to read user reviews of the cruise experience, including tips on which side of the boat to sit on for the best views.

Yangshuo: Biking Through the Karst Landscape

Yangshuo is best explored by bicycle or scooter. Apple Maps is surprisingly good for navigating Yangshuo’s main roads, like the route from West Street to the Yulong River. However, for the off-road trails that lead to Moon Hill or the Xianggong Mountain viewpoint, switch to Maps.Me.

One hidden gem is the “Golden Waterfall” trail, which starts near the Yulong River Bridge. This trail is not marked on any tourist map, but it appears on Maps.Me as a faint path through the bamboo groves. Follow it for 20 minutes, and you’ll reach a secluded swimming spot that most tourists miss.

For food and accommodation, Baidu Maps is your best friend. Search for “啤酒鱼” (beer fish) in Yangshuo, and you’ll see a list of restaurants with ratings, photos, and even the average price per person. The top-rated ones, like “谢三姐啤酒鱼,” are usually crowded but worth the wait.

Longji Rice Terraces: Hiking the Dragon’s Backbone

The Longji Rice Terraces are divided into two main areas: Ping’an (平安) and Dazhai (大寨). Both are accessible by bus from Guilin, but the bus schedules are irregular. Use Gaode Maps to check the real-time departure times from the Guilin Bus Station. The bus to Dazhai is less frequent but offers a more authentic experience.

Once you arrive, Maps.Me is essential for hiking. The terraces are a maze of stone steps, and it’s easy to get lost if you stray from the main path. Download the offline map of “Longsheng County” and look for the trail markers that connect Ping’an to Dazhai. This is a 4–5 hour hike that rewards you with panoramic views of the terraces from every angle.

Safety tip: Mobile signal is weak in the terraces, so don’t rely on real-time navigation. Download all maps before you start hiking. Also, carry a power bank because GPS drains your battery quickly in mountainous terrain.

Guilin City: Urban Exploration and Food Hunting

In Guilin city, you’ll spend most of your time around the “Two Rivers and Four Lakes” area, the Zhengyang Pedestrian Street, and the Guilin Museum. Apple Maps works well for walking between these attractions, but Baidu Maps is better for finding food.

For example, if you’re craving Guilin rice noodles (桂林米粉), search for “老东江米粉” on Baidu Maps. The app will show you the branch closest to your location, along with reviews that mention the best time to visit (avoid lunch rush, as the queue can be 30 minutes long). You can also use Baidu’s “nearby” feature to find hidden snack stalls that sell “油茶” (oil tea) or “马蹄糕” (water chestnut cake).

Practical Tips for Using Navigation Apps in Guilin

1. Download Offline Maps Before You Arrive

This cannot be overstated. Guilin’s rural areas have spotty 4G coverage, and international roaming data can be expensive. Open Gaode Maps or Maps.Me while you’re still on Wi-Fi at your hotel, and download the offline maps for “Guilin City,” “Yangshuo County,” and “Longsheng County.” For Gaode, you’ll need to search for these regions in Chinese and then tap “Download” in the menu.

2. Learn Basic Chinese Characters for Important Places

Even the best apps have limitations with English search. Memorize the Chinese characters for your key destinations:

  • 桂林 (Guìlín)
  • 阳朔 (Yángshuò)
  • 龙脊梯田 (Lóngjǐ Tītián)
  • 漓江 (Lí Jiāng)
  • 象鼻山 (Xiàngbí Shān)

Write these on a piece of paper or save them in your Notes app. When you need to show a taxi driver or a bus conductor, you can simply point to the characters.

3. Use a VPN if You Plan to Use Google Maps

If you absolutely must use Google Maps for its familiar interface, you’ll need a reliable VPN installed on your iPhone before you arrive in China. ExpressVPN and NordVPN are the most commonly used, but even they can be blocked occasionally. As a backup, always have Gaode or Baidu installed.

4. Combine Apps for the Best Experience

No single app is perfect for all situations. Use this combination for a seamless trip:

  • Gaode Maps for real-time bus and Didi navigation.
  • Maps.Me for offline hiking and rural exploration.
  • Baidu Maps for finding restaurants, attractions, and reading reviews.
  • Didi for all taxi and car rides.

Final Thoughts on Navigating Guilin with iOS

Guilin is a city that rewards preparation. The landscape is breathtaking, but the infrastructure can be challenging for international travelers. By equipping your iPhone with the right mix of navigation apps—Gaode, Baidu, Maps.Me, and Didi—you can focus on the experience instead of worrying about getting lost.

Remember that the best journeys often involve a little bit of uncertainty. Don’t be afraid to take a wrong turn on a bicycle path in Yangshuo, or to stop and ask a local farmer for directions in the Longji Terraces. The apps are your tools, but the adventure is yours to create. Whether you’re standing on the top of Xianggong Mountain watching the sunrise over the Li River, or slurping noodles at a street stall in Guilin’s old town, the right navigation app will ensure that your only concern is how many photos you can take before your battery dies.

Safe travels, and may your GPS always find the hidden path.
