Guilin’s Best Bird’s-Eye Viewpoints for Stunning Vistas

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a jewel in China’s Guangxi region, is famed for its surreal karst landscapes, winding rivers, and lush greenery. While exploring its trails and waterways is magical, witnessing Guilin from above is an entirely different experience. From towering peaks to hidden lookout spots, here are the best bird’s-eye viewpoints to capture Guilin’s breathtaking vistas.

The Iconic Xianggong Mountain

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Xianggong Mountain (相公山) is arguably the most famous viewpoint in Guilin, offering a panoramic spectacle of the Li River snaking through emerald peaks. Located in Yangshuo County, this spot is a favorite among photographers, especially at sunrise when mist blankets the karst formations.

How to Get There

A short but steep 20-minute hike leads to the summit, where multiple viewing platforms await. The trail is well-maintained, making it accessible for most travelers. Arrive early to secure a prime spot—sunrise crowds can be intense!

Pro Tip

Visit during autumn (September–November) for clear skies and golden hues, or in spring (March–May) for lush greenery and fog-draped peaks.

The Hidden Gem: Laozhai Mountain

Off-the-Beaten-Path Beauty

For those seeking solitude, Laozhai Mountain (老寨山) in Xingping Town delivers. Unlike Xianggong, this hike is rugged and less touristy, rewarding adventurers with unobstructed views of the Li River’s dramatic bends.

The Climb

The ascent takes about 30–40 minutes, with steep steps and rocky sections. Wear sturdy shoes and bring water—the effort is worth it. Sunset here is legendary, painting the sky in fiery oranges and purples.

Local Insight

Xingping is the backdrop for the 20 RMB bill’s design. After your hike, explore the ancient town’s cobbled streets and riverside cafes.

The Legendary Moon Hill

A Natural Wonder

Moon Hill (月亮山) is a karst arch formation resembling a crescent moon. While you can admire it from below, climbing to the top unveils a sweeping view of Yangshuo’s countryside.

Hiking Details

The trail is moderate, taking roughly 45 minutes. Along the way, stop at the “moon cave” for a unique frame of the landscape. The summit overlooks rice paddies, bamboo forests, and distant peaks.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning or late afternoon avoids the midday heat. Combine this with a bike ride through Yangshuo’s scenic backroads for a full day of exploration.

The Towering Diecai Hill

Guilin’s Urban Oasis

Located in the heart of Guilin, Diecai Hill (叠彩山) is a convenient escape from the city bustle. Its name means “Folded Brocade Hill,” inspired by the layered rock formations.

What to Expect

The hike is gentle, with paved paths leading to several pavilions. The summit, “Cloud-Touching Peak,” offers 360-degree views of Guilin’s skyline, the Li River, and surrounding karst towers.

Bonus Spot

Don’t miss the Wind Cave, a cool, breezy tunnel adorned with ancient inscriptions—a perfect pit stop on your way up.

The Adventurous TV Tower Mountain

For the Thrill-Seekers

TV Tower Mountain (电视塔山) is a local secret, named after the transmission tower at its peak. The unmarked trail is steep and challenging, but the payoff is an unparalleled vista of Guilin and the Li River.

Getting There

Start from Yushan Park and follow the dirt path upward. The hike takes about an hour. Locals recommend going with a guide if you’re unfamiliar with the route.

Why It’s Special

This spot is rarely crowded, making it ideal for uninterrupted photography or quiet reflection. Sunset and sunrise are equally stunning.

The Picturesque Potted Landscape Viewing Platform

A Unique Perspective

For a different angle, head to the Potted Landscape Viewing Platform (盆景观景台) in Yangshuo. This elevated deck overlooks the Yulong River, where bamboo rafts drift past towering peaks.

Easy Access

No strenuous hike needed—just a short walk from the parking area. It’s perfect for travelers short on time or those who prefer a relaxed outing.

Nearby Attractions

Pair your visit with a bamboo raft ride or a stop at the nearby Big Banyan Tree, a 1,400-year-old landmark.

The Serene Seven-Star Park Viewpoints

Guilin’s Green Lung

Seven-Star Park (七星公园) is the city’s largest green space, home to multiple viewpoints. The most notable is Putuo Hill, where you can see the park’s namesake “seven-star” karst formations.

Highlights

Climb to the Moon Rock or Camel Hill for varied perspectives. The park also features caves, temples, and a panda enclosure—ideal for a full-day excursion.

Visitor Tip

Visit in the late afternoon when the light softens, casting a golden glow over the landscape.

The Dramatic Longji Rice Terraces

Beyond Karst Landscapes

While not in Guilin proper, the Longji Rice Terraces (龙脊梯田) are a must-see detour. These cascading terraces, carved into the mountains by the Zhuang and Yao people, transform with the seasons.

Best Viewpoints

  1. Jin Keng Terraces (金坑梯田): The largest and most photogenic, especially at sunrise.
  2. Ping’an Terraces (平安梯田): Easier access and great for sunset.

Seasonal Magic

  • Spring: Reflective water-filled terraces.
  • Summer: Vibrant green layers.
  • Autumn: Golden harvest hues.
  • Winter: Frost-dusted fields.

The Tranquil Fubo Hill

History Meets Scenery

Fubo Hill (伏波山) blends cultural heritage with natural beauty. Named after a Han Dynasty general, it offers views of the Li River and Guilin’s skyline.

What to See

Explore the Returned Pearl Cave and the Thousand Buddha Cliff, then climb to the summit for a peaceful panorama.

Ideal For

History buffs and those who enjoy shorter hikes with cultural stops.

The Ultimate Sunrise Spot: Yao Mountain

Guilin’s Highest Peak

Yao Mountain (尧山) stands at 909 meters, providing the highest vantage point in the area. On clear days, you can see Guilin, the Li River, and endless karst peaks.

How to Visit

Drive or take a cable car to the mid-point, then hike the rest. Sunrise here is magical, with clouds often floating between the peaks.

Insider Advice

Pack layers—it’s cooler at the top, even in summer.

Final Thoughts on Exploring Guilin from Above

Whether you’re a photographer, hiker, or casual traveler, Guilin’s elevated viewpoints promise unforgettable memories. Each spot offers a unique perspective on this dreamlike landscape, proving that sometimes, the best way to see paradise is from above.
