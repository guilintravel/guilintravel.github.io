Guilin Fishing Spots: An Angler’s Checklist

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its iconic karst landscapes and winding rivers, isn’t just a paradise for photographers and hikers—it’s also a dream destination for anglers. Whether you’re casting a line for leisure or aiming for a trophy catch, Guilin’s waterways offer some of the most scenic and rewarding fishing experiences in China. Here’s your ultimate checklist for the best fishing spots, local tips, and must-know details for a successful angling adventure.

The Best Fishing Spots in Guilin

1. Li River (Lijiang)

The Li River is the crown jewel of Guilin’s fishing scene. Known for its crystal-clear waters and dramatic limestone peaks, this UNESCO-listed river is home to species like grass carp, mandarin fish, and the elusive Li River catfish.

Best Time to Fish: Early morning or late afternoon, especially during spring and autumn.
Hot Spots: Near Yangshuo’s quieter bends or around Xingping Village, where the currents slow down.
Pro Tip: Local fishermen often use live bait like small shrimp or worms—mimic their techniques for better results.

2. Yulong River (Little Li River)

A quieter alternative to the Li River, the Yulong is perfect for those seeking solitude. Its shallow, emerald-green waters are teeming with carp and bream.

Best Time to Fish: Mid-morning when the sun warms the water.
Hot Spots: Near the ancient bridges of Jiuxian or Fuli Town.
Pro Tip: Fly fishing works well here—try lightweight lures to avoid spooking the fish.

3. Two Rivers and Four Lakes Scenic Area

Located in downtown Guilin, this man-made network of lakes and canals is stocked with fish year-round. It’s ideal for casual anglers who want convenience without sacrificing scenery.

Best Time to Fish: Evenings, when the city lights reflect off the water.
Hot Spots: Around Ronghu Lake, where larger carp gather near the reeds.
Pro Tip: Check local regulations—some areas require permits or have catch-and-release rules.

4. Longsheng Rice Terraces Area

While famous for its cascading terraces, Longsheng’s mountain streams hide populations of wild trout and smallmouth bass. The cooler water temperatures make it a refreshing summer spot.

Best Time to Fish: Late spring to early summer, after the snowmelt.
Hot Spots: The streams near Ping’an Village or Jinkeng Terraces.
Pro Tip: Pack light—hiking to these spots can be steep, but the views (and fish) are worth it.

Essential Gear for Guilin Fishing

Rods and Reels

  • A medium-action spinning rod (6–7 feet) is versatile enough for Guilin’s rivers and lakes.
  • For fly fishing enthusiasts, a 4–5 weight rod works well in the Yulong’s shallow currents.

Bait and Lures

  • Live bait: Worms, shrimp, or local insects (ask villagers for advice).
  • Artificial lures: Small spoons, soft plastics, or floating flies for surface action.

Other Must-Haves

  • A lightweight, foldable chair for riverbank comfort.
  • Polarized sunglasses to spot fish in Guilin’s clear waters.
  • A waterproof bag for your phone and snacks—sudden rain showers are common.

Local Fishing Culture and Etiquette

Respect the Waterways

Guilin’s rivers are lifelines for both wildlife and communities. Avoid littering, and follow catch limits to preserve fish populations.

Learn from Locals

Many Guilin fishermen use traditional bamboo rods or nets. Strike up a conversation—you might pick up techniques passed down for generations.

Permits and Rules

Some areas, like protected sections of the Li River, require fishing permits. Check with local authorities or your hotel before casting.

Beyond Fishing: What Else to Do

Explore Yangshuo’s Countryside

After a morning of fishing, rent a bike and pedal through rice paddies or try rock climbing on the karst cliffs.

Night Markets and Fresh Catches

Head to Guilin’s night markets (like Zhengyang Pedestrian Street) to sample grilled Li River fish or beer fish, a local specialty.

Take a Bamboo Raft Tour

Even if you’re not fishing, drifting down the Yulong on a bamboo raft offers a unique perspective of Guilin’s beauty.

Seasonal Considerations

  • Spring (March–May): Ideal for active fish post-winter.
  • Summer (June–August): Early mornings avoid the heat; watch for afternoon storms.
  • Autumn (September–November): Prime time—cool weather and hungry fish.
  • Winter (December–February): Slow fishing, but fewer crowds.

Final Tips for a Memorable Trip

  • Hire a Guide: Local guides know hidden spots and can arrange gear.
  • Pack for Weather: Quick-dry clothing and rain gear are essentials.
  • Try Night Fishing: Some species, like catfish, are more active after dark.

Guilin’s fishing spots blend adventure, culture, and stunning scenery. Whether you’re a seasoned angler or a first-timer, this checklist ensures you’re ready to reel in the best of what Guilin’s waters have to offer. Tight lines!
