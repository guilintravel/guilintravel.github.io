Can You Use International Credit Cards to Withdraw Cash from ATMs in Guilin?

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog

Guilin, famous for its stunning karst landscapes and serene rivers, attracts millions of visitors from around the world each year. As an international traveler, having easy access to cash is essential for exploring this city’s unique attractions, enjoying local delicacies, and purchasing souvenirs. A common question among travelers is whether Guilin’s ATMs accept international credit cards for cash withdrawals. Here’s a comprehensive guide to answer that question and provide insights into using ATMs in Guilin.

Availability of ATMs in Guilin

Location and Accessibility

Guilin is a well-developed city with a solid infrastructure to support tourism. You’ll find ATMs in various convenient locations, including:

  • Major Banks: ATMs are available at branches of major Chinese banks such as Bank of China, ICBC (Industrial and Commercial Bank of China), and China Construction Bank.
  • Tourist Areas: ATMs are strategically located near popular tourist spots like Elephant Trunk Hill, Seven Star Park, and along the Li River.
  • Hotels and Airports: Most mid-range and luxury hotels in Guilin have ATMs on-site or nearby. ATMs are also available at Guilin Liangjiang International Airport.

Language Options

Many ATMs in Guilin offer multiple language options, including English. This makes it easier for international tourists to navigate the withdrawal process without confusion. Look for the language selection option on the screen when you start the transaction.

Using International Credit Cards at ATMs

Supported Card Networks

ATMs in Guilin generally support major international card networks, including:

  • Visa: Widely accepted across ATMs in China, including Guilin. Look for the Visa logo on the ATM machine.
  • Mastercard: Most ATMs also accept Mastercard, which is equally prevalent as Visa.
  • American Express (AmEx): While less common than Visa or Mastercard, certain ATMs operated by Bank of China or ICBC may accept AmEx.
  • Cirrus and Maestro: Travelers with Cirrus or Maestro cards can use ATMs from selected banks like ICBC and China Construction Bank.

Daily Withdrawal Limits

Chinese ATMs typically have daily withdrawal limits, which may vary depending on the bank and your card issuer. Most ATMs in Guilin allow a maximum withdrawal of 2,500 to 3,000 RMB per transaction. However, your card’s issuing bank might impose its own limits, so it’s wise to check before traveling.

Fees and Exchange Rates

Using international credit cards at ATMs in Guilin involves certain costs:

  • ATM Fees: Chinese banks usually charge a nominal fee (around 20-30 RMB) for international card withdrawals.
  • Foreign Transaction Fees: Your card issuer may charge additional fees, typically 1-3% of the transaction amount.
  • Exchange Rates: The exchange rate applied is typically set by your card’s network (Visa, Mastercard, etc.) and is usually more favorable than currency exchange counters.

To minimize fees, consider withdrawing larger amounts at once rather than making multiple smaller transactions.

Tips for Using ATMs in Guilin

Choosing the Right ATM

Not all ATMs in Guilin are created equal. To ensure a smooth transaction:

  • Use Major Bank ATMs: Stick to ATMs operated by reputable banks like Bank of China, ICBC, or China Construction Bank, as they are more likely to support international cards and have better security.
  • Avoid Standalone ATMs: Machines in less secure locations, such as convenience stores, may be prone to skimming devices.

Preparing for Possible Issues

While Guilin is relatively tourist-friendly, you might encounter occasional challenges:

  • Card Rejections: Some ATMs might not accept your card due to network compatibility issues. In such cases, try another ATM from a different bank.
  • Cash Availability: Certain ATMs might run out of cash during peak times, especially in tourist-heavy areas. Always have a backup plan.
  • Card Retention: If an ATM retains your card, contact the bank immediately using the hotline number displayed on the machine.

Safety Precautions

When using ATMs in Guilin, follow these safety tips:

  • Use ATMs in Well-Lit Areas: Choose ATMs located in secure, busy areas, especially at night.
  • Shield Your PIN: Cover the keypad when entering your PIN to prevent theft.
  • Monitor Account Activity: Check your bank statements regularly to ensure no unauthorized transactions occur.

Alternatives to ATMs

Currency Exchange

If you’re unable to withdraw cash using your international credit card, you can exchange foreign currency at:

  • Banks: Major banks like Bank of China offer currency exchange services, but you may need your passport for identification.
  • Airports and Hotels: Currency exchange counters at airports and upscale hotels provide convenience but may have higher fees and less favorable rates.

Mobile Payments

China is known for its advanced mobile payment systems like Alipay and WeChat Pay. While these platforms are ubiquitous in urban areas, their usability for foreigners has improved recently:

  • Linking Foreign Cards: Some mobile payment apps now allow international visitors to link foreign cards for transactions.
  • QR Codes: Mobile payments using QR codes are widely accepted in Guilin’s restaurants, shops, and tourist sites.

Credit and Debit Card Payments

In larger establishments such as hotels, upscale restaurants, and international chain stores, you can often use credit and debit cards directly. However, smaller shops, street vendors, and local eateries might only accept cash or mobile payments.

Preparing for Your Trip

Notify Your Bank

Before traveling to Guilin, inform your bank or credit card provider about your travel plans to avoid having your transactions flagged as suspicious.

Carry Backup Options

Bring a mix of payment options, including:

  • Cash: Always have some local currency for small purchases.
  • Backup Cards: Carry at least one extra card in case of loss or technical issues.

Understand Local Banking Hours

If you need in-person assistance, note that banks in Guilin typically operate from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM on weekdays and may have shorter hours on weekends.

Final Thoughts

Guilin offers a relatively seamless experience for international travelers when it comes to accessing cash through ATMs. By understanding the nuances of the local banking system, preparing for potential challenges, and exploring alternative payment methods, you can ensure a stress-free trip and focus on enjoying the breathtaking beauty of Guilin’s landscapes.
