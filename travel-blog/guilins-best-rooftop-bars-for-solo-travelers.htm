Guilin’s Best Rooftop Bars for Solo Travelers

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin is one of those rare places that feels almost unreal. The limestone karsts rise out of the earth like ancient sculptures, the Li River snakes through the city with a quiet grace, and the air carries a scent of wet stone and blooming osmanthus. For solo travelers, Guilin offers a unique blend of adventure, introspection, and unexpected social encounters. And there is no better way to experience the city’s magic than from above—perched on a rooftop bar, cocktail in hand, watching the sun dip behind the jagged peaks.

Solo travel is about freedom, but it can also be lonely. Rooftop bars solve that problem beautifully. They offer a communal space without forcing conversation, a view that invites reflection, and a staff that often becomes your temporary friend. In Guilin, the rooftop scene is surprisingly vibrant, blending local flavors with international flair. Whether you are a budget backpacker or a luxury seeker, there is a rooftop waiting for you.

Why Rooftop Bars Are Perfect for Solo Travelers in Guilin

Before diving into the specific bars, let’s talk about why rooftops are the solo traveler’s best friend in Guilin.

First, the geography. Guilin is surrounded by karst mountains that look their best at golden hour. From a rooftop, you get a panoramic view that no ground-level café can match. You can see the peaks of Diecai Hill, the winding Li River, and the city lights flickering on. It’s a visual feast that makes eating and drinking alone feel like a cinematic experience.

Second, the social dynamic. Rooftop bars attract a mix of locals, expats, and travelers. As a solo diner or drinker, you are never the odd one out. Bartenders are often chatty, and fellow patrons are usually open to a nod or a brief conversation. If you want solitude, you have it. If you want company, it is available.

Third, the food and drink. Guilin’s rooftop bars are not just about alcohol. Many serve excellent local dishes with a twist, like beer fish with a modern presentation or Guilin rice noodles reimagined as tapas. For solo travelers, this means you can sample multiple flavors without committing to a full meal.

Finally, safety. Rooftop bars are usually in well-trafficked areas with good security. For solo female travelers especially, this is a huge plus. You can relax, enjoy the view, and not worry about your surroundings.

The Top Rooftop Bars in Guilin for Solo Travelers

1. The Roof at Shangri-La Hotel Guilin

Let’s start with the most iconic. The Shangri-La Hotel’s rooftop bar is not just a bar; it’s an experience. Perched on the 10th floor, it offers a 270-degree view of the Li River and the surrounding karst formations. The design is sleek but warm, with wooden accents and soft lighting that make it feel like a private club.

For solo travelers, the appeal here is the service. The bartenders are trained to engage without being intrusive. They will recommend a cocktail based on your mood—try the “Karst Sunset,” a blend of local osmanthus syrup, vodka, and fresh lime. It’s refreshing and slightly floral, perfect for a warm evening.

The food menu is small but curated. The grilled river fish with chili and lemongrass is a must-try. It’s served on a sizzling plate, and the aroma alone will make you forget you are eating alone. Pair it with a glass of sauvignon blanc, and you have a meal that rivals any fine dining experience.

The best time to go is just before sunset. Arrive around 5:30 PM, grab a seat by the railing, and watch the sky turn from blue to orange to purple. The staff will not rush you, so you can linger for hours. It is a bit pricey, but for solo travelers, the ambiance and view are worth every yuan.

2. Mingyue Rooftop Bar at Guilin Bravo Hotel

If you want something more intimate, the Mingyue Rooftop Bar is your spot. Located on the 8th floor of the Guilin Bravo Hotel, this bar is smaller and quieter than the Shangri-La. It has a cozy, almost secret vibe, with bamboo screens and hanging lanterns that create a sense of privacy.

The view here is different—instead of the river, you get a direct line of sight to Diecai Hill and the city’s old town. The rooftops below are a patchwork of traditional tile roofs and modern buildings, and the contrast is visually striking.

For solo travelers, the highlight is the cocktail list. The bartender, Xiao Li, is a local legend. He makes his own infusions using Guilin’s famous Sanhua liquor, a strong rice wine. Try the “Mountain Mist,” which combines Sanhua liquor with lychee juice and a hint of ginger. It’s potent but smooth, and it warms you from the inside out.

The food here is more casual. They serve small plates like fried spring rolls, spicy duck necks, and grilled corn with chili powder. It’s perfect for solo snacking. The staff is friendly and will often sit with you for a few minutes to chat about local life. It is a great place to practice your Mandarin or just listen to the hum of the city below.

3. Sky Bar at Guilin Lijiang Waterfall Hotel

This is the bar for people who love spectacle. The Lijiang Waterfall Hotel is famous for its artificial waterfall, but the rooftop bar is the real hidden gem. Located on the 12th floor, it offers a sweeping view of the city center and the surrounding mountains. The bar itself is modern and minimalist, with white sofas and glass tables that make the view the star.

The crowd here is younger and more energetic. You will find backpackers, digital nomads, and local students celebrating something. For solo travelers, this means you can easily strike up a conversation. The bartenders are young and hip, and they often play indie music or lo-fi beats in the background.

The drink menu is creative. Try the “Li River Breeze,” a mix of gin, elderflower liqueur, cucumber, and soda. It is light and crisp, perfect for sipping while you people-watch. They also have a happy hour from 5 PM to 7 PM, which is a great way to save money as a solo traveler.

Food-wise, they offer a selection of Western and Asian fusion dishes. The beef sliders with wasabi mayo are surprisingly good, and the tempura shrimp tacos are a crowd favorite. Portions are small, so you can order a few things and try different flavors.

One tip: go on a Friday or Saturday night. The bar often has live music or a DJ, and the energy is infectious. Even if you are alone, you will feel like part of the party.

4. The Terrace at Guilin Park Hotel

For solo travelers who prefer a quieter, more refined experience, The Terrace at Guilin Park Hotel is a dream. This rooftop bar is tucked away on the 6th floor of a boutique hotel near Seven Star Park. It is small, with only about 20 seats, but the view is breathtaking. You look out over the park’s lush greenery and the distant karst peaks, and the noise of the city fades away.

The vibe here is elegant but relaxed. The furniture is wicker and wood, and there are potted plants everywhere. It feels like a garden in the sky. The staff is attentive but not hovering, which is perfect for solo travelers who want to read a book or journal while sipping a drink.

The drink menu focuses on wine and champagne. They have a small but well-curated list of French and Italian wines, as well as a few local options. The “Guilin Sparkler” is a local sparkling wine that is surprisingly good—crisp, dry, with a hint of apple. It pairs beautifully with the cheese platter, which includes local cheeses and dried fruits.

If you are hungry, they also serve afternoon tea sets. The “Seven Star Set” includes mini sandwiches, scones, and pastries, all made in-house. It is a bit indulgent for one person, but you can always take leftovers back to your room.

The Terrace is best for early evenings. The light is soft, the temperature is pleasant, and the park below is alive with families and couples. It is a beautiful place to be alone without feeling lonely.

5. Yuzi Paradise Rooftop Bar

This one is a bit of a secret. Yuzi Paradise is an art-themed resort located about 30 minutes from central Guilin, but the rooftop bar is worth the trip. The resort itself is a sculpture garden, with modern art installations scattered across the grounds. The rooftop bar sits on top of the main building and offers a 360-degree view of the surrounding countryside.

The bar is open to non-guests, but you need to take a taxi or a Didi to get there. It is a bit of a journey, but the reward is a view that feels like a painting. The karst peaks stretch out in every direction, and the sky seems bigger here. It is quiet, almost meditative.

The drink menu is simple. They serve beer, wine, and a few cocktails. The “Sculptor’s Dream” is a gin-based cocktail with rosemary and grapefruit, and it is excellent. The food is limited to snacks like nuts and olives, but you can order from the resort’s main restaurant.

For solo travelers, this is a great place to spend an afternoon. Bring a book or a sketchpad, find a corner, and let the view inspire you. The staff is laid-back and will leave you alone unless you need something. It is a true escape from the city.

Tips for Solo Travelers at Guilin’s Rooftop Bars

Now that you know the bars, here are some practical tips to make your solo rooftop experience even better.

Timing Is Everything

The best time to visit a rooftop bar in Guilin is between 5 PM and 7 PM. This is the golden hour, when the light is soft and the mountains glow. It is also when most bars have happy hour specials. Arrive early to get a good seat, especially at popular spots like the Shangri-La.

Dress Comfortably but Smartly

Guilin can be hot and humid, even in the evening. Wear light, breathable fabrics, but avoid looking too casual. Most rooftop bars have a relaxed dress code, but you will feel more confident if you look put-together. A nice shirt or a summer dress works perfectly.

Bring a Book or Journal

Solo dining or drinking can feel awkward if you are just staring at your phone. Bring a book, a journal, or even a travel guide. It gives you something to do and makes you look approachable. Other travelers might strike up a conversation based on what you are reading.

Don’t Be Afraid to Talk to the Bartender

Bartenders are your best friends as a solo traveler. They know the menu, the local area, and often have great stories. Ask them for recommendations, or just chat about Guilin. They will appreciate your interest, and you might learn something new.

Try Local Ingredients

Guilin is famous for its osmanthus flowers, Sanhua liquor, and river fish. Many rooftop bars incorporate these into their cocktails and dishes. Be adventurous. Try something you have never had before. It is part of the solo travel experience.

Stay Safe

Guilin is generally safe, but always be aware of your surroundings. Keep your valuables close, and don’t accept drinks from strangers. If you are a solo female traveler, let the staff know if you feel uncomfortable. They are usually very protective of their guests.

The Solo Traveler’s Rooftop Routine

Here is a sample evening routine that combines the best of Guilin’s rooftop bars.

Start at The Terrace at Guilin Park Hotel around 5 PM. Have a glass of sparkling wine and a cheese platter while you watch the sun begin its descent. The park below will be full of locals practicing tai chi or walking their dogs. It is peaceful and grounding.

Around 6:30 PM, head to the Mingyue Rooftop Bar at Guilin Bravo Hotel. The walk is about 15 minutes, and the streets will be alive with evening activity. Order a “Mountain Mist” cocktail and a plate of fried spring rolls. The view of Diecai Hill is stunning at this hour, and the bar is quiet enough for reflection.

Finally, end your night at Sky Bar at Guilin Lijiang Waterfall Hotel. The energy will be higher, and the music will be louder. Order a “Li River Breeze” and find a spot near the railing. Watch the city lights come on, and let the night wash over you. By the time you leave, you will feel like you have experienced Guilin in a way that few travelers do.

Final Thoughts on Solo Rooftop Exploration

Guilin’s rooftop bars are more than just places to drink. They are vantage points for understanding the city. From above, you see the layers of history, nature, and modernity that make Guilin unique. You see the old women selling vegetables on the street, the tourists snapping photos, the monks walking to temple, and the river flowing endlessly.

For solo travelers, these rooftops offer a rare kind of freedom. You can be alone without being lonely. You can observe without being observed. You can sip a cocktail and feel like the world is yours for a moment.

So go. Find a rooftop. Order something you have never tried. Watch the sun set. And let Guilin work its magic on you. The city is waiting, and the view is spectacular.
