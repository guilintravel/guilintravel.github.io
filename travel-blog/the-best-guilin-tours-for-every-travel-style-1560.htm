The Best Guilin Tours for Every Travel Style

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its surreal karst landscapes, winding rivers, and vibrant culture, is a dream destination for travelers of all kinds. Whether you're an adventure seeker, a luxury traveler, or a culture enthusiast, there’s a perfect Guilin tour tailored just for you. Here’s a breakdown of the best Guilin tours for every travel style.

For the Adventure Junkie: Thrilling Outdoor Escapes

If your idea of a perfect vacation involves adrenaline-pumping activities, Guilin won’t disappoint.

Rock Climbing in Yangshuo

Yangshuo is a rock climber’s paradise, with over 400 bolted routes catering to all skill levels. The limestone peaks offer breathtaking views as you ascend. Local guides provide equipment and training, making it accessible even for beginners.

Bamboo Rafting on the Yulong River

For a more relaxed yet adventurous experience, bamboo rafting on the Yulong River is a must. Unlike the crowded Li River, the Yulong offers a serene escape with fewer tourists. Some tours even include stops at hidden waterfalls and local villages.

Cycling Through the Countryside

Rent a bike and explore Guilin’s rural landscapes at your own pace. The route from Yangshuo to Xingping is particularly scenic, passing through rice paddies, karst formations, and traditional farming villages.

For the Luxury Traveler: Indulge in Elegance

If you prefer five-star comfort and exclusive experiences, Guilin has plenty to offer.

Private Li River Cruise

Skip the crowded tourist boats and opt for a private Li River cruise. These luxury vessels come with gourmet meals, premium wines, and personalized service. The journey from Guilin to Yangshuo is even more magical when enjoyed in privacy.

Stay at a Boutique Resort

Yangshuo’s boutique resorts, like the Banyan Tree or Alila, offer stunning views of the karst mountains along with world-class spas and private pools. Some even arrange sunrise Tai Chi sessions or moonlit dinners by the river.

Helicopter Tours

For the ultimate splurge, book a helicopter tour over Guilin’s iconic landscapes. Seeing the Li River and karst peaks from above is an unforgettable experience.

For the Culture Enthusiast: Dive into Local Traditions

Guilin’s rich history and ethnic diversity make it a cultural goldmine.

Explore Longji Rice Terraces

The Longji (Dragon’s Backbone) Rice Terraces are a marvel of agricultural engineering. Visiting the Zhuang and Yao minority villages here offers insight into traditional farming techniques and local customs. Stay overnight in a wooden guesthouse for an authentic experience.

Visit Ancient Towns like Daxu

Daxu, an ancient town along the Li River, is a living museum of Ming and Qing dynasty architecture. Wander its cobblestone streets, visit centuries-old tea houses, and watch artisans crafting handmade goods.

Learn Traditional Crafts

Take a workshop in Yangshuo to learn Chinese calligraphy, cooking, or even cormorant fishing techniques. These hands-on experiences provide a deeper connection to local culture.

For the Foodie: A Culinary Journey

Guilin’s cuisine is a highlight of any visit, blending spicy, sour, and savory flavors.

Guilin Rice Noodles

No trip is complete without trying Guilin’s famous rice noodles (Guilin mifen). Head to a local market for the most authentic versions, topped with pickled vegetables, peanuts, and chili sauce.

Beer Fish in Yangshuo

Yangshuo’s signature dish, beer fish (pijiu yu), is cooked with local beer and fresh tomatoes. Pair it with a cold LiQuan beer for the full experience.

Cooking Classes

Join a cooking class to learn how to make Guilin’s specialties. Many tours include a market visit to pick fresh ingredients before the lesson.

For the Photographer: Capture the Perfect Shot

Guilin is a visual masterpiece, offering endless opportunities for stunning photos.

Sunrise at Xianggong Hill

For the best panoramic views, hike up Xianggong Hill before dawn. The sunrise over the Li River and karst peaks is a photographer’s dream.

Cormorant Fishing at Dusk

Capture the iconic scene of fishermen using trained cormorants to catch fish under the golden light of sunset. This ancient practice is rare but still performed for cultural preservation.

Moon Hill’s Natural Arch

The Moon Hill in Yangshuo features a natural arch that frames the sky perfectly. Time your visit for soft lighting in the early morning or late afternoon.

For the Family Traveler: Fun for All Ages

Guilin is incredibly family-friendly, with activities that kids and adults will love.

Elephant Trunk Hill

This iconic landmark resembles an elephant drinking from the river. Kids will enjoy climbing the hill and exploring the surrounding park.

Reed Flute Cave

A magical underground world of stalactites and stalagmites illuminated by colorful lights. The cave’s formations have whimsical names like “Crystal Palace” that spark children’s imaginations.

Bamboo Boat Rides

A gentle bamboo boat ride on the Li River is relaxing for parents and exciting for kids. Some tours even include interactive elements like feeding water buffalo.

For the Solo Traveler: Freedom to Explore

Guilin is safe and welcoming for solo travelers, with plenty of opportunities to meet fellow adventurers.

Join a Group Tour

Companies like China Highlights offer small-group tours that balance structure with flexibility. It’s a great way to see the sights while making new friends.

Stay in a Social Hostel

Yangshuo has several hostels with communal spaces, like the Yangshuo Outside Inn, where solo travelers can swap stories and plan outings together.

Night Markets

Guilin’s night markets are perfect for solo exploration. Try street food, shop for souvenirs, and soak up the lively atmosphere.

No matter your travel style, Guilin has a tour that will make your journey unforgettable. From heart-pounding adventures to serene cultural immersions, this destination truly has it all.
