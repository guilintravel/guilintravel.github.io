Family-Friendly Attractions in Guilin

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a city located in southern China, is known for its breathtaking natural landscapes, picturesque mountains, and serene rivers. It is one of the most beautiful destinations in China, drawing tourists from around the world. For families, Guilin offers a wide range of attractions and activities that can be both fun and educational. Whether you're looking to explore unique natural wonders or engage in cultural experiences, Guilin has something for everyone. In this article, we'll explore some of the best family-friendly attractions in Guilin that are sure to create lasting memories for visitors of all ages.

1. Li River Cruise

The Li River is arguably the most famous natural attraction in Guilin, known for its stunning karst mountain landscape. A cruise along the Li River is an excellent way for families to enjoy the natural beauty of the region. The river winds through towering limestone peaks, lush greenery, and picturesque villages. It’s a perfect opportunity to relax, take in the views, and capture memorable photos.

The river cruise typically begins in Guilin and continues down to Yangshuo, a charming town that is also a popular destination for tourists. The boat ride is calm and enjoyable, making it a great choice for families with young children or elderly members. During the cruise, you can spot fishermen using traditional cormorants to catch fish, a fascinating sight for visitors of all ages. Additionally, the peaceful environment of the river provides a wonderful opportunity to unwind and connect with nature.

2. Elephant Trunk Hill

Located in the heart of Guilin, Elephant Trunk Hill is one of the city's most iconic landmarks. The hill is named after its unique shape, which resembles an elephant drinking water from the Li River. This scenic spot is perfect for families to explore, with plenty of activities for children to enjoy, such as walking around the hill, taking photos, and discovering the surrounding park.

At the base of Elephant Trunk Hill, families can stroll through lush gardens, explore caves, and even climb the hill to enjoy panoramic views of the city and the Li River. The hill is not too steep, so it is accessible for families with young children or those with limited mobility. It’s a wonderful place to appreciate both the natural beauty and the cultural significance of Guilin.

3. Reed Flute Cave

One of the most fascinating natural wonders in Guilin is the Reed Flute Cave (Ludi Yan), which is famous for its stunning limestone formations. This cave is a must-see attraction for families, as it offers a unique and exciting experience. The cave is filled with a variety of stalactites, stalagmites, and rock formations that have been shaped over millions of years. The colorful lights inside the cave add an extra layer of magic, making it a visually stunning experience.

The walk through the cave is easy and suitable for visitors of all ages, making it a great option for families. As you stroll through the cave, you’ll encounter different sections with names such as “The Crystal Palace,” “The Dragon Column,” and “The Mushroom Peak,” each offering distinct shapes and colors. Families can take their time exploring and enjoy the beauty of the cave while learning about its geological formation.

4. Seven Star Park

For families looking for a mix of nature, culture, and recreation, Seven Star Park (Qixing Gongyuan) is an excellent choice. This large park is located just outside the city center and offers a variety of attractions that are perfect for families. It’s a great place for a relaxing day out, with lush green spaces, beautiful lakes, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities.

The park is home to several interesting attractions, including the Seven Star Cave, the Camel Hill, and the Ancient Banyan Tree, which is over 1,000 years old. Families can explore the park on foot or rent bicycles to cover more ground. There’s also a zoo in the park, which is home to a variety of animals, including pandas, monkeys, and peacocks. The combination of nature and wildlife makes it a great destination for children to learn and interact with animals.

5. Yangshuo Countryside

Located just outside of Guilin, Yangshuo is a charming town surrounded by stunning karst mountains, rice paddies, and winding rivers. It’s an ideal destination for families who want to experience the rural beauty of southern China. Families can rent bikes or take a bamboo raft ride to explore the picturesque countryside, where they can see farmers at work, traditional villages, and lush landscapes.

Yangshuo is also home to a variety of outdoor activities that are suitable for families, such as rock climbing, kayaking, and even cooking classes. The town is known for its laid-back atmosphere, so families can relax, enjoy local delicacies, and experience the slower pace of rural life. For families with children, Yangshuo is a fantastic place to bond with nature and each other.

6. Guilin Two Rivers and Four Lakes Scenic Area

The Two Rivers and Four Lakes Scenic Area is a large, scenic area in the heart of Guilin that offers a combination of cultural, natural, and recreational experiences. The area consists of Li River, Taohua River, Shanhu Lake, Ronghu Lake, Guihu Lake, and Lijiang River, all of which are interconnected and offer scenic boat tours.

Families can take a relaxing boat cruise around the lakes, passing through beautiful gardens, ancient bridges, and historic sites. The area is beautifully lit up at night, providing a magical atmosphere for evening boat rides. Children will enjoy seeing the pagodas, bridges, and lush greenery that line the rivers, making it an ideal place for family photos and a peaceful escape from the city.

In addition to the boat rides, the area also offers cultural experiences, such as visiting ancient temples and historic landmarks. There are also parks and recreational facilities where families can picnic, take a stroll, or rent bikes.

7. Guilin Forest Park

If your family enjoys nature and hiking, Guilin Forest Park (Guilin Senlin Gongyuan) is a perfect place to visit. Located on the outskirts of the city, this vast park is home to lush forests, clear lakes, and tranquil walking trails. The park is relatively quiet compared to some of the more popular attractions in Guilin, offering families a peaceful and relaxing environment to enjoy the outdoors.

One of the highlights of the park is the Liuhua Lake, where families can enjoy a leisurely boat ride or rent a pedal boat. The park also has various pavilions and tea houses where you can rest and enjoy traditional Chinese tea. The gentle walking paths make it an easy place for families with children or older members to explore without difficulty.

8. Guilin Food Street

No trip to Guilin is complete without experiencing the local food scene, and Guilin Food Street (Guilin Laojie) is the perfect place to sample local specialties. The street is lined with small restaurants and food stalls offering a variety of Guilin dishes, including Guilin rice noodles, beer fish, and dumplings.

For families, this food street is an exciting and interactive way to explore the local culture through food. Parents and children can walk through the bustling street, try different dishes, and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere. It’s a fun and engaging experience for food lovers of all ages, and children can learn about the culinary traditions of Guilin while trying new and delicious dishes.

9. Guilin Tea Culture Center

For families interested in Chinese tea culture, the Guilin Tea Culture Center is a fascinating and educational experience. This cultural center offers interactive tea ceremonies, tea tastings, and demonstrations, allowing families to learn about the history and significance of tea in Chinese culture.

The center is an excellent place for children to discover how tea is made, from picking the leaves to brewing the perfect cup. There are also opportunities for families to try different types of tea, including green tea, oolong tea, and black tea. This cultural experience is both fun and informative, making it a great choice for families looking to dive into Chinese traditions.

10. Fubo Hill

Fubo Hill is a smaller but charming hill located near the Li River. Known for its unique rock formations and beautiful views of the city, Fubo Hill is a peaceful spot for families to visit. The hill is not too steep, making it accessible for families with young children. The surrounding area features pavilions and caves that can be explored, offering a serene environment to take a break from the busier tourist spots.

At the top of Fubo Hill, visitors can enjoy stunning panoramic views of Guilin’s cityscape, mountains, and rivers. It’s a perfect place for families to relax, enjoy the views, and take photos of the beautiful landscape.

Guilin is a city full of natural beauty, rich culture, and unique experiences. For families, it offers a wide variety of attractions that cater to different interests and age groups. Whether you're exploring stunning caves, enjoying a boat ride along the river, or learning about local traditions, Guilin provides endless opportunities for family fun and bonding. With so much to see and do, Guilin is an excellent destination for families looking for adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion.
