Major Attractions in Guilin

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a picturesque city in southern China, is renowned for its breathtaking karst mountains, serene rivers, and lush green landscapes. Its natural beauty has inspired poets and artists for centuries, making it a top travel destination for nature enthusiasts and cultural explorers. Here’s a comprehensive guide to the must-visit attractions in Guilin, each offering a unique glimpse into the city’s charm.

Li River (Lijiang)

The Li River is arguably Guilin’s most iconic attraction, winding its way through stunning karst formations and quaint fishing villages.

  • Cruises:
    A boat cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo is the best way to experience the river. Along the way, you’ll pass landmarks like Elephant Trunk Hill, Nine Horse Fresco Hill, and Xingping Town.
  • Photography:
    The river offers countless photo opportunities, particularly during sunrise or sunset when the landscape is bathed in golden hues.
  • Tips:
    Opt for a bamboo raft if you prefer a more intimate and tranquil experience compared to larger cruise boats.

Reed Flute Cave (Ludi Yan)

Known as the “Palace of Natural Arts,” Reed Flute Cave is a spectacular limestone cavern filled with stalactites, stalagmites, and colorful lighting.

  • Highlights:
    The cave’s formations are illuminated in vibrant colors, creating an otherworldly atmosphere. Key spots include the Crystal Palace and Dragon Pagoda.
  • Accessibility:
    It’s located about 5 kilometers from Guilin’s city center, making it a convenient stop for visitors.
  • Advice:
    Wear comfortable shoes, as the cave floors can be slippery.

Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiangbishan)

This hill is named for its resemblance to an elephant drinking water from the Li River.

  • Significance:
    Elephant Trunk Hill is a symbol of Guilin and is featured in many local artworks and postcards.
  • Activities:
    Visitors can climb the hill for panoramic views of the surrounding area or explore Water Moon Cave at the base of the hill.
  • Best Time to Visit:
    Early morning or late afternoon provides the best lighting for photos and a quieter experience.

Longji Rice Terraces

Located in Longsheng County, about 80 kilometers from Guilin, the Longji Rice Terraces are an engineering marvel and a cultural treasure.

  • Scenic Beauty:
    These terraced fields stretch across the mountainside, creating stunning patterns that change with the seasons—lush green in summer, golden in autumn, and snow-covered in winter.
  • Villages:
    The terraces are home to ethnic minority communities such as the Zhuang and Yao people. Visiting these villages offers a glimpse into their traditions and way of life.
  • Hiking:
    Several trails wind through the terraces, offering varying levels of difficulty and breathtaking views.

Seven Star Park (Qixing Gongyuan)

Seven Star Park, named after its seven peaks, is Guilin’s largest park and a favorite among families and nature lovers.

  • Main Attractions:
    The park features Seven Star Cave, Camel Hill, and Flower Bridge. It’s also home to a zoo where you can see pandas.
  • Relaxation:
    The park’s serene environment makes it an ideal spot for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.
  • Entry Fee:
    The park charges a modest entrance fee, with additional costs for specific attractions.

Yangshuo

While technically outside Guilin, Yangshuo is an essential part of any Guilin itinerary. This charming town is surrounded by dramatic karst landscapes and offers a mix of natural and cultural experiences.

  • West Street (Xi Jie):
    A bustling pedestrian street filled with cafes, shops, and restaurants, West Street is a hub of activity and a great place to experience local culture.
  • Outdoor Adventures:
    Yangshuo is a hotspot for rock climbing, biking, and hiking. The Yulong River is particularly popular for bamboo rafting.
  • Impression Sanjie Liu:
    This outdoor performance, directed by Zhang Yimou, showcases the beauty of the Li River and the local culture through music and dance.

Fubo Hill

Fubo Hill rises dramatically from the Li River and offers a blend of natural beauty and historical significance.

  • Key Features:
    Highlights include Sword-Testing Stone, Thousand-Buddha Cave, and the panoramic views from the top of the hill.
  • Historical Significance:
    The hill’s caves contain ancient carvings and inscriptions dating back over a thousand years.

Daxu Ancient Town

One of the best-preserved ancient towns in Guangxi, Daxu is located along the Li River and offers a journey back in time.

  • Architecture:
    The town features well-preserved Qing Dynasty buildings, stone streets, and historic bridges.
  • Local Life:
    Visiting Daxu provides a glimpse into traditional Chinese rural life, with its teahouses, markets, and workshops.

Guilin Botanical Garden

For plant enthusiasts, the Guilin Botanical Garden is a tranquil retreat featuring diverse flora and beautifully landscaped gardens.

  • Sections:
    The garden includes areas dedicated to tropical plants, medicinal herbs, and ornamental flowers.
  • Relaxation:
    It’s a quiet escape from the city, perfect for those seeking a peaceful outing.

Yao Mountain

Yao Mountain is the highest peak in Guilin and offers sweeping views of the city and surrounding countryside.

  • Cable Car:
    A cable car ride to the summit provides stunning aerial views and is a highlight of any visit.
  • Seasonal Beauty:
    The mountain is particularly beautiful in spring when flowers bloom and in autumn when the leaves turn vibrant colors.

Guilin Night Market

While not a traditional sightseeing spot, the Guilin Night Market is an excellent place to experience the city’s vibrant culture and sample local delicacies.

  • Food and Shopping:
    The market offers everything from street food to handmade crafts. Popular items include Guilin rice noodles and souvenirs like traditional fans.
  • Atmosphere:
    The lively ambiance and colorful stalls make it a fun and memorable experience.

Practical Tips for Visiting Guilin

  • Best Time to Visit:
    The ideal time to visit Guilin is in the spring (April to June) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and the landscapes are at their most vibrant.
  • Transportation:
    Guilin is well-connected by train and air. Within the city, taxis and buses are the most convenient modes of transportation.
  • Accommodations:
    From luxury hotels to budget hostels, Guilin offers a wide range of options. Staying near the city center or in Yangshuo provides easy access to major attractions.

Guilin’s stunning landscapes and cultural heritage make it a must-visit destination for travelers. With its blend of natural wonders and historical treasures, the city promises an unforgettable experience for all who venture there.
