Guilin’s Best Temples for Couples to Visit

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its breathtaking karst landscapes and serene rivers, is a dream destination for couples seeking romance and adventure. But beyond the iconic Li River and Yangshuo’s countryside, Guilin hides another treasure—its ancient temples. These sacred sites offer tranquility, cultural depth, and even a touch of mysticism, making them perfect for couples who want to explore love, spirituality, and history together.

Here’s a curated list of Guilin’s best temples for couples, blending romance, tradition, and Instagram-worthy backdrops.

1. Seven Star Park’s Longyin Temple (七星公园龙隐寺)

A Hidden Gem in Guilin’s Iconic Park

Nestled within Seven Star Park, Longyin Temple is a serene escape from the bustling city. Couples can stroll hand-in-hand through lush greenery before discovering this secluded temple, where the whispers of history and nature intertwine.

Why Couples Love It

  • Romantic Walks: The park’s winding paths and caves (like the famous Seven Star Cave) create an intimate atmosphere.
  • Spiritual Bonding: Light incense together and make a wish for your relationship at the temple’s prayer halls.
  • Panoramic Views: Climb to higher vantage points for stunning views of Guilin’s skyline—perfect for a couple’s photo op.

2. Fubo Hill’s Returned Pearl Pagoda (伏波山还珠洞)

Where Legend Meets Love

Fubo Hill isn’t just a scenic spot—it’s steeped in legend. The Returned Pearl Pagoda, perched on the hill, is tied to a tale of a fisherman who returned a magical pearl to the Dragon King. For couples, this symbolizes loyalty and selflessness—qualities every strong relationship needs.

Highlights for Couples

  • Cave Explorations: Wander through the hill’s caves adorned with ancient Buddhist carvings.
  • Riverside Romance: The nearby Li River offers boat rides at sunset, ideal for post-temple relaxation.
  • Cultural Connection: Learn about Guilin’s folklore together, adding depth to your travels.

3. Xiangbi Mountain’s Guanyin Temple (象鼻山观音寺)

Love Under the Elephant’s Trunk

Xiangbi Mountain (Elephant Trunk Hill) is Guilin’s most recognizable landmark, but few know about the peaceful Guanyin Temple at its base. Dedicated to the Goddess of Mercy, this temple is a sanctuary for couples seeking blessings for harmony and compassion in their relationship.

Couple-Friendly Activities

  • Symbolic Rituals: Write wishes on red ribbons and tie them around the temple’s ancient trees.
  • Scenic Backdrops: Pose for photos with the mountain’s iconic elephant-shaped rock formation.
  • Tea Ceremonies: Nearby teahouses offer private sessions—a calming way to reflect on your journey together.

4. Daxu Ancient Town’s Wanshou Temple (大圩万寿寺)

A Time Capsule for Two

For couples who adore history, Daxu’s Wanshou Temple is a must. This Ming Dynasty temple sits in a well-preserved ancient town, where cobbled streets and riverside views transport you to another era.

Romantic Touches

  • Old-World Charm: Walk along the stone-paved lanes, stopping at antique shops for unique souvenirs.
  • Riverside Picnics: The town’s quiet spots by the Li River are perfect for a private meal.
  • Local Legends: Hear stories of past dynasties from elderly locals, adding a storytelling element to your date.

5. Yao Mountain’s Yaoshan Temple (尧山尧庙)

Love at Guilin’s Highest Point

For adventurous couples, a trip to Yaoshan Temple atop Yao Mountain is unforgettable. Reachable by cable car or hike, this temple offers sweeping views of Guilin’s endless green peaks—a metaphor for love’s boundless possibilities.

Why It’s Perfect for Couples

  • Adrenaline & Serenity: Choose between a thrilling cable car ride or a peaceful hike through pine forests.
  • Sunrise/Sunset Magic: Time your visit for golden-hour views that’ll make your hearts skip a beat.
  • Blessing Rituals: Monks here often bless visitors—a meaningful moment for couples.

6. Nanxi Mountain’s Lingqu Temple (南溪山灵渠寺)

A Waterfront Retreat

Near the ancient Lingqu Canal, this temple combines spiritual vibes with aquatic beauty. Couples can explore the canal’s history before unwinding at the temple’s quiet courtyards.

Couple-Centric Experiences

  • Boat Rides: Drift along the canal, dubbed “The Earliest Contour Canal in the World.”
  • Meditation Sessions: Join a guided meditation to sync your energies.
  • Garden Strolls: The temple’s bonsai gardens are a living art exhibit, inspiring deep conversations.

Tips for Couples Visiting Guilin’s Temples

Dress Respectfully

Many temples require modest attire. Light, breathable fabrics work best in Guilin’s humidity.

Go Early or Late

Avoid crowds by visiting temples at opening or before closing—ideal for quiet moments together.

Capture the Mood

Guilin’s soft light enhances photos. Use the temples’ arches and lanterns for frame-worthy shots.

Try Temple Food

Some temples serve vegetarian meals—a unique culinary experience to share.

Guilin’s temples aren’t just stops on a tour; they’re gateways to deeper connection. Whether you’re seeking blessings, adventure, or simply a break from the ordinary, these sacred spaces promise memories that’ll last far beyond your trip.
