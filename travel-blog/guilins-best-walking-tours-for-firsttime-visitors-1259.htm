Guilin’s Best Walking Tours for First-Time Visitors

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its surreal karst landscapes, winding rivers, and charming rural scenery, is one of China’s most iconic destinations. For first-time visitors, exploring the city on foot is the best way to soak in its natural beauty and cultural richness. Whether you’re strolling along the Li River, wandering through ancient streets, or hiking up scenic viewpoints, Guilin offers unforgettable walking experiences. Here’s a curated list of the best walking tours to make the most of your trip.

The Classic Li River Riverside Walk

No visit to Guilin is complete without a walk along the Li River. This leisurely route offers postcard-perfect views of limestone peaks, bamboo groves, and local fishermen on bamboo rafts.

Starting Point: Diecai Hill Park

Begin your walk near Diecai Hill Park, where you can climb the hill for a panoramic view of Guilin’s skyline and the river. The park itself is a great warm-up before heading toward the riverbank.

Route Highlights

  • Elephant Trunk Hill: Guilin’s most famous landmark, resembling an elephant drinking from the river.
  • Folded Brocade Hill (Diecai Shan): A scenic spot with caves, pavilions, and lush greenery.
  • Liberty Bridge: A perfect spot for photos, especially at sunrise or sunset.

Pro Tip: Early mornings are ideal to avoid crowds and capture the misty river views.

Exploring Guilin’s Ancient Streets

For a taste of local history and culture, take a walking tour through Guilin’s ancient streets, where traditional architecture meets modern cafes and souvenir shops.

Zhengyang Pedestrian Street

This bustling street is lined with Ming and Qing dynasty-style buildings, now housing teahouses, handicraft stores, and street food vendors. Don’t miss:
- Guilin Rice Noodles: A must-try local dish.
- Handmade Souvenirs: Look for intricate paper umbrellas and embroidered silks.

Dongxi Alley

A quieter alternative, Dongxi Alley offers a glimpse into old Guilin with its well-preserved courtyards and hidden cafes.

Yangshuo Countryside Walk

A short drive from Guilin, Yangshuo is a paradise for walkers. The countryside here is dotted with rice paddies, karst peaks, and quaint villages.

Yulong River Bamboo Trail

This tranquil path follows the Yulong River, far less crowded than the Li River. Highlights include:
- Bamboo Rafting: Optional but highly recommended for a different perspective.
- Moon Hill: A short detour leads to this natural arch with stunning views.

Xingping Village Walk

Famous for being on the 20 RMB banknote, Xingping offers:
- Laozhai Mountain: A moderate hike rewarding you with a bird’s-eye view of the Li River’s bends.
- Local Fishing Villages: Meet fishermen using cormorants to catch fish—a dying tradition.

Seven Star Park Exploration

Guilin’s largest park, Seven Star Park, is a fantastic place for a half-day walking tour, combining nature, caves, and cultural sites.

Must-See Spots

  • Seven Star Cave: An illuminated underground wonderland.
  • Flower Bridge: A historic bridge perfect for photos.
  • Camel Hill: A rock formation resembling a kneeling camel.

Best Time to Visit: Late afternoon when the light is soft, and the park is less crowded.

Night Walk Along Two Rivers and Four Lakes

Guilin’s Two Rivers and Four Lakes scenic area is magical after dark, with illuminated bridges, pagodas, and tree-lined promenades.

Route Suggestions

  • Start at Sun and Moon Twin Pagodas: These glittering towers reflect beautifully on the water.
  • Walk Along Fir Lake: Stop by the ancient Banyan Tree, a symbol of Guilin.
  • End at Glass Bridge: A modern architectural marvel lit up at night.

Insider Tip: Take a short boat ride for a different vantage point of the city lights.

Practical Tips for Walking Tours in Guilin

  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes are a must, especially for countryside trails.
  • Weather: Spring and autumn are ideal; summers can be hot and humid.
  • Local Guides: Consider hiring a guide for hidden gems and cultural insights.
  • Hydration: Carry water, as some routes have limited vendors.

Guilin’s walking tours offer an intimate way to connect with its landscapes and traditions. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, there’s a path waiting for you.
