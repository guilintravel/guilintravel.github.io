Guilin on a Budget: Travel Costs & Money-Saving Tips

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its iconic karst mountains, serene rivers, and lush landscapes, is a dream destination for travelers. But exploring this picturesque region doesn’t have to break the bank. With smart planning and insider tips, you can experience Guilin’s beauty without overspending. Here’s your ultimate guide to traveling Guilin on a budget.

How Much Does a Trip to Guilin Cost?

Before diving into money-saving strategies, let’s break down the average costs for a budget-friendly trip to Guilin.

Accommodation Costs

  • Hostels & Guesthouses: $5–$15 per night for a dorm bed or basic private room.
  • Budget Hotels: $20–$40 per night for a comfortable, clean room.
  • Mid-Range Hotels: $50–$80 per night for added amenities like breakfast or river views.

Pro tip: Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak seasons (April–October), to secure the best rates.

Food & Dining

  • Street Food & Local Eateries: $1–$3 per dish (try Guilin米粉—Guilin rice noodles!).
  • Mid-Range Restaurants: $5–$10 per meal.
  • Western or Upscale Dining: $15+ per meal.

Eating like a local is the key to saving money. Skip touristy spots near attractions and head to neighborhoods like Zhengyang Pedestrian Street for affordable, authentic meals.

Transportation

  • Public Buses: $0.30–$0.50 per ride.
  • Bike Rentals: $1–$3 per day.
  • Taxis: $3–$10 for short trips (use ride-hailing apps like Didi for better rates).
  • Intercity Buses/Trains: $5–$20 for trips to Yangshuo or Longsheng.

Attractions & Activities

  • Li River Cruise (Budget Option): $30–$50 (opt for the shorter Yangshuo route).
  • Bike Rental + Yangshuo Countryside Tour: $5–$10.
  • Elephant Trunk Hill: $10 entry fee.
  • Free Hikes: Xianggong Hill (free, stunning sunrise views).

Top Money-Saving Tips for Guilin

1. Travel During the Shoulder Season

Peak season (summer and holidays) means higher prices. Visit in March–April or September–October for pleasant weather and lower costs.

2. Use Public Transportation

Guilin’s buses are cheap and efficient. For longer distances, trains are often cheaper than flights.

3. Eat Local

Avoid restaurants with English menus near tourist sites. Instead, try:
- Guilin Rice Noodles (米粉): A must-try for under $2.
- Stuffed Li River Snails (田螺酿): A local delicacy.
- Night Markets: Zhengyang Street and Dongxi Lane offer cheap, tasty bites.

4. Skip the Expensive Li River Cruise

Instead of the full-day luxury cruise ($100+), take:
- The Bamboo Raft from Xingping to Yangshuo ($20–$30).
- A Public Ferry (cheaper but less scenic).

5. Free & Low-Cost Activities

  • Hike Xianggong Hill for panoramic views.
  • Explore Yangshuo’s Countryside by bike (rent for $2/day).
  • Walk Along the Li River at sunset.

6. Bargain at Markets

Vendors often inflate prices for tourists. Politely negotiate for souvenirs, especially at:
- West Street (Yangshuo).
- Guilin Night Markets.

7. Stay in Hostels with Perks

Many hostels offer free:
- Walking tours.
- Bike rentals.
- Local tips from staff.

8. Book Tickets Online

Platforms like Ctrip or Klook often have discounts for attractions like:
- Reed Flute Cave.
- Seven Star Park.

Budget-Friendly Itinerary for 3 Days

Day 1: Guilin City Highlights

  • Morning: Elephant Trunk Hill ($10).
  • Afternoon: Free walk along Two Rivers and Four Lakes.
  • Evening: Zhengyang Pedestrian Street for cheap eats.

Day 2: Yangshuo Adventure

  • Morning: Bus to Yangshuo ($5), bike rental ($3).
  • Afternoon: Free hike to Moon Hill or a bamboo raft ($25).
  • Evening: West Street exploration (budget dinner $5).

Day 3: Longsheng Rice Terraces (Optional)

  • Day Trip: Bus to Longsheng ($15), hike Ping’an Village (free).

Final Thoughts

Guilin’s natural beauty is accessible even for budget travelers. By prioritizing local experiences, using public transport, and avoiding tourist traps, you can enjoy this stunning region without overspending. Pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready for an affordable adventure in Guilin!
