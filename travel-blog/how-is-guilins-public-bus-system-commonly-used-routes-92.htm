How Is Guilin's Public Bus System? Commonly Used Routes

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, famous for its breathtaking karst landscapes and Li River, is also known for being a traveler-friendly city. Its public bus system is an essential part of the city's infrastructure, making it easy for both locals and tourists to navigate. Guilin’s bus system is efficient, affordable, and well-connected, offering access to major attractions and residential areas alike. If you're visiting Guilin, understanding the city's bus network can greatly enhance your travel experience.

Overview of Guilin’s Bus System

Affordability

Guilin’s bus fares are budget-friendly, with most rides costing around 1 to 2 yuan. Some air-conditioned buses charge slightly more, typically 2 to 3 yuan. Payment can be made in cash, or you can use a prepaid transportation card (such as the Guilin Tong card) for convenience.

Operating Hours

Most buses operate between 6:00 AM and 10:30 PM, although some routes may have extended hours or night service. It’s advisable to check the specific schedule for your route beforehand.

Accessibility

Guilin’s buses are generally well-maintained and accessible. Many newer buses are equipped with low floors, making them suitable for seniors and people with disabilities. Announcements are made in both Mandarin and English on many routes, ensuring tourists can navigate without language barriers.

Environmental Commitment

The city has introduced a growing fleet of electric and hybrid buses, aligning with its efforts to promote sustainable tourism and reduce emissions.

Commonly Used Bus Routes

Route 1: Guilin Railway Station to Qixing Park

This route is particularly popular among tourists because it connects the bustling Guilin Railway Station with key attractions like Qixing (Seven Star) Park. The park is renowned for its natural beauty, caves, and cultural relics, making this bus route a great choice for sightseeing.

Route 2: Guilin Liangjiang International Airport Shuttle

Although not a traditional city bus, the airport shuttle is a vital part of Guilin's transit network. The shuttle connects Liangjiang International Airport to the city center, with stops at major hotels and transportation hubs. The fare is higher than standard city buses, typically around 20 to 30 yuan, but it offers comfort and convenience for travelers.

Route 10: Guilin Bus Station to Reed Flute Cave

Reed Flute Cave, known for its stunning limestone formations and colorful lighting, is a must-see destination. Route 10 provides a direct connection from Guilin Bus Station to this iconic attraction.

Route 23: City Center to Elephant Trunk Hill

Elephant Trunk Hill is one of Guilin's most famous landmarks, resembling an elephant drinking water from the Li River. Route 23 is a convenient option for reaching this attraction, with frequent service throughout the day.

Route 58: Guilin North Railway Station to Downtown Guilin

For travelers arriving by high-speed train at Guilin North Railway Station, Route 58 offers an easy way to get to the city center. This route is especially useful for those planning to visit Zhongshan Road, a popular shopping and dining area.

Route 91: Guilin to Yangshuo

Although it’s a longer ride, Route 91 connects Guilin to Yangshuo, a picturesque county famous for its karst mountains and outdoor activities. While the route can take over an hour, it’s an economical alternative to private transportation.

Tips for Using Guilin’s Bus System

Download Navigation Apps

Apps like Baidu Maps or WeChat’s transportation feature can provide real-time bus schedules, route information, and estimated arrival times. These apps often support English interfaces, making them user-friendly for international travelers.

Use the Guilin Tong Card

Purchasing a Guilin Tong card is highly recommended for frequent bus users. This rechargeable card allows you to board buses more quickly and eliminates the need to carry exact change. It’s available at transportation hubs and convenience stores.

Plan Ahead for Tourist Routes

Some popular routes, like those to Reed Flute Cave or Elephant Trunk Hill, can get crowded, especially during peak tourist seasons. Try to board early in the day or during off-peak hours to avoid long waits.

Be Prepared for Limited Night Service

Although Guilin’s bus network is extensive during the day, night service is limited. If you’re planning a late outing, consider alternative transportation options like taxis or ride-hailing apps.

Exploring Guilin’s Main Attractions by Bus

Li River Cruise

While the Li River Cruise itself isn’t part of the bus network, buses to the Zhujiang Wharf departure point are available. Check routes like 98 or dedicated tourist shuttles for convenient access.

Longji Rice Terraces

To reach the stunning Longji Rice Terraces, you’ll need to take a combination of buses. Start with a city bus to Guilin Bus Station, then transfer to an intercity bus heading toward Longsheng County. From there, smaller shuttle buses can take you to the terraces.

Daxu Ancient Town

Buses to Daxu Ancient Town, a well-preserved site with Ming and Qing dynasty architecture, are less frequent but still accessible. Check with local bus stations for specific routes and departure times.

Environmental and Cultural Impact

Guilin’s commitment to eco-tourism is evident in its transition to electric buses and efforts to minimize pollution. Additionally, riding local buses offers a unique cultural experience, allowing visitors to observe daily life and interact with locals.

Challenges and Solutions

Language Barriers

While many bus announcements are bilingual, not all routes offer English translations. Carrying a map or using a translation app can help bridge the gap.

Crowded Buses

During peak hours or tourist seasons, buses can become crowded. To avoid discomfort, consider traveling early in the morning or later in the afternoon.

Limited Rural Access

For destinations outside Guilin’s urban area, like the Longji Rice Terraces or Yangshuo, intercity buses or private tours may be more convenient than public buses.

Final Thoughts

Guilin’s bus system is an affordable, efficient, and eco-friendly way to explore the city and its surrounding attractions. Whether you’re visiting iconic landmarks like Elephant Trunk Hill or venturing to natural wonders like Reed Flute Cave, the network provides reliable transportation options. By planning ahead and familiarizing yourself with commonly used routes, you can navigate Guilin like a local and fully immerse yourself in its rich culture and stunning scenery.
