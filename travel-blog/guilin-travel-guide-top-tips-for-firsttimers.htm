Guilin Travel Guide: Top Tips for First-Timers

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, often referred to as the "crown jewel of China's landscape," is a dream destination for travelers seeking breathtaking natural beauty. With its iconic karst mountains, winding rivers, and lush countryside, this city in Guangxi Province offers an unforgettable experience. Whether you're a nature lover, adventure seeker, or culture enthusiast, Guilin has something for everyone. Here’s everything you need to know before your first visit.

Why Guilin Should Be on Your Travel List

Guilin’s scenery is so legendary that it’s featured on China’s 20 RMB banknote. The city is famous for its:

  • Karst Landscapes – Towering limestone peaks that look like something from a fantasy novel.
  • Li River – A serene waterway that offers some of the best river cruises in the world.
  • Rice Terraces – The nearby Longji Rice Terraces are a masterpiece of agricultural engineering.
  • Cultural Heritage – From ancient villages to minority ethnic cultures, Guilin is rich in history.

Best Time to Visit Guilin

Timing your trip right can make all the difference. Here’s a seasonal breakdown:

Spring (March to May)

  • Mild weather with blooming flowers.
  • Ideal for outdoor activities like hiking and cycling.

Summer (June to August)

  • Peak tourist season with warm temperatures.
  • Great for river cruises, but expect crowds.

Autumn (September to November)

  • The most recommended season—cool weather and clear skies.
  • Perfect for photography, especially along the Li River.

Winter (December to February)

  • Fewer tourists and lower prices.
  • Somewhat chilly, but the misty landscapes are magical.

Must-Visit Attractions in Guilin

1. Li River Cruise (Yangshuo Route)

A cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo is the highlight of any trip. The 4-hour journey takes you past:
- Elephant Trunk Hill – Guilin’s most famous landmark.
- Nine Horses Fresco Hill – A cliff face resembling galloping horses.
- Xingping Ancient Town – A charming riverside village.

Pro Tip: Book a bamboo raft for a more intimate experience on the Yulong River.

2. Yangshuo County

Yangshuo is a backpacker’s paradise with:
- West Street (Xi Jie) – A lively street full of cafes, bars, and souvenir shops.
- Moon Hill – A natural arch perfect for a short hike.
- Biking Adventures – Rent a bike and explore the countryside.

3. Longji Rice Terraces

About 2.5 hours from Guilin, these terraces are a marvel of human ingenuity.
- Best Viewpoints: Ping’an Village and Jinkeng Terraces.
- Best Time to Visit: Spring (water-filled terraces) and Autumn (golden harvest).

4. Reed Flute Cave

A mesmerizing underground world of stalactites and stalagmites illuminated by colorful lights.

5. Two Rivers and Four Lakes Scenic Area

A picturesque urban park where you can take a night cruise to see illuminated bridges and pagodas.

Unique Experiences in Guilin

Cormorant Fishing Shows

Watch fishermen use trained cormorant birds to catch fish—a tradition dating back centuries.

Learn to Cook Guilin Cuisine

Take a cooking class and master dishes like:
- Guilin Rice Noodles (Mifen) – A breakfast staple.
- Beer Fish (Pijiu Yu) – A Yangshuo specialty.

Tai Chi by the Li River

Join locals for a peaceful morning Tai Chi session with a stunning mountain backdrop.

Practical Tips for First-Time Visitors

Getting Around

  • Airport: Guilin Liangjiang International Airport (KWL) has flights from major Chinese cities.
  • Trains: High-speed trains connect Guilin to cities like Guangzhou and Chengdu.
  • Local Transport: Taxis and Didi (China’s Uber) are affordable. Biking is great in Yangshuo.

Where to Stay

  • Luxury: Shangri-La Hotel Guilin (riverside views).
  • Mid-Range: Yangshuo Mountain Retreat (countryside charm).
  • Budget: Hostels in Yangshuo’s West Street area.

Money & Language

  • Currency: Chinese Yuan (CNY). ATMs are widely available.
  • Language: English is limited outside tourist areas. Learn basic Mandarin phrases or use translation apps.

Avoiding Tourist Scams

  • Only book tours through reputable agencies.
  • Agree on taxi fares before getting in.
  • Be cautious of overpriced souvenirs in touristy areas.

Final Thoughts

Guilin is more than just a destination—it’s an experience that stays with you long after you leave. From cruising the Li River to exploring ancient villages, every moment here feels like stepping into a painting. With these tips, your first trip to Guilin will be smooth, memorable, and filled with wonder.
