Guilin Travel Map: Best Photo Spots and Viewpoints

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

The name Guilin has long been synonymous with breathtaking natural beauty. "East or west, Guilin landscape is best!" is not merely a local saying but a truth etched into the very limestone karsts that dominate its skyline. For any traveler, and especially for the photographer, Guilin is less a destination and more of a living, breathing canvas. This guide is your essential travel map, designed to lead you through the misty rivers and towering peaks to the very spots where you can capture the soul of this magical region.

The Heart of Guilin: Li River & Elephant Trunk Hill

No journey to Guilin is complete without immersing oneself in the scenery that has inspired Chinese poets and painters for centuries. The city itself offers immediate access to iconic views.

Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiangbishan)

This is arguably the most recognizable landmark in Guilin. Located at the confluence of the Li River and the Peach Blossom River, this hill naturally resembles a giant elephant dipping its trunk into the water. It’s the quintessential Guilin photo.

Best Photo Spots: * Across the River: The classic shot is taken from the opposite bank, using a wide-angle lens to capture the full reflection in the calm morning water. * Sunrise at Xiangbishan: Arrive before dawn. As the sun rises behind the city, it casts a warm, golden glow on the elephant, with the sky shifting through a spectrum of colors. The mist often lingering on the river adds a mystical quality. * Moon Tower: On the hill itself, the Moon Tower frames a perfect circular "moon" through which you can photograph the city, creating a unique composition of nature and architecture.

Two Rivers and Four Lakes Scenic Area

This intricately linked water system in the city center is a masterpiece of urban landscaping. Canals, pagodas, and bridges connect the lakes, offering a serene and photogenic environment day and night.

Best Photo Spots: * Sun and Moon Twin Pagodas (Riyue Shuangta): The golden Sun Pagoda and the silver-glazed Moon Pagoda are stunning, especially after sunset when they are brilliantly illuminated. The best vantage point is from the shore of Shanhu Lake, capturing their perfect reflection on the water's surface. A long exposure shot here can create a magical, glass-like effect. * Ancient Banyan Trees and Glass Bridge: The combination of ancient, gnarled banyan trees and the modern, crystalline Glass Bridge provides a fascinating contrast. Shoot from under the canopy of the trees to frame the bridge.

The Crown Jewel: Yangshuo's Karst Landscape

While Guilin city is the gateway, Yangshuo is the main event. The landscape here is more dramatic, with karst peaks sprouting from the earth in every direction.

Yulong River

Often called the "Little Li River," the Yulong is in many ways more intimate and peaceful. The best way to experience it is via a bamboo raft ride.

Best Photo Spots: * Bamboo Rafting: Capture the journey itself. A shot from one raft looking at another drifting through the serene landscape, with water buffalo grazing on the banks, is a timeless image. * The Yulong River Bridge: This simple, ancient stone bridge is a fantastic subject. Photograph it at sunset, with the silhouettes of karst peaks in the background and the golden light reflecting off the river. * Riverside Rice Paddies: During the growing season, the vibrant green of the rice paddies against the grey karsts is a stunning contrast. Find a high spot along the riverbank for a sweeping landscape shot.

Xingping Ancient Town and the Li River

This is where the landscape found its way onto the 20 RMB banknote. Xingping retains an old-world charm that feels a world away from the bustle of Yangshuo.

Best Photo Spots: * The 20 RMB Viewpoint: Follow the signs in Xingping to the official photo spot. While it can be busy, the view is iconic. For a more unique take, walk a few hundred meters in either direction along the riverbank to find your own composition of the same peaks. * Laozhai Mountain (Lao Zhai Shan): For the most dedicated photographers, a hike up Laozhai Mountain is a must. The 30-45 minute steep climb is rewarded with a 360-degree panoramic view of the Li River winding its way through a forest of karst peaks. This is the ultimate location for sunrise or sunset photography. * Fishing with Cormorants: While now largely staged for tourists, the image of a fisherman on a bamboo raft with his cormorant birds, silhouetted against the setting sun, is an enduring symbol of the region. Early morning or late evening is the best time to capture this scene.

Beyond the Rivers: Unique Vantage Points

To truly capture the diversity of Guilin's beauty, you must venture away from the water and into the fields and up the mountains.

Longji Rice Terraces (Dragon's Backbone)

Located a few hours from Guilin city, this is a landscape of a different scale and character. Carved into the mountainsides over centuries by the Zhuang and Yao people, the terraces are a breathtaking feat of human ingenuity.

Best Photo Spots: * Jinkeng Red Yao Terraces - "Thousand-Layer Cake to the Sky": This area offers the most spectacular views. The cable car ride up provides an aerial perspective of the sweeping curves. * Ping'an Zhuang Terraces - "Seven Stars Around the Moon": This famous formation is best photographed from the "Moon Viewing" platform. The terraces form a spiral around a central core, resembling stars and the moon. * Seasonal Variations: The best time to photograph the terraces depends on the look you want. * Spring (April-June): The terraces are filled with water, creating incredible mirror-like reflections of the sky. * Summer (July-August): Vibrant, lush green rice shoots cover the mountains. * Autumn (Late Sept-Oct): The golden, ripe rice creates a stunning landscape of warm, rich colors. * Winter (Occasionally): A dusting of snow creates a rare and magical black-and-white scene.

Xianggong Mountain

For those who want the grand panorama of the Li River without an arduous hike, Xianggong Mountain is the answer. It's a short, manageable climb to a purpose-built viewing platform.

Best Photo Spots: * The Viewing Platform at Sunrise: This is the primary reason to come here. The platform offers an unobstructed view of the Li River making a dramatic, horseshoe-shaped bend through a sea of karst peaks. On a good morning, a sea of clouds (yun hai) forms below the peaks, creating a truly heavenly scene. It’s a landscape photographer's dream.

Cultural Frames: Adding Life to Your Landscape

A great travel photo often tells a story beyond the scenery. Incorporating local culture adds depth and context to your Guilin portfolio.

West Street (Yangshuo)

While chaotic, this street is the cultural pulse of Yangshuo. The challenge and reward lie in capturing its vibrant energy.

Best Photo Spots: * Long Exposure at Dusk: As the neon signs light up and the crowds move, use a tripod for a long exposure. This will turn the moving people into artistic blurs, contrasting with the sharp, historic architecture of the buildings. * Candid Street Portraits: With permission, capture the faces of the vendors, the street food chefs, and the musicians. The mix of traditional and modern life here is fascinating.

Liu Sanjie Light Show

Directed by Zhang Yimou, this open-air show uses the Li River itself as a stage, with the karst peaks as a natural backdrop.

Best Photo Spots: * From the Audience: While flash and professional cameras are often restricted, a high-quality smartphone or a camera with a good high-ISO performance can capture the scale and color of the performance. * From a Boat on the River: Some private tours offer a view from the river, providing a completely different and less obstructed perspective of the spectacle.

Exploring the Countryside by Bike

Renting a bike is the best way to stumble upon unplanned, authentic photo opportunities.

Best Photo Spots: * The Yulong River Valley: The paths along the river lead through small villages, ancient bridges, and open fields. You'll find farmers at work, children playing, and water buffalo cooling off—all with the stunning karst landscape as your constant backdrop. * Hidden Lanes: Don't be afraid to turn off the main path. Some of the most memorable photos come from the quiet, hidden lanes where daily life unfolds in a timeless manner.
