A 6-Day Guilin and Huangyao Ancient Town Itinerary

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin and Huangyao Ancient Town are two of China’s most picturesque destinations, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. This 6-day itinerary will take you through the best of both worlds—from the iconic karst landscapes of Guilin to the timeless charm of Huangyao’s cobblestone streets. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, this trip has something for everyone.

Day 1: Arrival in Guilin and City Highlights

Morning: Arrival and Check-In

Your adventure begins as you land in Guilin, a city renowned for its stunning limestone formations and the Li River. After checking into your hotel (options range from luxury stays like the Shangri-La to boutique guesthouses), freshen up and grab a quick breakfast.

Afternoon: Exploring Elephant Trunk Hill and Reed Flute Cave

Start your exploration with Elephant Trunk Hill, Guilin’s most famous landmark. Shaped like an elephant drinking from the river, this natural wonder is a must-see. Snap some photos and enjoy the surrounding park.

Next, head to the Reed Flute Cave, a mesmerizing underground world of stalactites and stalagmites illuminated by colorful lights. The cave’s name comes from the reeds growing outside, which were once used to make flutes.

Evening: Strolling Along Two Rivers and Four Lakes

As the sun sets, take a leisurely walk or a boat cruise along the Two Rivers and Four Lakes scenic area. The illuminated bridges and pagodas create a magical atmosphere. For dinner, try local specialties like Guilin rice noodles or beer fish at a riverside restaurant.

Day 2: Li River Cruise to Yangshuo

Morning: Scenic Li River Cruise

No trip to Guilin is complete without a Li River cruise to Yangshuo. Board a traditional bamboo raft or a larger tourist boat and glide past towering karst peaks, water buffaloes, and fishing villages. The scenery is so breathtaking that it’s featured on the 20 RMB bill!

Afternoon: Yangshuo Countryside Exploration

Upon arriving in Yangshuo, rent a bike or an e-bike to explore the countryside. Ride through rice paddies, visit the Yulong River, and stop by small villages like Xingping, where you can see the iconic view of the karst mountains reflected in the water.

Evening: West Street and Impression Sanjie Liu

In the evening, wander through West Street, a lively pedestrian area filled with cafes, bars, and souvenir shops. Cap off the night with Impression Sanjie Liu, a spectacular outdoor performance directed by Zhang Yimou, set against the natural backdrop of the Li River.

Day 3: Adventure and Relaxation in Yangshuo

Morning: Hot Air Balloon Ride or Rock Climbing

For thrill-seekers, start the day with a hot air balloon ride over Yangshuo’s karst landscape. Alternatively, try rock climbing on one of the area’s limestone cliffs—Yangshuo is a global hotspot for climbers.

Afternoon: Cooking Class or Bamboo Rafting

Foodies can join a Guilin cooking class to learn how to make dishes like stuffed Li River snails or stir-fried water spinach. If you prefer relaxation, opt for a bamboo rafting trip on the Yulong River, where you’ll float past idyllic scenery.

Evening: Free Time in Yangshuo

Spend your last evening in Yangshuo soaking up the laid-back vibe. Enjoy a craft beer at a rooftop bar or shop for handmade souvenirs like painted fans and embroidered textiles.

Day 4: Journey to Huangyao Ancient Town

Morning: Transfer to Huangyao

After breakfast, take a private car or bus (about 3 hours) to Huangyao Ancient Town, a well-preserved Ming- and Qing-dynasty village. Unlike more touristy spots, Huangyao retains an authentic, tranquil charm.

Afternoon: Exploring Huangyao’s Highlights

Check into a traditional guesthouse and begin exploring. Key sights include:
- Dai Long Bridge: A picturesque stone bridge perfect for photos.
- Ancient Residences: Wander through centuries-old homes with intricate carvings.
- Old Banyan Trees: These massive trees are considered sacred by locals.

Evening: Nighttime Ambiance

As night falls, lanterns light up the cobblestone streets, creating a dreamy atmosphere. Enjoy a quiet dinner at a family-run restaurant, sampling local dishes like tofu pudding and oil tea.

Day 5: Immersive Cultural Experiences in Huangyao

Morning: Sunrise and Local Markets

Wake up early to catch the sunrise over the town’s misty rooftops. Then, visit the morning market, where villagers sell fresh produce, handmade crafts, and snacks like sticky rice dumplings.

Afternoon: Traditional Workshops

Participate in a bean paste-making workshop or try your hand at calligraphy. Huangyao is known for its fermented black bean paste, a staple in Guangxi cuisine.

Evening: Folk Performances

If you’re lucky, you might catch a traditional folk opera or drum performance in the town square. Alternatively, enjoy a quiet evening sipping tea by the river.

Day 6: Return to Guilin and Departure

Morning: Final Stroll and Souvenir Shopping

Before heading back to Guilin, take one last walk through Huangyao’s alleys. Pick up souvenirs like handmade soy sauce or embroidered cloth.

Afternoon: Back to Guilin and Farewell

Return to Guilin (about 3 hours) and, if time allows, visit Sun and Moon Pagodas for a final glimpse of the city’s beauty. Then, it’s off to the airport for your journey home—or to your next adventure!

This 6-day itinerary balances adventure, culture, and relaxation, ensuring you experience the best of Guilin and Huangyao. From the dramatic landscapes of the Li River to the timeless charm of ancient alleyways, every moment is a postcard-worthy memory.
