Best One-Day Trip Destinations Around Guilin

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, located in southern China’s Guangxi province, is famous for its breathtaking karst landscapes, serene rivers, and rich cultural heritage. While the city itself offers numerous attractions, the surrounding areas are filled with stunning destinations that make for perfect one-day trips. Whether you are looking for dramatic mountains, picturesque villages, or immersive cultural experiences, there are plenty of places near Guilin to explore.

Yangshuo: The Iconic Karst Wonderland

Located about an hour’s drive from Guilin, Yangshuo is one of the most famous destinations in the region. Known for its mesmerizing limestone peaks, winding rivers, and vibrant local culture, it is a must-visit for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Li River Cruise or Bamboo Rafting

One of the best ways to experience Yangshuo’s beauty is by taking a Li River cruise or bamboo rafting along the Yulong River. The Li River route from Guilin to Yangshuo offers stunning views, including the famous "Nine Horses Fresco Hill" and the scene depicted on the 20-yuan banknote. Bamboo rafting on the Yulong River provides a more intimate and peaceful journey through the karst landscape.

Cycling Through the Countryside

Renting a bike and cycling through the picturesque countryside is a fantastic way to explore Yangshuo. Popular routes include:

  • The Yulong River Scenic Area, where you can pass through rice fields, ancient stone bridges, and small villages.
  • The Ten-Mile Gallery, a scenic road featuring stunning peaks and caves.

Moon Hill

This natural rock formation with a large, circular hole at its peak is one of Yangshuo’s iconic landmarks. A short but steep hike (about 800 steps) rewards you with panoramic views of the surrounding karst landscape.

West Street

For those looking to experience Yangshuo’s vibrant atmosphere, West Street is the place to go. This historic street is lined with shops, cafés, and restaurants offering a mix of Chinese and Western cuisine. It is also a great place to enjoy live music and nightlife.

Longji Rice Terraces: The Dragon’s Backbone

About two hours north of Guilin, the Longji Rice Terraces are among the most spectacular terraced fields in China. These terraces, built over 600 years ago by the Zhuang and Yao ethnic groups, create breathtaking patterns across the mountains.

Dazhai vs. Ping’an Villages

The two main areas to explore are:

  • Dazhai Village, known for its stunning views and longer hiking trails. The cable car here offers an easy way to reach the highest viewpoints.
  • Ping’an Village, the more tourist-friendly option with easy-to-access viewpoints such as "Seven Stars with Moon" and "Nine Dragons and Five Tigers."

Best Times to Visit

  • Spring (April–May): When the terraces are filled with water, creating a mirror-like effect.
  • Summer (June–July): When the fields are lush and green.
  • Autumn (September–October): When the terraces turn golden before the harvest.

Daxu Ancient Town: A Glimpse into the Past

Located about 20 kilometers southeast of Guilin, Daxu Ancient Town is one of the best-preserved historical towns in the region. With a history of over 1,000 years, this town offers a step back in time with its well-preserved Qing dynasty architecture, stone-paved streets, and traditional wooden houses.

Highlights of Daxu Ancient Town

  • Longevity Bridge, a beautiful old stone bridge over the Li River.
  • Ancient Shops and Residences, showcasing traditional Chinese architecture.
  • Local Markets, where you can taste traditional Guangxi snacks and buy handmade crafts.

Unlike more commercialized ancient towns in China, Daxu remains relatively quiet and authentic, making it an ideal spot for cultural exploration.

Silver Cave: A Hidden Underground Wonderland

Located about 85 kilometers from Guilin, Silver Cave is one of the most spectacular karst caves in the region. It stretches over 2 kilometers and features stunning stalactites and stalagmites formed over thousands of years. The cave is divided into three sections, with highlights including:

  • The Snow-Mountain Waterfall, a sparkling white rock formation resembling cascading water.
  • The Music Stone Screen, which creates musical sounds when tapped.
  • The Buddha’s Hand, a naturally formed rock resembling a giant hand.

Silver Cave is beautifully lit with colorful lights, enhancing its otherworldly appearance. It is best visited in the morning to avoid crowds.

Xingping Ancient Town: The Best View of the Li River

Xingping, located 25 kilometers north of Yangshuo, is a charming old town with some of the best views of the Li River. The area is famous for its breathtaking scenery, especially the "20-Yuan Banknote Viewpoint," which showcases the landscape printed on China’s 20-yuan note.

What to Do in Xingping

  • Hike to Laozhai Hill for a panoramic view of the Li River and surrounding karst peaks.
  • Explore the Old Town, where well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty buildings create a historic atmosphere.
  • Take a River Cruise to see some of the most famous scenic spots, including "Nine Horses Fresco Hill" and "Yellow Cloth Shoal."

Gudong Waterfall Scenic Area: A Natural Escape

If you are looking for a refreshing outdoor adventure, Gudong Waterfall Scenic Area, located about 25 kilometers from Guilin, is an excellent choice. This scenic area features multiple cascading waterfalls, lush forests, and unique red sandstone formations.

Activities in Gudong Waterfall Scenic Area

  • Climbing the Waterfalls: Unlike most waterfall parks, visitors here can climb directly up the waterfalls using handrails and ropes.
  • Exploring the Ancient Forest, home to centuries-old trees and rare plant species.
  • Walking Across the Suspension Bridges, offering fantastic views of the surrounding nature.

Yao Mountain: The Highest Peak Near Guilin

Yao Mountain, located just 12 kilometers east of Guilin, is the highest mountain in the area, reaching an elevation of 909 meters. It offers some of the best panoramic views of Guilin’s famous karst landscape.

Best Ways to Experience Yao Mountain

  • Take the Cable Car: A scenic ride to the summit, passing through tea plantations and forests.
  • Hike to the Top: A more challenging but rewarding way to experience the mountain.
  • Visit the Mausoleum of Jingjiang Princes, a historic site at the base of the mountain.

Bajiaozhai National Park: The Lesser-Known Danxia Landform

For those looking to explore a unique geological wonder, Bajiaozhai National Park is an excellent choice. Located about 150 kilometers from Guilin, this park features spectacular Danxia landforms—steep, red sandstone cliffs similar to those in Zhangjiajie.

Top Highlights

  • Bajiaozhai Scenic Area, offering breathtaking views of the dramatic rock formations.
  • Hiking Trails, leading to incredible viewpoints and hidden temples.
  • Zhonglu Temple, a centuries-old Buddhist temple perched on the cliffs.

Since Bajiaozhai is less touristy than other sites, it offers a peaceful and immersive nature experience.

From ancient villages and dramatic rice terraces to stunning karst peaks and underground caves, the areas surrounding Guilin are filled with breathtaking natural and cultural wonders. Whether you are seeking adventure, history, or relaxation, these one-day trip destinations provide an unforgettable experience.
