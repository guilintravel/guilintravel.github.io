How to Make Restaurant Reservations in Guilin and Are English Menus Available?

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, renowned for its breathtaking landscapes and rich culinary heritage, attracts tourists from around the globe. Whether you're craving traditional Guilin noodles or an upscale dining experience with a view of the Li River, knowing how to navigate restaurant reservations and menu accessibility is crucial for a seamless dining experience. Here's a comprehensive guide to help you make the most of your culinary journey in Guilin.

Understanding the Reservation System

Do All Restaurants Require Reservations?

Not all restaurants in Guilin require reservations. For casual dining, street food stalls, or smaller local eateries, you can usually walk in without an issue. However, popular restaurants, fine dining establishments, and those located in tourist-heavy areas often recommend or require reservations, especially during peak hours or holidays.

Methods to Make Reservations

  1. Phone Calls:

    • The most common method is to call the restaurant directly. Many restaurants in Guilin have staff who understand basic English, but for more fluent communication, consider using a translation app.
    • Prepare to state your name, number of people, date, and time clearly.

  2. Online Platforms:

    • Apps like Dianping (China’s equivalent of Yelp) or Meituan offer reservation options for many restaurants. While these platforms are primarily in Chinese, translation apps or browser extensions can help you navigate them.
    • Some internationally recognized restaurants in Guilin may also be listed on global platforms like OpenTable.

  3. Hotel Concierge Services:

    • If you’re staying at a hotel, the concierge can assist you with reservations. This is particularly useful for high-end restaurants or if language barriers arise.

  4. Walk-Ins with Waiting Lists:

    • For restaurants that don't take reservations, they may operate a waiting list. Arriving early is recommended, especially during peak dining times.

Tips for Securing a Reservation

  • Book at least a day in advance for popular venues.
  • For large groups or special occasions, call several days or even weeks ahead.
  • Confirm your reservation on the day of your visit to ensure it hasn’t been misplaced or canceled.

Are English Menus Available?

Tourist-Friendly Restaurants

In tourist-heavy areas such as downtown Guilin, Yangshuo, and around the Li River, many restaurants provide English menus to cater to international visitors. These menus often include pictures, making it easier to identify dishes.

Local Eateries

Smaller, family-run establishments may not have English menus. However, the following strategies can help:

  • Translation Apps: Apps like Google Translate or Pleco allow you to scan and translate menus in real time.
  • Point-and-Choose: Many local restaurants have menus with pictures. Simply pointing at what looks appealing works well.
  • Learn Key Words: Familiarize yourself with a few Chinese words or phrases for popular dishes, such as “米粉” (mifen) for rice noodles.

Special Requests

If you have dietary restrictions or preferences, communicating them might be a challenge at some locations. Preparing translated phrases or asking your hotel to write down your requests in Chinese can be helpful.

Must-Try Restaurants in Guilin

Traditional Guilin Cuisine

  1. Guilin Rice Noodles (桂林米粉):

    • Found at nearly every corner, these rice noodles are a must-try. Check out local favorites like Ronggui Mifen or Wugujia for authentic flavors.
    • English menus are rare at small noodle shops, but the simplicity of the dish often makes ordering straightforward.

  2. Zhengyang Pedestrian Street:

    • This bustling area is packed with restaurants offering local delicacies. Most places cater to tourists, so English menus are often available.

Upscale Dining

  1. Li River Cuisine at Shangri-La Hotel:

    • Located by the Li River, this restaurant offers an exquisite dining experience with an English menu and staff who speak English fluently.
    • Advance reservations are highly recommended.

  2. Rice Noodles Hot Pot City:

    • A modern twist on traditional hot pot, this spot often attracts foodies. Call ahead to secure a table, and ask about menu translations if needed.

Scenic Dining Experiences

  1. Yangshuo’s West Street Cafés:

    • A short drive from Guilin, West Street in Yangshuo is lined with restaurants and cafés offering picturesque views of karst mountains. Many provide English menus and cater to international tastes.

  2. Yulong River Bamboo Raft Dining:

    • Combine dining with an unforgettable bamboo raft ride. Meals are typically pre-arranged through tour operators, so reservations are a must.

Dining Etiquette in Guilin

Table Manners

  • It’s customary to wait for everyone to be seated and for the host to start the meal before digging in.
  • Sharing dishes is the norm, so don’t expect individual portions.

Tipping Culture

  • Tipping is not a common practice in most parts of China, including Guilin. However, upscale restaurants and international hotels may include a service charge.

Payment Methods

  • Cash and mobile payments like WeChat Pay and Alipay are widely accepted. Credit cards are less commonly used, so ensure you have an alternative.

Seasonal Dining in Guilin

Peak Tourist Seasons

  • Spring and autumn are the busiest times, making reservations more crucial.
  • Some seasonal specialties, like fresh river fish, are best enjoyed during these months.

Off-Peak Periods

  • Visiting during the quieter winter months can mean easier access to popular restaurants and shorter waiting times.

Local Favorites You Shouldn't Miss

Beer Fish

  • A Yangshuo specialty, this dish combines fresh river fish with beer and spices for a flavorful experience. Many restaurants along the Li River offer this on their menus, often with English translations.

Osmanthus-Flavored Snacks

  • Guilin is famous for its osmanthus flowers, which are used in a variety of desserts and teas. Look for these at dessert shops or tea houses in the city center.

Stir-Fried Snail

  • For the adventurous eater, stir-fried river snails are a local delicacy. Head to specialty restaurants for this dish, where the menus may have English descriptions.

Final Tips for Dining in Guilin

  • Always double-check operating hours, as some smaller establishments may close during mid-afternoon.
  • If unsure about portion sizes, ask the staff or start with fewer dishes to avoid over-ordering.
  • Engage with the locals! Even if there's a language barrier, a smile and polite gestures go a long way in making your dining experience enjoyable.

Dining in Guilin is not just about the food; it’s about the experience of connecting with the city’s culture and people. With a bit of preparation and flexibility, you can savor everything this culinary destination has to offer.
