First-Timer’s Guide to Guilin’s Shopping Streets

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its iconic karst landscapes and serene Li River, is a dream destination for travelers. But beyond the postcard-perfect views, the city boasts vibrant shopping streets that offer a mix of traditional crafts, modern boutiques, and mouthwatering street food. Whether you’re hunting for souvenirs, sampling local snacks, or just soaking up the atmosphere, this guide will help you navigate Guilin’s best shopping spots like a pro.

The Must-Visit Shopping Streets in Guilin

Zhengyang Pedestrian Street (正阳步行街)

Located in the heart of Guilin, Zhengyang Pedestrian Street is the city’s most famous shopping hub. This bustling walkway is lined with shops selling everything from hand-painted fans to embroidered silk scarves.

  • What to Buy: Look for Guilin’s famous osmanthus tea, handicrafts, and ethnic minority textiles.
  • Food to Try: Don’t miss Guilin rice noodles (米粉, mǐfěn) from street vendors or stinky tofu (臭豆腐, chòu dòufu) for the brave.
  • Pro Tip: Bargaining is common here, so don’t hesitate to negotiate prices.

West Street (西街) in Yangshuo

While technically not in Guilin city, West Street in nearby Yangshuo is a must-visit for shoppers. This lively street blends Chinese and Western influences, with cafes, bars, and souvenir shops side by side.

  • What to Buy: Calligraphy scrolls, handmade jewelry, and ethnic Yao embroidery.
  • Food to Try: Beer fish (啤酒鱼, píjiǔ yú), a local specialty, is a must.
  • Pro Tip: Visit at night when the street transforms into a lively night market.

Shangshui Food Street (尚水美食街)

If you’re a foodie, Shangshui Food Street is your paradise. This narrow alley is packed with stalls serving Guilin’s best street food.

  • What to Eat: Oil tea (油茶, yóuchá), glutinous rice cakes, and barbecued skewers.
  • Pro Tip: Go with an empty stomach—there’s too much to sample in one visit!

Hidden Gems for Unique Finds

Guilin Night Market (桂林夜市)

For a more local experience, head to Guilin Night Market near Zhongshan Road. It’s less touristy but full of authentic goods.

  • What to Buy: Antique trinkets, handmade leather goods, and local spices.
  • Pro Tip: Arrive after 8 PM for the liveliest atmosphere.

Solitary Beauty Peak Cultural Street

Near Guilin’s Solitary Beauty Peak, this street specializes in traditional Chinese art.

  • What to Buy: Ink paintings, seal carvings, and bamboo crafts.
  • Pro Tip: Some artists offer custom pieces on the spot.

Shopping Etiquette & Tips

Bargaining Like a Local

  • Start at 50% of the asking price and meet somewhere in the middle.
  • Smile and be polite—it goes a long way!

Payment Methods

  • Cash is still king in smaller shops, but Alipay/WeChat Pay works in most places.
  • ATMs are available, but carrying some RMB is wise for street vendors.

Avoiding Tourist Traps

  • Be wary of overpriced jade or "antiques" sold near major attractions.
  • Stick to reputable shops if buying high-value items.

Seasonal Shopping Highlights

Osmanthus Festival (October)

Guilin’s famous osmanthus flowers bloom in October, and the city celebrates with markets selling osmanthus-scented products like tea, candy, and perfumes.

Lunar New Year Markets

If visiting in January/February, don’t miss the red lanterns, paper cuttings, and festive snacks sold in temporary markets.

Final Thoughts for First-Timers

Guilin’s shopping streets are as diverse as its landscapes. Whether you’re hunting for souvenirs, tasting local flavors, or just enjoying the vibe, these streets offer a slice of Guilin’s culture. Pack light, bring your bargaining skills, and get ready to explore!
