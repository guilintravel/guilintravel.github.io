Iconic Photography Locations in Guilin

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog

Guilin, famed for its karst landscapes, serene rivers, and picturesque countryside, is a paradise for photographers. From dramatic peaks to tranquil water reflections, Guilin offers a wealth of stunning locations that cater to both amateur and professional photographers. Here’s a guide to the most iconic photography spots in Guilin, each offering unique opportunities to capture its natural and cultural beauty.

The Li River (Lijiang)

The Li River is synonymous with Guilin, stretching over 80 kilometers from Guilin to Yangshuo. This breathtaking waterway is a must-visit for landscape photography enthusiasts.

  1. Xingping Town

    • Xingping offers one of the most iconic views of the Li River. The scene of karst peaks and the river at sunrise is a favorite among photographers.
    • Photography Tip: Arrive early to capture the golden hour. Consider taking a bamboo raft for unique angles of the river and surrounding peaks.

  2. Nine Horses Fresco Hill (Jiuma Hua Shan)

    • This location is famous for its cliff face, which resembles nine horses in motion. The patterns make for an interesting photographic subject.
    • Photography Tip: Use a telephoto lens to zoom in on the cliff’s details, especially during sunset when the light enhances the textures.

  3. Yangdi-Xingping Hiking Trail

    • For adventurous photographers, this trail provides multiple vantage points of the river and surrounding karst formations.
    • Photography Tip: Bring a wide-angle lens to capture the expanse of the scenery and a tripod for stability along the rocky paths.

Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiangbishan)

Elephant Trunk Hill, named for its resemblance to an elephant drinking water, is one of Guilin’s most recognizable landmarks.

  • Photography Tip: Shoot during the blue hour (just after sunset or before sunrise) to capture the reflection of the hill in the water, framed by soft lighting.
  • Best Perspective: Visit the park surrounding the hill to get close-up shots or use the bridge nearby for a wider view.

Longji Rice Terraces (Longsheng)

Located about 2.5 hours from Guilin, the Longji Rice Terraces are a marvel of agricultural engineering and a photographer's dream. The terraced fields transform throughout the year, offering unique scenes for each season.

  1. Ping’an Village

    • The terraces around Ping’an Village are among the most photographed. The patterns of the fields are particularly striking from higher viewpoints.
    • Photography Tip: Visit during the spring (flooding season) or autumn (harvest season) for the most vibrant landscapes.

  2. Dazhai Village

    • Known for its expansive and dramatic terraces, Dazhai offers fewer crowds and equally stunning views.
    • Photography Tip: Use a drone for aerial shots that showcase the symmetry and scale of the terraces.

Reed Flute Cave (Ludi Yan)

Reed Flute Cave is an underground wonder filled with colorful stalactites and stalagmites. The cave's lighting creates a surreal atmosphere perfect for creative photography.

  • Photography Tip: Use a tripod for long-exposure shots to capture the vibrant colors and intricate details of the formations.
  • Challenge: The low light can be tricky; bring a camera capable of performing well in high ISO settings.

Yulong River

The Yulong River, often referred to as the “Little Li River,” offers a quieter and equally photogenic alternative. The river is surrounded by bamboo groves and small villages.

  1. Dragon Bridge (Yulong Qiao)

    • This ancient stone bridge is a favorite spot for photographers due to its rustic charm and scenic backdrop.
    • Photography Tip: Arrive early to avoid crowds and capture the serene reflection of the bridge in the river.

  2. Rafting Scenes

    • Bamboo rafts with local ferrymen make for quintessential Guilin shots.
    • Photography Tip: Use a fast shutter speed to freeze motion or a slower shutter speed to create a smooth effect on the flowing water.

Moon Hill (Yueliang Shan)

Moon Hill, with its natural arch resembling a moon, is a prominent feature in Yangshuo. The hike to the top rewards photographers with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Photography Tip: Use a wide-angle lens to capture the scale of the arch and the view through it. Sunrise and sunset provide the best lighting conditions.
  • Hiking Caution: The trail can be steep, so wear appropriate footwear and pack light.

Cuiping Hill

Cuiping Hill, located in the countryside of Yangshuo, is an off-the-beaten-path location known for its layered karst peaks and picturesque villages.

  • Photography Tip: Visit at sunset for dramatic shadows and golden light over the hills.
  • Best Perspective: Use a drone to capture the rolling landscape from above.

Xianggong Hill

Xianggong Hill is one of the most popular spots for capturing sunrise views over the Li River. The hilltop provides a sweeping panorama of karst peaks shrouded in mist.

  • Photography Tip: Arrive at least an hour before sunrise to secure a good spot and set up your equipment. A wide-angle lens works best for capturing the vastness of the scenery.
  • Local Insight: Hire a guide or driver to reach the hill, as it’s somewhat remote.

Fuli Town

Fuli Town is known for its traditional fan-painting art and ancient architecture, offering both cultural and architectural photography opportunities.

  • Photography Tip: Capture artisans at work to create dynamic images, or explore the old streets for scenes of daily life.
  • Best Timing: Visit in the morning when the town is more active with local vendors and workshops.

Guilin's Countryside Villages

  1. Xingping Ancient Town

    • A blend of history and natural beauty, Xingping offers photogenic old streets, stone houses, and access to scenic riverside spots.
    • Photography Tip: Explore the alleys for candid street photography opportunities and capture the locals’ way of life.

  2. Huangyao Ancient Town

    • While technically outside Guilin, Huangyao is worth the trip for its well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.
    • Photography Tip: Visit during festivals to photograph traditional celebrations and local costumes.

Dusk and Night Photography in Guilin

  1. Two Rivers and Four Lakes

    • The illuminated pagodas, bridges, and pathways make this area ideal for night photography.
    • Photography Tip: Use a tripod for long-exposure shots to capture the reflections and lighting effects.

  2. Sun and Moon Twin Pagodas

    • These twin pagodas are lit at night, creating a mesmerizing scene over the water.
    • Photography Tip: Experiment with different angles to capture their reflection and the surrounding lights.

Guilin’s timeless beauty and diverse landscapes make it a haven for photographers. Whether you’re capturing misty peaks, tranquil rivers, or bustling villages, the region offers countless opportunities to create stunning visual stories. With careful planning and the right gear, your photographic journey in Guilin is sure to be unforgettable.
