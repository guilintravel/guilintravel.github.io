Guilin’s Best Markets: Shopping Travel Planner

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its breathtaking karst landscapes and serene Li River, is a dream destination for travelers. But beyond the postcard-perfect views, the city boasts vibrant markets that offer a glimpse into local life, unique souvenirs, and mouthwatering street food. Whether you’re hunting for handicrafts, spices, or antiques, this shopping travel planner will guide you through Guilin’s best markets.

1. Zhengyang Pedestrian Street: The Heart of Guilin’s Shopping Scene

Why Visit?

Zhengyang Pedestrian Street is Guilin’s most famous shopping hub, blending modern retail with traditional charm. Stretching from Zhongshan Road to the scenic Shanhu Lake, this bustling street is lined with shops, cafes, and street performers.

What to Buy

  • Hand-painted fans and calligraphy: Local artists sell exquisite artwork.
  • Silk products: Scarves, dresses, and quilts made from Guangxi silk.
  • Local snacks: Try Guilin mifen (rice noodles) or osmanthus cakes.

Pro Tip

Visit in the evening when the street comes alive with neon lights and food stalls. Bargaining is expected, so don’t hesitate to negotiate!

2. West Street (Xi Jie) in Yangshuo: A Shopper’s Paradise

Why Visit?

Just an hour from Guilin, Yangshuo’s West Street is a must-visit for travelers. This lively market street fuses Chinese culture with international influences, making it perfect for eclectic finds.

What to Buy

  • Ethnic minority crafts: Jewelry and embroidery from the Zhuang and Yao people.
  • Handmade soaps and essential oils: Local vendors sell natural skincare products.
  • Bamboo items: From chopsticks to woven baskets.

Pro Tip

Many shops accept foreign currencies, but carrying RMB is still recommended for smaller vendors.

3. Guilin Night Market: A Foodie’s Delight

Why Visit?

For an authentic taste of Guilin, head to the night market near Zhongshan Road. The aroma of sizzling skewers, steaming dumplings, and spicy noodles fills the air.

Must-Try Foods

  • Beer fish (Pijiu Yu): A Yangshuo specialty cooked with local beer.
  • Stuffed Li River snails (Tianluo): A spicy, flavorful snack.
  • Sweet glutinous rice cakes: Sticky rice treats with red bean paste.

Pro Tip

Go with an empty stomach and follow the longest queues—they usually lead to the best stalls!

4. Guilin Antique Market: Treasures from the Past

Why Visit?

Hidden near Seven Star Park, this market is a goldmine for collectors. From vintage coins to porcelain, you’ll find fascinating relics.

What to Look For

  • Old Chinese coins and jade pieces: Some vendors sell authentic Ming and Qing dynasty items.
  • Minority textiles: Intricately embroidered fabrics from local tribes.
  • Tea sets: Traditional Yixing clay teapots.

Pro Tip

Be cautious of counterfeits. If a deal seems too good to be true, it probably is.

5. Farmers’ Markets: Fresh and Local

Why Visit?

For a true local experience, visit Guilin’s morning farmers’ markets. The Qixing Market and Binjiang Market are packed with fresh produce, spices, and live seafood.

What to Buy

  • Dried chili and spices: Perfect for recreating Guilin flavors at home.
  • Fresh lychees and longans: Grown in Guangxi’s fertile soil.
  • Fermented tofu (Choudoufu): An acquired taste but a local favorite.

Pro Tip

Arrive early (before 9 AM) for the best selection.

6. Minority Markets: Cultural Immersion

Why Visit?

Guilin is surrounded by ethnic villages like Longji and Sanjiang, where minority groups sell handmade goods.

What to Buy

  • Silver jewelry: Miao and Dong artisans craft stunning pieces.
  • Indigo-dyed fabrics: Traditional Yao clothing.
  • Wood carvings: Intricate designs depicting folklore.

Pro Tip

Weekends are the best time to visit, as many villagers bring extra goods to sell.

Final Shopping Tips for Guilin

  • Cash is king: Smaller vendors may not accept cards or digital payments.
  • Bargain politely: Start at 50% of the asking price and meet in the middle.
  • Check export restrictions: Some antiques and wildlife products may be illegal to take home.

Guilin’s markets are more than just shopping spots—they’re cultural experiences. Whether you’re savoring street food, hunting for antiques, or admiring minority crafts, each market tells a story of this enchanting region.
