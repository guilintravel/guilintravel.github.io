The Best Guilin Travel Apps for Digital Nomads

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its breathtaking karst landscapes, serene Li River, and vibrant local culture, is a dream destination for digital nomads. Whether you're working remotely or exploring between Zoom calls, having the right apps can make your stay seamless. Here’s a curated list of the best Guilin travel apps to help you navigate, stay connected, and make the most of your time in this picturesque city.

Essential Navigation and Transportation Apps

1. Baidu Maps (百度地图)

While Google Maps is unreliable in China, Baidu Maps is the go-to navigation app. It offers detailed directions for public transport, walking, and cycling. The app also includes real-time traffic updates and offline maps—perfect for those day trips to Yangshuo or Longji Rice Terraces.

2. Didi Chuxing (滴滴出行)

China’s answer to Uber, Didi is a must-have for hassle-free rides. The app supports English and offers multiple ride options, from budget-friendly taxis to premium cars. Pro tip: Use the "English Assistant" feature if you’re not fluent in Mandarin.

3. AMap (高德地图)

Another great alternative to Google Maps, AMap provides accurate public transit routes and even suggests less crowded buses or metro lines. Its voice-guided navigation is a lifesaver when exploring Guilin’s winding streets.

Accommodation and Co-Working Spaces

4. Airbnb

For digital nomads seeking unique stays, Airbnb lists everything from riverside lofts in Yangshuo to traditional courtyard homes in Guilin’s old town. Many hosts offer long-term discounts, making it ideal for extended stays.

5. Xiaozhu (小猪短租)

This local platform offers affordable short-term rentals, often with better deals than international sites. Filter by Wi-Fi speed and workspace setups to find the perfect remote work spot.

6. Nomad List

Not just for accommodations, Nomad List helps you connect with other digital nomads in Guilin. Check reviews on Wi-Fi reliability, coworking spaces, and the best cafes to work from.

Food and Dining

7. Meituan (美团)

China’s super-app for food delivery, Meituan lets you order everything from Guilin rice noodles (米粉) to Western comfort food. The app also features discounts and user reviews to help you pick the best spots.

8. Ele.me (饿了么)

Another top food delivery app, Ele.me is great for late-night cravings. Many restaurants offer English menus, and the delivery tracking feature ensures your food arrives hot.

9. Dianping (大众点评)

Think of it as China’s Yelp. Dianping provides honest reviews, photos, and rankings for restaurants, cafes, and street food stalls. Use it to discover hidden gems like local breweries or riverside tea houses.

Language and Communication

10. Pleco

A must-have for non-Mandarin speakers, Pleco is a powerful dictionary app with OCR (camera translation) and flashcards. It’s invaluable for reading menus or street signs.

11. WeChat (微信)

More than just a messaging app, WeChat is essential for payments, booking tickets, and even ordering taxis. Many businesses in Guilin accept WeChat Pay, so linking your card is a smart move.

12. Google Translate

While VPN-dependent, Google Translate’s conversation mode and offline language packs can be a lifesaver when communicating with locals.

Exploring Guilin’s Attractions

13. Trip.com (携程)

Book train tickets, flights, and tours all in one place. Trip.com often has exclusive deals on Li River cruises and entrance tickets to popular spots like Reed Flute Cave.

14. Klook

For curated experiences, Klook offers discounted activities such as bamboo rafting, cooking classes, and guided hikes. Their e-tickets save you time at crowded attractions.

15. Guilin Travel Official App

This local government app provides updated info on festivals, weather alerts, and lesser-known scenic spots. It’s a goldmine for off-the-beaten-path adventures.

Staying Productive

16. ExpressVPN

A reliable VPN is non-negotiable for accessing Google, Slack, and other work tools. ExpressVPN consistently works well in China.

17. Trello or Notion

Stay organized with these productivity apps. Plan your work sprints around Guilin’s sunrise hikes or sunset river views.

18. Meetup

Find local events, language exchanges, or coworking meetups to network with fellow nomads and expats.

Health and Safety

19. Alipay (支付宝)

Beyond payments, Alipay’s "Mini Programs" include hospital registrations, pharmacy deliveries, and even bike-sharing.

20. China Air Quality Index (AQI)

Check real-time pollution levels before planning outdoor work sessions or hikes.

With these apps, your Guilin adventure as a digital nomad will be smooth, efficient, and unforgettable. Pack your laptop, grab your phone, and get ready to work from paradise!
