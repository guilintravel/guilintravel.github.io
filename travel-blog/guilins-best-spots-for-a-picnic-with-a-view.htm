Guilin’s Best Spots for a Picnic with a View

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

There’s something almost magical about enjoying a meal outdoors with Guilin’s dramatic karst landscape as your backdrop. While many travelers come to cruise the Li River or explore caves, one of the most intimate and memorable ways to connect with this region’s natural beauty is by having a picnic. Picture this: you’re sitting on a soft bamboo mat, unpacking a basket of local snacks, with emerald-green peaks rising around you and the gentle sound of the river flowing nearby. This isn’t just a meal; it’s an experience.

Guilin, located in Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, is famous for its surreal scenery that has inspired poets and painters for centuries. While popular activities like biking through Yangshuo or taking a Li River boat tour are must-dos, picnicking allows you to slow down and absorb the views at your own pace. It’s a trend that’s gaining traction among both domestic tourists and international travelers looking for quieter, more sustainable ways to enjoy nature.

In this guide, we’ll explore some of the most picturesque and accessible spots in and around Guilin perfect for a scenic picnic. We’ll also touch on what to pack, local delicacies to try, and how to picnic responsibly in this fragile ecosystem.

Why Picnic in Guilin?

Guilin’s unique topography—towering limestone karsts, serene rivers, and lush countryside—makes it an ideal destination for outdoor dining. Unlike crowded tourist sites, a picnic offers solitude and a personal connection with the environment. It’s also a budget-friendly option that aligns with slower, more mindful travel.

The Rise of Eco-Tourism and Picnicking

Recently, picnicking has become a hot topic in travel circles, especially as more people seek eco-friendly activities. In Guilin, local authorities have promoted sustainable tourism practices to protect the natural environment. Picnicking, when done responsibly (leaving no trace, using reusable containers), minimizes impact while maximizing enjoyment. Social media platforms like Xiaohongshu (Little Red Book) are filled with posts of influencers sharing their “aesthetic picnics” against Guilin’s stunning vistas, driving interest among younger travelers.

Top Picnic Spots with Breathtaking Views

1. Li River Banks Near Yangshuo

The Li River is the heart of Guilin’s scenery, and finding a quiet spot along its banks is easier than you might think. Just a short bike ride from Yangshuo town, you’ll discover peaceful areas where the river curves gently, and karst peaks reflect on the water’s surface. One favorite location is near the Yulong River confluence, where you can set up on a small beach or grassy patch. Early morning or late afternoon offers the best light for photos and cooler temperatures.

What to pack: Grab some guilin mifen (rice noodles) from a local vendor, along with fresh fruit like persimmons or lychees. Don’t forget a thermos of tea!

2. Elephant Trunk Hill Park

While Elephant Trunk Hill itself is a tourist hotspot, the surrounding park has secluded areas perfect for a picnic. Find a bench or spread a blanket under a banyan tree with a view of the hill’s iconic elephant-shaped silhouette. It’s especially romantic at sunset when the crowds thin out.

Insider tip: Visit on weekdays to avoid weekend crowds. The park entrance fee is worth it for the well-maintained grounds and facilities.

3. Longji Rice Terraces

For a picnic with elevation, head to the Longji Rice Terraces in Longsheng County, about two hours from Guilin. The terraces, carved into mountainsides, create a mesmerizing pattern of curves and lines. Hike to a viewpoint like “Nine Dragons and Five Tigers” and enjoy your meal with a panoramic vista. Seasons change the view—lush green in spring, golden in autumn—making it a year-round destination.

What to pack: Bring hearty local snacks such as sticky rice with bamboo steam or zongzi (glutinous rice dumplings). These are easy to carry and energizing for hiking.

4. Fubo Hill

Fubo Hill, located in downtown Guilin, offers a quick escape with minimal effort. A short hike leads to hilltop pavilions with sweeping views of the city and river. It’s a great spot for a sunrise or midday picnic. The hill is less crowded than some other attractions, giving you a sense of privacy.

5. Moon Hill in Yangshuo

Moon Hill, named for its natural moon-shaped arch, is another fantastic picnic destination. After a moderate hike to the viewing platform, reward yourself with a snack while gazing down at the countryside. It’s a popular spot, so go early to secure a quiet moment.

What to Pack for Your Guilin Picnic

A successful picnic is all about preparation. Here’s a checklist to ensure you have everything you need:

  • Local Delicacies: Instead of packing from home, support local vendors. Try osmanthus cake, a Guilin specialty, or beer fish (a Yangshuo dish) packed in a container. Fresh tropical fruits are abundant and refreshing.
  • Reusable Utensils: Help reduce waste by bringing reusable cutlery, cups, and containers. Many shops in Yangshuo sell bamboo sets perfect for picnics.
  • Comfort Items: A lightweight blanket or mat, wet wipes, and a small trash bag for cleaning up.
  • Hydration: Carry plenty of water, especially if hiking. Local teas or sugarcane juice are also great options.
  • Camera or Smartphone: You’ll want to capture the views! Guilin’s scenery is incredibly photogenic.

Incorporating Local Culture into Your Picnic

To make your picnic truly authentic, consider incorporating elements of local Zhuang or Yao culture. For example, you might listen to traditional folk music on your phone or try your hand at using bamboo chopsticks. In Longsheng, you can even buy hand-woven textiles from minority villages to use as a picnic blanket.

Timing Your Picnic for the Best Experience

Guilin’s climate is subtropical, with hot, humid summers and mild winters. The best times for picnicking are: - Spring (March to May): Mild temperatures and blooming flowers. - Autumn (September to November): Clear skies and comfortable weather. Avoid summer midday heat and sudden rain showers by planning picnics in early morning or late afternoon.

Responsible Picnicking: Leave No Trace

As picnicking grows in popularity, it’s crucial to practice sustainability. Always carry out all trash, avoid disturbing wildlife, and stay on designated paths. Guilin’s ecosystem is delicate—let’s keep it pristine for future visitors.

Beyond the Picnic: Other Hot Activities

While in Guilin, combine your picnic with other trending activities: - Bamboo Rafting: Drift down the Yulong River for a different perspective. - Photography Tours: Join a local guide for sunrise photo sessions at iconic spots. - Farm Stays: Experience rural life by staying in a guesthouse near the terraces.

Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, a picnic in Guilin offers a unique way to engage with one of China’s most beautiful landscapes. So pack your basket, pick a spot, and dine with a view that’s nothing short of poetic.
