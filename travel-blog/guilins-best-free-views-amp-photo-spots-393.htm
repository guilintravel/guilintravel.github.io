Guilin’s Best Free Views & Photo Spots

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin’s landscape is the stuff of postcards—towering karst peaks, winding rivers, and lush greenery that seem almost too perfect to be real. The best part? You don’t need to splurge on expensive tours or entrance fees to experience its beauty. From hidden viewpoints to iconic spots, here’s your guide to Guilin’s most breathtaking (and free!) photo opportunities.

The Must-See Free Views in Guilin

Xianggong Mountain (Xiānggōng Shān)

For the ultimate panoramic shot of the Li River, hike up Xianggong Mountain at sunrise. The 20-minute climb rewards you with a 360-degree view of the river snaking through emerald peaks. Pro tip: Arrive before dawn to secure a spot—this viewpoint gets crowded fast!

Fubo Hill (Fúbō Shān)

Skip the paid attractions inside Fubo Hill and head straight to its free outer trails. The hill offers stunning vistas of the city skyline framed by karst formations. Don’t miss the hidden cave near the base, where locals practice tai chi at sunrise.

Diecai Hill (Diécǎi Shān) Overlook

While Diecai Hill charges for its pavilions, the surrounding paths are free and just as scenic. Wander along the foothills for unobstructed views of Guilin’s iconic peaks. Sunset here casts a golden glow over the city—perfect for moody travel shots.

Hidden Gems for Unique Photos

Yulong River Bamboo Bridges

Escape the tourist crowds of the Li River and head to the Yulong River’s rustic bamboo bridges. These handwoven structures make for dreamy, off-the-beaten-path photos, especially when farmers lead water buffalo across at dusk.

Moon Hill’s Free Viewing Angle

Most visitors pay to climb Moon Hill, but you can capture its famous crescent-shaped arch from the road below. Park near the village of Gaotian and shoot from the rice fields for a pastoral twist.

Guilin’s Secret Tea Terraces

Ask locals for directions to the unmarked tea plantations near Longsheng. While the famous Longji Terraces charge admission, these smaller plots offer equally mesmerizing layered landscapes—without the ticket booth.

Urban Photography Hotspots

Two Rivers & Four Lakes Night Walk

Guilin’s city center transforms into a glittering wonderland after dark. Stroll along the illuminated榕湖 (Róng Hú) and杉湖 (Shān Hú) lakes for reflections of pagodas and bridges. The golden Twin Pagodas are particularly photogenic.

Zhengyang Pedestrian Street

This bustling walkway blends old-world charm with modern energy. Capture candid shots of street food vendors, calligraphers painting with water on pavement, and traditional qipao-clad influencers posing against Ming-style architecture.

Liberation Bridge Sunset

For an iconic cityscape, position yourself on Liberation Bridge as the sun dips behind Elephant Trunk Hill. The juxtaposition of vintage boats and neon city lights creates a timeless Guilin moment.

Pro Tips for Instagram-Worthy Shots

  • Fog is your friend: Visit in autumn or early morning when mist clings to the peaks for an ethereal effect.
  • Borrow a local prop: Politely ask fishermen if you can photograph their cormorant birds—it’s a classic Guilin scene.
  • Go vertical: Use portrait mode to emphasize the towering karst formations against tiny boats or bicycles.

Pack light, charge your camera, and let Guilin’s free landscapes do the rest. Every corner here feels like a painting—no filter needed.
