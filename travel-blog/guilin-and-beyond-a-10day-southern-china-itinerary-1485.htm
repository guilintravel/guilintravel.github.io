Guilin and Beyond: A 10-Day Southern China Itinerary

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Southern China is a treasure trove of natural wonders, ancient towns, and vibrant culture. This 10-day itinerary takes you through the best of Guilin, Yangshuo, and beyond, offering a mix of adventure, relaxation, and unforgettable experiences.

Day 1-3: Exploring Guilin’s Iconic Landscapes

Arrival in Guilin

Start your journey in Guilin, a city famed for its karst mountains and winding rivers. After checking into your hotel, take a leisurely stroll along the Li River or visit Elephant Trunk Hill, the city’s most recognizable landmark.

Cruise the Li River to Yangshuo

On Day 2, embark on a scenic Li River cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo. The four-hour journey passes through breathtaking landscapes of limestone peaks, bamboo groves, and rural villages. Keep your camera ready for Nine Horses Fresco Hill and Xingping Ancient Town, the backdrop of the 20 RMB note.

Yangshuo’s Countryside Adventures

Spend Day 3 exploring Yangshuo’s countryside. Rent a bike and ride through Yulong River Valley, stopping at hidden villages and rice paddies. For thrill-seekers, try rock climbing on the karst cliffs or take a bamboo raft down the Yulong River.

Day 4-5: Longji Rice Terraces and Minority Culture

Hike the Dragon’s Backbone Terraces

A two-hour drive from Guilin brings you to the Longji Rice Terraces, also known as the Dragon’s Backbone. These cascading terraces, built over 600 years ago, are a marvel of agricultural engineering. Hike to Ping’an Village or Dazhai Village for panoramic views.

Experience Zhuang and Yao Culture

Stay overnight in a traditional wooden guesthouse and immerse yourself in the culture of the Zhuang and Yao ethnic minorities. Enjoy a local meal of bamboo rice and try on colorful hand-embroidered clothing.

Day 6-7: Detour to Huangyao Ancient Town

Step Back in Time

Leave the crowds behind and head to Huangyao Ancient Town, a well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty village. Wander through narrow cobblestone streets, cross ancient stone bridges, and visit ancestral halls.

Local Flavors and Crafts

Sample Huangyao’s specialty dishes, like fermented tofu and river fish. Don’t miss the chance to watch artisans create handmade oil-paper umbrellas, a craft passed down for generations.

Day 8-10: The Hidden Gems of Southern Guangxi

Detian Waterfall: China’s Most Beautiful Falls

Venture further south to Detian Waterfall, the largest transnational waterfall in Asia. Straddling the China-Vietnam border, the falls are at their most powerful between May and October. Take a bamboo raft for an up-close view.

Exploring the Border Town of Pingxiang

End your trip in Pingxiang, a lively border town with a mix of Chinese and Vietnamese influences. Visit the Friendship Pass, a historic gateway between the two countries, and browse the bustling Dongxing Market for souvenirs like Vietnamese coffee and tropical fruits.

Travel Tips for Southern China

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter is mild but less vibrant.

Getting Around

Trains and buses connect major cities, but renting a car or hiring a private driver is ideal for remote areas like Longji and Detian.

Must-Try Foods

  • Guilin rice noodles (mifen)
  • Beer fish (Yangshuo’s signature dish)
  • Bamboo rice (Longji specialty)

This 10-day journey through southern China blends natural beauty, cultural immersion, and off-the-beaten-path discoveries. Whether you’re cruising the Li River or hiking terraced fields, every moment promises to be unforgettable.
