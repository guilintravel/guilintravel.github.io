Guilin Travel Itinerary: 3 Days of Adventure

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a jewel in China’s Guangxi region, is a dream destination for travelers seeking breathtaking landscapes, cultural richness, and outdoor adventures. With its iconic karst mountains, winding rivers, and vibrant local culture, Guilin offers an unforgettable escape. This 3-day itinerary is packed with must-see spots, hidden gems, and thrilling activities to make the most of your trip.

Day 1: Exploring Guilin’s Natural Wonders

Morning: Li River Cruise to Yangshuo

Start your adventure with a scenic Li River cruise, the crown jewel of Guilin’s attractions. The 4-hour journey from Guilin to Yangshuo takes you past towering limestone peaks, lush bamboo groves, and traditional fishing villages. Keep your camera ready for the famous Nine Horses Fresco Hill and Xingping Town, the backdrop of the 20 RMB note.

Pro tip: Book a bamboo raft tour for a more intimate experience on the Yulong River, a quieter alternative to the Li River.

Afternoon: Yangshuo Countryside Cycling

Once in Yangshuo, rent a bike and explore the countryside. The flat terrain makes it easy to pedal through rice paddies, orchards, and quaint villages. Stop by Moon Hill, a natural arch with a stunning viewpoint, or visit Butterfly Spring Park for a mix of nature and folklore.

Don’t miss West Street (Xi Jie), Yangshuo’s bustling pedestrian street lined with cafes, souvenir shops, and street performers. Grab a coffee or try local snacks like beer fish (píjiǔ yú) and stuffed snails (tián luó).

Evening: Impression Liu Sanjie Show

Cap off the day with the Impression Liu Sanjie, a mesmerizing outdoor light and music show directed by Zhang Yimou. Set against the Li River, this performance showcases local Zhuang minority culture with hundreds of actors in colorful costumes.

Day 2: Adventure and Culture in Guilin

Morning: Reed Flute Cave and Elephant Trunk Hill

Head back to Guilin and explore the Reed Flute Cave (Lúdí Yán), a surreal underground world of stalactites and stalagmites illuminated by colorful lights. The cave’s name comes from the reeds growing outside, which were once used to make flutes.

Next, visit Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiàngbí Shān), Guilin’s iconic landmark. The hill resembles an elephant drinking from the Li River and offers a short hike with panoramic views.

Afternoon: Seven Star Park and Tea Appreciation

Spend the afternoon at Seven Star Park (Qīxīng Gōngyuán), Guilin’s largest green space. Highlights include the Seven Star Cave, Flower Bridge, and the Guilin Zoo, home to rare pandas.

For a cultural break, join a tea appreciation session at a local teahouse. Sample osmanthus tea (guìhuā chá), a Guilin specialty, and learn about Chinese tea rituals.

Evening: Night Cruise on Two Rivers and Four Lakes

As the sun sets, embark on a night cruise along Guilin’s Two Rivers and Four Lakes. The illuminated pagodas, bridges, and karst scenery create a magical atmosphere. The Sun and Moon Twin Pagodas (Rìyuè Shuāng Tǎ) are particularly stunning when lit up.

Day 3: Longji Rice Terraces and Minority Culture

Morning: Longji Rice Terraces Hike

Venture 2.5 hours from Guilin to the Longji Rice Terraces (Lóngjǐ Tītián), also known as the Dragon’s Backbone. These cascading terraces, built by the Zhuang and Yao minorities, are a marvel of agricultural engineering.

Hike through Ping’an Village or Dazhai Village, where you’ll encounter traditional wooden stilt houses and locals in ethnic attire. The Nine Dragons and Five Tigers and Seven Stars Around the Moon viewpoints offer jaw-dropping vistas.

Afternoon: Cultural Immersion in Huangluo Yao Village

On your way back, stop at Huangluo Yao Village, home to the Red Yao women known for their long hair, which can reach over 6 feet! Learn about their customs, try on traditional clothing, and enjoy a bamboo-tube rice (zhútǒng fàn) lunch.

Evening: Farewell Dinner at Guilin Night Market

Return to Guilin and celebrate your trip with a feast at Zhengyang Pedestrian Street or Dongxi Alley. Sample Guilin’s famous rice noodles (mǐfěn), stinky tofu (chòu dòufu), and spicy snails (là luó). Pick up souvenirs like hand-painted fans or osmanthus-scented candies before you go.

Bonus Tips for Your Guilin Adventure

  • Best time to visit: Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) for mild weather and clear skies.
  • Transport: Use public buses, taxis, or bike rentals. For Longji, consider a guided tour.
  • Packing list: Comfortable hiking shoes, rain gear, and a reusable water bottle.
  • Local etiquette: Always ask before photographing locals, especially in minority villages.

Guilin’s blend of natural beauty, adrenaline-pumping activities, and rich culture makes it a top-tier destination. Whether you’re cruising the Li River, biking through Yangshuo, or hiking the Longji Terraces, every moment here is postcard-perfect.
