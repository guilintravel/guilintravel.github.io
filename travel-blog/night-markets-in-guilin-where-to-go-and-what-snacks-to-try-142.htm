Night Markets in Guilin: Where to Go and What Snacks to Try

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a picturesque city in Guangxi Province, is not only known for its stunning karst landscapes but also for its vibrant night markets. These lively venues offer an unforgettable experience where you can immerse yourself in local culture, try delicious street food, and find unique souvenirs. Below is a guide to some of Guilin’s most popular night markets and their must-try snacks.

Zhengyang Pedestrian Street Night Market

Highlights

Zhengyang Pedestrian Street is one of the most famous shopping and dining areas in Guilin. At night, the street transforms into a bustling night market filled with food stalls and vendors selling local crafts.

  • Atmosphere: The lively vibe here makes it a favorite for both tourists and locals. Bright lights, music, and the aroma of street food create an inviting atmosphere.
  • Shopping: Alongside the food, you'll find handicrafts, jewelry, and local specialties like osmanthus cakes and chili sauce.

Must-Try Snacks

  1. Guilin Rice Noodles (桂林米粉): A staple of the local cuisine, these noodles are served with a tangy broth, pickled vegetables, and crispy peanuts.
  2. Luo Si Fen (螺蛳粉): This spicy and flavorful snail noodle dish is a hit with adventurous eaters.
  3. Grilled Oysters: Fresh oysters topped with garlic, chili, and scallions are grilled to perfection.
  4. Sweet Osmanthus Rice Cake: Made with glutinous rice and infused with the scent of osmanthus flowers, this dessert is a local delicacy.

Xicheng Pedestrian Street Night Market

Highlights

Xicheng Pedestrian Street is another popular destination for night market enthusiasts. Located in the downtown area, it offers a blend of traditional and modern food stalls, along with small shops selling souvenirs and clothing.

  • Atmosphere: More relaxed than Zhengyang, making it ideal for families or those looking for a quieter experience.
  • Entertainment: Street performers and musicians often entertain crowds, adding to the charm of the area.

Must-Try Snacks

  1. Skewers (串串): Choose from an array of meat, seafood, and vegetables skewered and grilled over open flames. Don’t forget to dip them in spicy sauce.
  2. Stinky Tofu: This pungent yet delicious snack is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, served with a spicy and sweet sauce.
  3. Fried Dumplings: These crispy dumplings are filled with pork, chives, and sometimes shrimp, making them a crowd favorite.
  4. Bubble Tea: A refreshing drink to pair with your snacks, with various flavors and toppings like tapioca pearls and jelly.

Two Rivers and Four Lakes Scenic Area Night Market

Highlights

Located near the iconic water system of Guilin, this night market offers a scenic dining experience. Enjoy street food while walking along the illuminated lakes and rivers, making it a romantic and picturesque outing.

  • Atmosphere: Perfect for couples or those looking for a relaxing evening.
  • Location: Close to landmarks like the Sun and Moon Pagodas, providing stunning views during your visit.

Must-Try Snacks

  1. Grilled Fish (烤鱼): Freshly caught fish marinated with spices and herbs, grilled to a smoky flavor.
  2. Sticky Rice Dumplings (糍粑): These chewy rice balls are often filled with sweet red bean paste and dusted with sesame seeds.
  3. Fried Rice with Eggs: A simple yet satisfying dish cooked with aromatic garlic and scallions.
  4. Fried Bananas: Crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, these make for a delightful dessert.

East and West Alleys Night Market

Highlights

The East and West Alleys area combines historical charm with modern flair. The night market here offers not just food but also a cultural glimpse into Guilin’s history.

  • Atmosphere: A mix of traditional architecture and contemporary nightlife, perfect for those who love photography.
  • Cultural Appeal: Alongside food, the market offers handicrafts, calligraphy, and other cultural souvenirs.

Must-Try Snacks

  1. Hot Pot Skewers (麻辣烫): A variety of skewered ingredients cooked in a boiling, spicy broth.
  2. Egg Waffles: Originally a Hong Kong delicacy, these crispy waffles are filled with custard or chocolate.
  3. Glutinous Rice Rolls: Filled with savory pork or sweet red bean paste, these rolls are a versatile snack.
  4. Local Fruit Juices: Freshly squeezed juices made from seasonal fruits like mango, dragon fruit, or lychee.

Guilin Zhongshan Road Night Market

Highlights

This market is one of the largest in Guilin and stretches for several blocks along Zhongshan Road. It’s a paradise for food lovers, with countless stalls offering a wide variety of snacks.

  • Atmosphere: Busy and energetic, with a wide range of options to suit all tastes.
  • Variety: From local specialties to international flavors, this market has something for everyone.

Must-Try Snacks

  1. Steamed Rice Cakes (蒸米糕): Soft and fluffy rice cakes served with coconut or osmanthus syrup.
  2. Barbecue Lamb: Tender lamb skewers seasoned with cumin and chili powder.
  3. Guilin Mung Bean Jelly (绿豆粉): A refreshing cold dish made from mung bean starch, served with a tangy sauce.
  4. Peanut Brittle: A sweet and crunchy treat made with caramelized sugar and roasted peanuts.

Tips for Visiting Guilin’s Night Markets

  1. Timing: Most night markets open around 6 PM and continue until late at night. Arrive early to avoid crowds.
  2. Cash or Mobile Payments: While many vendors accept mobile payments like WeChat Pay or Alipay, some may only accept cash.
  3. Try Local Specialties: Focus on snacks unique to Guilin for an authentic culinary experience.
  4. Bargain for Souvenirs: Vendors often allow bargaining for non-food items like crafts and accessories.
  5. Check Hygiene Standards: Choose stalls with a steady flow of customers, as this often indicates fresh and high-quality food.

Guilin’s night markets are more than just places to eat—they are cultural experiences that allow you to connect with the city’s vibrant atmosphere. Whether you’re savoring a bowl of Guilin rice noodles or exploring local crafts, these markets offer a glimpse into the heart and soul of Guilin.
