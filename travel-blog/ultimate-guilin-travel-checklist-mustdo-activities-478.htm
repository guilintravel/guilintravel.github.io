Ultimate Guilin Travel Checklist: Must-Do Activities

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, often referred to as China’s "landscape wonderland," is a dream destination for travelers seeking breathtaking natural beauty, cultural richness, and unforgettable adventures. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned explorer, this ultimate Guilin travel checklist ensures you won’t miss any of the must-do activities. From cruising the Li River to exploring hidden caves, here’s everything you need to experience in Guilin.

1. Cruise the Li River

No trip to Guilin is complete without a Li River cruise. This iconic journey takes you through some of the most stunning karst landscapes in the world.

Highlights of the Li River Cruise

  • Yangshuo to Xingping: The most scenic stretch, featuring the famous "Nine Horses Fresco Hill" and the backdrop of the 20 RMB note.
  • Bamboo Rafting: For a more intimate experience, opt for a traditional bamboo raft ride along the Yulong River.
  • Photography Spots: Sunrise and sunset offer magical lighting for capturing the misty peaks.

Pro Tip: Book your cruise in advance during peak seasons (April–October) to avoid sold-out tickets.

2. Explore Yangshuo’s Countryside

Yangshuo is the heart of Guilin’s outdoor adventures and rural charm. Rent a bike or an e-bike to discover its hidden gems.

Top Activities in Yangshuo

  • Moon Hill: Hike up to the natural arch for panoramic views.
  • West Street: A bustling hub for souvenirs, cafes, and nightlife.
  • Impression Sanjie Liu: A mesmerizing outdoor light show directed by Zhang Yimou, set against the Li River.

3. Visit the Longji Rice Terraces

A few hours from Guilin, the Longji (Dragon’s Backbone) Rice Terraces are a marvel of agricultural engineering.

Best Times to Visit

  • Spring: Water-filled terraces create mirror-like reflections.
  • Autumn: Golden harvest season offers stunning contrasts.

Must-See Villages

  • Ping’an Village: The most accessible and tourist-friendly.
  • Dazhai Village: Less crowded, with breathtaking viewpoints like "Seven Stars Around the Moon."

4. Discover Reed Flute Cave

Step into a surreal underground world at Reed Flute Cave, known for its illuminated stalactites and stalagmites.

What to Expect

  • Colorful Light Displays: The cave’s formations are lit with vibrant hues.
  • Ancient Inscriptions: Some carvings date back to the Tang Dynasty.

5. Hike Guilin’s Iconic Peaks

For the best aerial views, conquer these famous peaks:

Fubo Hill

  • Legendary Rock Carvings: Over 200 Buddhist statues and inscriptions.
  • View of the City: A short hike rewards you with a 360-degree panorama.

Elephant Trunk Hill

  • Guilin’s Symbol: The hill resembles an elephant drinking from the river.
  • Night Illumination: The area lights up beautifully after dark.

6. Savor Guilin’s Local Cuisine

Guilin’s food scene is as vibrant as its landscapes. Don’t miss these dishes:

Must-Try Foods

  • Guilin Rice Noodles (Mifen): A breakfast staple with savory broth and toppings.
  • Beer Fish (Pijiu Yu): A Yangshuo specialty cooked with local beer.
  • Stuffed Li River Snails: A unique delicacy for adventurous eaters.

7. Experience Local Culture

Immerse yourself in Guilin’s traditions with these activities:

Tea Tasting

  • Visit a tea plantation to sample Guilin’s famous osmanthus tea.

Minority Village Tours

  • Learn about the customs of the Zhuang and Yao ethnic groups in nearby villages.

8. Take a Night Cruise on Two Rivers and Four Lakes

For a romantic or relaxing evening, glide through Guilin’s illuminated waterways.

Highlights

  • Sun and Moon Pagodas: Lit up at night, these twin pagodas are a photographer’s dream.
  • Bridges and Gardens: Each bridge has a unique design and history.

9. Adventure Activities for Thrill-Seekers

Guilin isn’t just about serene landscapes—it’s also an adventure playground.

Rock Climbing

  • Yangshuo is a global hotspot for limestone cliff climbing.

Hot Air Ballooning

  • Float above the karst peaks for an unforgettable perspective.

10. Shop for Unique Souvenirs

Bring home a piece of Guilin with these local finds:

  • Hand-Painted Fans: Traditional artwork featuring Guilin’s scenery.
  • Scented Sachets: Filled with osmanthus flowers, a local favorite.
  • Minority Embroidery: Intricate designs from Zhuang and Yao artisans.

Final Tips for Your Guilin Trip

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March–May) and autumn (September–November) offer mild weather.
  • Transportation: Buses and bikes are convenient for getting around; taxis are affordable.
  • Language: Learn a few basic Mandarin phrases or use a translation app for smoother interactions.

With this checklist in hand, you’re ready to experience the best of Guilin. Pack your bags, charge your camera, and get ready for an adventure in one of China’s most enchanting destinations!
