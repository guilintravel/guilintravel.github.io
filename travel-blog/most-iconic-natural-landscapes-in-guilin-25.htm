Most Iconic Natural Landscapes in Guilin

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog

Guilin, located in southern China, is renowned for its breathtaking natural landscapes, which have inspired poets, artists, and travelers for centuries. The region is famous for its dramatic karst mountains, winding rivers, and lush greenery, making it one of the most visually stunning places in the world. Whether you're looking to explore towering peaks, serene rivers, or picturesque caves, Guilin offers a wide variety of natural wonders that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. In this article, we’ll take a closer look at the most iconic natural landscapes in Guilin that capture the essence of this extraordinary region.

1. Li River (Li Jiang) – The Heart of Guilin’s Natural Beauty

Perhaps the most iconic landscape in Guilin is the Li River, which flows through the city and winds its way toward Yangshuo, a town famous for its dramatic karst peaks and rural charm. The Li River is best known for its picturesque views of limestone peaks, traditional bamboo rafts, and idyllic villages nestled along its shores. The river’s serene waters, combined with the surrounding karst mountains, make it a paradise for photographers and nature lovers alike.

Cruising the Li River

A cruise along the Li River is considered one of the best ways to experience the natural beauty of the region. The 83-kilometer journey from Guilin to Yangshuo is particularly popular, offering breathtaking views of dramatic peaks rising sharply from the water. The landscape changes along the way, with each bend of the river revealing new vistas of mist-shrouded mountains, farmland, and small villages. This part of the river is often compared to Chinese ink-wash paintings, and it’s easy to see why—its beauty is otherworldly.

Karst Peaks and Traditional Rafts

One of the most striking features of the Li River is its karst peaks, which are steep, pointed limestone formations that rise dramatically from the flat surrounding landscape. These peaks are often shrouded in mist, creating an ethereal and timeless atmosphere. Along the river, you’ll see traditional bamboo rafts being used by local fishermen, providing an authentic glimpse of life along the waterway.

2. Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiangbi Shan) – A Natural Icon of Guilin

Elephant Trunk Hill is another of Guilin’s most famous natural landmarks. Located at the confluence of the Li River and the Taohua River, this hill gets its name from its resemblance to an elephant drinking water. The hill is one of the symbols of Guilin and is a must-visit for anyone exploring the city.

The Elephant Trunk Feature

The most distinctive feature of Elephant Trunk Hill is the large rock formation that looks like an elephant’s trunk reaching into the river. The hill stands 55 meters high and features a natural arch, through which the Li River flows. This striking formation is often photographed, as it provides one of the best views of the river and the surrounding landscape.

Exploring the Hill

Visitors to Elephant Trunk Hill can hike to the top of the hill for panoramic views of the Li River and the city of Guilin. The hill is also home to several temples and pavilions, which add to its historical and cultural significance. As you walk along the paths, you’ll encounter lush vegetation, steep cliffs, and scenic overlooks that showcase the unique beauty of this natural wonder.

3. Reed Flute Cave (Ludi Yan) – An Underground Wonderland

Located a short distance from the city center, Reed Flute Cave is one of the most famous karst caves in Guilin. This natural limestone cave is a wonderland of stalactites, stalagmites, and underground rivers. The cave has been a popular tourist attraction for centuries and is known for its stunning mineral formations, many of which have been sculpted over millions of years.

Exploring the Cave

The cave stretches for over 500 meters and is filled with colorful stalactites and stalagmites that are illuminated by a special lighting system. The formations inside the cave take on various shapes, from delicate curtains of rock to massive pillars. As you walk through the cave, you’ll encounter intricate rock formations resembling animals, plants, and mythical creatures, which is part of the cave's appeal.

The "Art Gallery of Nature"

Reed Flute Cave is often referred to as the “Art Gallery of Nature” due to the vibrant colors and intricate shapes of its mineral formations. The cave is not only a geological wonder but also a photographer’s dream, as the colorful lights enhance the natural beauty of the cave’s interior. It’s an experience that combines both natural history and artistic beauty.

4. Longji Rice Terraces (Dragon's Backbone Rice Terraces) – A Masterpiece of Human-Nature Harmony

About two hours from Guilin, the Longji Rice Terraces (Dragon’s Backbone Rice Terraces) are another of Guilin’s natural marvels. These ancient rice terraces, carved into the mountainside over 600 years ago, offer some of the most stunning views in China. The terraces stretch across the hillsides in neat, sweeping rows, creating a mesmerizing landscape that changes throughout the year.

The Terraces in Different Seasons

The rice terraces are particularly beautiful during different seasons. In spring, the fields are flooded with water, creating a mirrored effect that reflects the surrounding mountains and skies. During the summer months, the terraces turn a vibrant green as the rice plants grow. In autumn, the fields turn golden during harvest season, and in winter, the terraces are often covered in a blanket of mist or even snow, creating a more ethereal, mystical atmosphere.

Visiting Longji

The Longji Rice Terraces are located in the Longsheng area, and the best way to experience them is by hiking through the area. There are several well-maintained trails that lead visitors through the terraces, offering opportunities to interact with local villagers and learn about the traditional farming methods still used today. Along the trails, you’ll encounter villages inhabited by ethnic minorities such as the Zhuang and Yao, whose cultures are deeply intertwined with the landscape.

5. Seven Star Park (Qixing Gongyuan) – A Blend of Nature and Culture

Seven Star Park, located just outside the center of Guilin, is one of the city’s most popular parks and a place where nature and culture come together. The park is named after the seven peaks that resemble the Big Dipper constellation, and it is home to a variety of natural features, including caves, lakes, and forests.

The Seven Peaks

The Seven Peaks in the park are the most distinctive feature, and they are one of the best places to hike in the area. The peaks rise above the park and offer panoramic views of the city and surrounding karst mountains. Visitors can hike to the top for a bird’s eye view of Guilin and its stunning natural surroundings.

Other Natural Attractions in Seven Star Park

In addition to the peaks, Seven Star Park is home to several other natural wonders, including the Seven Star Cave, which is known for its striking limestone formations. The park also features peaceful lakes where visitors can rent boats and paddle around, enjoying the tranquil surroundings.

6. Yulong River (Yulong He) – The "Little Li River"

A quieter and more serene alternative to the Li River, the Yulong River is often referred to as the “Little Li River” due to its similar landscape of karst peaks and crystal-clear waters. Located in Yangshuo, a town about an hour’s drive from Guilin, the Yulong River is an ideal destination for those seeking a more peaceful and intimate connection with nature.

Bamboo Rafting on the Yulong River

One of the best ways to experience the beauty of Yulong River is by taking a bamboo raft ride. The river is less crowded than the Li River, and the slow-moving raft trip offers a relaxing way to take in the surrounding landscape. As you drift down the river, you’ll pass by picturesque villages, fields, and limestone cliffs, all while enjoying the peaceful atmosphere of the region.

Cycling Along the Yulong River

Cycling is also a popular way to explore the area around the Yulong River. The countryside around Yangshuo is known for its picturesque views, and cycling through the rural landscape allows you to take in the beauty of the karst formations and traditional farming villages. The ride is relatively easy, with a mix of paved roads and dirt trails.

Guilin is a region of remarkable natural beauty, where every corner of the landscape seems to hold a new wonder. From the majestic karst peaks that line the Li River to the serene waters of the Yulong River, the natural attractions in this part of China are truly awe-inspiring. Whether you’re cruising down the river, hiking through ancient terraces, or exploring underground caves, Guilin offers an endless array of outdoor experiences that showcase the best of China’s natural heritage.
