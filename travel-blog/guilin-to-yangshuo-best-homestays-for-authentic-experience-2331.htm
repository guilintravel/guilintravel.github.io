Guilin to Yangshuo: Best Homestays for Authentic Experience

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

The journey from Guilin to Yangshuo is one of China’s most iconic travel routes, famed for its karst landscapes, serene Li River, and vibrant local culture. While luxury hotels and resorts abound, nothing beats the charm of staying in a homestay for an authentic, immersive experience. Here’s a curated list of the best homestays along this route, each offering unique insights into rural life, breathtaking views, and warm hospitality.

Why Choose a Homestay in Guilin and Yangshuo?

Homestays in this region provide more than just a place to sleep—they offer a window into the daily lives of locals. From farm-to-table meals to hands-on activities like rice planting or bamboo rafting, these stays let travelers connect deeply with the destination. Plus, many homestays are nestled in picturesque villages, far from the tourist crowds, ensuring tranquility and stunning scenery.

Top Homestays Along the Guilin-Yangshuo Route

1. Yangshuo Village Inn

Located in the heart of Yangshuo’s countryside, this family-run homestay is a favorite among travelers seeking peace and authenticity. The hosts, a local couple, serve homemade meals using ingredients from their garden. Guests can join in farming activities or simply relax on the terrace overlooking rice paddies and karst peaks.

Highlights:
- Traditional wooden architecture
- Bike rentals for exploring nearby trails
- Evening storytelling sessions about local folklore

2. Li River Retreat

Perched along the banks of the Li River, this homestay offers front-row seats to the region’s legendary scenery. The hosts organize private boat tours and fishing excursions, giving guests a chance to experience the river like a local. Rooms are simple but cozy, with large windows framing the landscape.

Highlights:
- Riverside bonfires at sunset
- Freshly caught fish for dinner
- Guided hikes to hidden viewpoints

3. Dragon’s Nest Homestay

Tucked away in a quiet village near Xingping, Dragon’s Nest is perfect for those who want to escape the hustle. The host family specializes in traditional Chinese medicine and offers herbal tea workshops. The property also has a small organic farm where guests can pick vegetables for their meals.

Highlights:
- Herbal bath experiences
- Close to the Xingping Ancient Town
- Sunrise photography tours

4. Moon Hill Homestay

For adventure seekers, this homestay near the famous Moon Hill is ideal. The hosts are avid climbers and can arrange guided rock-climbing trips. After a day of adventure, guests unwind in rustic-chic rooms with panoramic views of the karst formations.

Highlights:
- Climbing gear provided
- Outdoor BBQ nights
- Easy access to lesser-known hiking trails

5. Bamboo Grove Lodge

Set in a bamboo forest near Yangdi, this eco-friendly homestay emphasizes sustainability. The hosts use solar power and rainwater harvesting, and meals are prepared with zero waste. Guests can learn bamboo weaving or join a meditation session in the grove.

Highlights:
- Eco-conscious design
- Bamboo craft workshops
- Birdwatching tours

Unique Experiences Offered by Local Homestays

Beyond comfortable lodging, these homestays provide unforgettable activities that showcase the region’s culture and natural beauty.

Cooking Classes with Local Chefs

Many homestays offer cooking classes where guests learn to prepare Guilin米粉 (Guilin rice noodles) or啤酒鱼 (beer fish), a Yangshuo specialty. These sessions often start with a trip to the local market to pick fresh ingredients.

Farming and Harvesting

Participate in seasonal activities like tea picking, rice planting, or fruit harvesting. It’s a hands-on way to understand rural life and appreciate the hard work behind every meal.

Cultural Workshops

From calligraphy and paper fan painting to traditional music performances, homestays often collaborate with local artisans to preserve and share Guangxi’s cultural heritage.

Tips for Booking the Perfect Homestay

  1. Book Early – Popular homestays fill up quickly, especially during peak seasons (spring and autumn).
  2. Communicate Preferences – Let hosts know about dietary restrictions or special requests in advance.
  3. Pack Light – Many homestays are in remote areas with limited luggage storage.
  4. Learn Basic Phrases – While some hosts speak English, knowing a few Mandarin phrases like “谢谢” (thank you) enhances the experience.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Homestays

For travelers willing to venture further, these lesser-known gems offer even deeper immersion:

Fuli Village Homestay

Fuli is famous for its handmade fans and tranquil riverside setting. The homestay here includes fan-painting workshops and leisurely walks along the riverbank.

Longji Rice Terraces Homestay

Though slightly off the Guilin-Yangshuo route, a stay in the Longji Terraces area is worth the detour. Wake up to misty mountains and spend the day trekking through cascading rice fields.

Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, homestays between Guilin and Yangshuo promise memories that last a lifetime. From the misty mornings over the Li River to the starry nights in bamboo forests, these stays capture the soul of Guangxi.
