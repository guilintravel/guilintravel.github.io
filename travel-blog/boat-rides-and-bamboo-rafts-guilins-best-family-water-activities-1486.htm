Boat Rides and Bamboo Rafts: Guilin’s Best Family Water Activities

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its misty karst mountains and winding rivers, is a dream destination for families seeking adventure and relaxation. The city’s water-based activities—especially boat rides and bamboo rafts—offer unforgettable experiences for visitors of all ages. Whether you’re gliding down the Li River or drifting through the serene Yulong River, Guilin’s waterways promise breathtaking scenery and cultural immersion. Here’s your guide to the best family-friendly water activities in Guilin.

Why Guilin’s Water Activities Are Perfect for Families

Guilin’s rivers and lakes are not just scenic—they’re also incredibly accessible for families. The gentle currents, shallow waters, and well-organized tours make it easy for parents and kids to enjoy the outdoors together. Plus, the slow pace of bamboo rafting and leisurely boat cruises ensures that even the youngest travelers won’t feel overwhelmed.

Safety First: Kid-Friendly Water Adventures

Most guided tours in Guilin prioritize safety, providing life jackets and experienced local boatmen. Bamboo rafts are stable, and motorized boats have railings, so parents can relax while kids soak in the views.

Top Family-Friendly Boat Rides in Guilin

1. Li River Cruise: The Classic Guilin Experience

No trip to Guilin is complete without a Li River cruise. This iconic journey takes you past towering limestone peaks, rural villages, and water buffalo grazing along the banks. The 4-hour ride from Guilin to Yangshuo is perfect for families who want to sit back and enjoy the scenery.

Why Families Love It:
- Spacious boats with indoor and outdoor seating
- Onboard meals and snacks available
- Photo ops at famous landmarks like Xingping’s "20 Yuan Bill" view

Pro Tip: Book a morning cruise to avoid crowds and catch the best light for photos.

2. Night Cruise on the Two Rivers and Four Lakes

For a magical evening, hop on a night cruise through Guilin’s Two Rivers and Four Lakes. The illuminated bridges, pagodas, and dancing fountains create a fairy-tale atmosphere that kids adore.

Highlights:
- Light shows synchronized with music
- Passing under beautifully lit traditional bridges
- A relaxing way to end a day of sightseeing

Bamboo Rafting: A Must-Try Guilin Adventure

If your family loves hands-on experiences, bamboo rafting is a must. Unlike motorized boats, rafts offer a closer connection to nature—kids can even dip their hands in the water as you float by.

3. Yulong River Bamboo Rafts: Peaceful and Picturesque

The Yulong River, a quieter alternative to the Li River, is ideal for families. The rafts here are manually poled by local fishermen, making for a tranquil ride.

What Makes It Great for Kids:
- Shallow, calm waters perfect for safe rafting
- Opportunities to spot ducks and fish
- Short routes available (as little as 40 minutes)

Best Spot: The section between Jinlong Bridge and Gongnong Bridge offers the most scenic views.

4. Dragon River (Longsheng) Bamboo Rafting

For families visiting the Longji Rice Terraces, a bamboo raft ride on the Dragon River is a refreshing break from hiking. The cool mountain water and lush surroundings make it a hit with kids.

Bonus: Combine it with a visit to the nearby Yao and Zhuang minority villages for a cultural experience.

Unique Water Activities for Adventurous Families

5. Kayaking on the Li River

Older kids and teens might prefer kayaking for a more active adventure. Several operators offer guided kayak tours, allowing families to explore hidden coves and small islands.

Why Try It?
- A fun way to burn off energy
- Less crowded than traditional boat tours
- Great for spotting wildlife like kingfishers

6. Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP) in Yangshuo

Stand-up paddleboarding has become a trendy activity in Yangshuo’s calm river sections. Many resorts offer SUP rentals and beginner-friendly lessons.

Family Perks:
- Easy to learn (even for first-timers)
- A playful way to enjoy the water
- Instagram-worthy moments guaranteed

Tips for Planning Your Guilin Water Adventure

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Pleasant weather, blooming flowers.
  • Autumn (September-November): Clear skies, ideal for photos.

Avoid summer’s peak heat and occasional heavy rains, which can affect water levels.

What to Pack

  • Sunscreen and hats (the sun reflects off the water)
  • Water shoes for bamboo rafting
  • A waterproof phone case for snapshots

Booking Advice

  • Reserve Li River cruises in advance—they sell out fast.
  • For bamboo rafts, choose licensed operators for safety.

Guilin’s water activities offer something for every family, whether you prefer a leisurely cruise or an exciting paddleboarding session. The memories you make on these rivers and lakes will last a lifetime.
