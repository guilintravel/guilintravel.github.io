Guilin’s Best Boat Tours for First-Time Travelers

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

If there’s one destination in China that feels like stepping into a classical ink-wash painting, it’s Guilin. Nestled in the Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, this city is world-famous for its surreal karst landscape, misty rivers, and serene countryside. For first-time visitors, the best way to immerse yourself in this natural masterpiece is by boat. But with so many options—from the iconic Li River cruise to smaller, more intimate tours—choosing the right one can be overwhelming.

This guide is crafted to help you navigate Guilin’s most scenic and memorable boat tours. We’ll explore not just the classics, but also hidden gems, practical tips, and how to make the most of your journey on the water.

Why a Boat Tour is a Must-Do in Guilin

Guilin’s magic is intrinsically linked to its waterways. The Li River (Lijiang) and the Yulong River are the lifeblood of the region, carving their way through towering limestone peaks and lush bamboo groves. A boat tour isn’t just a activity; it’s the quintessential Guilin experience. It offers a unique, low-angle perspective that allows you to fully appreciate the scale and beauty of the mountains. You’ll glide past water buffalo grazing on riverbanks, fishermen using trained cormorants (a classic photo op!), and local villages seemingly untouched by time. It’s a peaceful, almost meditative way to connect with the landscape that has inspired poets and artists for centuries.

Top Boat Tour Recommendations for Your First Visit

1. The Classic Li River Cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo

This is the granddaddy of all Guilin boat tours and for good reason. This 4-5 hour journey covers an 83-kilometer stretch of the Li River, widely considered one of the most scenic in the world.

  • The Experience: You’ll board a larger, comfortable tourist boat in Guilin (typically from the Zhujiang Pier) and disembark in the vibrant town of Yangshuo. The trip is a constant parade of natural wonders. Key highlights include:

    • Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiangbishan): This iconic hill resembles an elephant drinking from the river and is a symbol of Guilin itself.
    • Nine Horses Fresco Hill (Jiumahua Shan): Test your imagination by trying to spot the nine horses painted by nature on the cliff face. Legend says finding all nine will bring you wisdom.
    • Yellow Cloth Shoal (Huangbu Tan): The water is so clear here that the reflection of the seven peaks alongside it is stunningly perfect, featured on the back of the 20 RMB note.

  • Best For: First-timers who want to see all the major highlights in one comprehensive, comfortable trip. Lunch is usually provided on board.

  • Pro Tip: Book your tickets in advance, especially during peak season (April-October). Try to get a seat on the upper deck for unobstructed views and photography.

2. The Bamboo Raft on the Yulong River

For a more tranquil, intimate, and romantic experience, a bamboo raft down the Yulong River is an absolute must. This is a tributary of the Li River, located in Yangshuo's countryside, and is far quieter.

  • The Experience: You’ll board a simple raft made of thick bamboo poles, poled by a local guide. The river is shallower and calmer, allowing you to drift peacefully through a picture-perfect landscape of farmland, ancient bridges, and grazing water buffalo. The sound of the water and the bamboo pole dipping into the river is incredibly soothing.

  • Best For: Couples, photographers, and anyone looking to escape the crowds and experience the serenity of the Chinese countryside.

  • Pro Tip: Don’t expect luxury. You might get a splash of water, so protect your electronics. The tours are shorter (1-2 hours) and can be combined with a cycling tour of the area. The most scenic section is from Jinlong Bridge to Jiuxian Village.

3. The Night Cruise on Guilin's Two Rivers and Four Lakes

While the Li River showcases Guilin’s natural beauty by day, this tour reveals its urban charm by night. The Two Rivers and Four Lakes project connects the Li River, Peach Blossom River (Taohuajiang), and four central lakes (Ronghu, Shanhu, Guihu, and Mulonghu) into a beautiful city water system.

  • The Experience: As darkness falls, the city lights up. You’ll cruise on a smaller boat past illuminated pagodas, famous bridges like the Sun and Moon Pagoda Twin Towers, and lush gardens. The lights reflecting on the water create a magical, almost surreal atmosphere. It’s a completely different side of Guilin.

  • Best For: Travelers spending a night in Guilin city itself. It’s a perfect after-dinner activity that is both relaxing and visually spectacular.

  • Pro Tip: The best photos are taken from the boat, so bring a camera with good low-light capabilities. The Sun and Moon Pagodas are the highlight, so be ready.

Beyond the Boat: Enhancing Your Guilin Experience

A boat tour is the centerpiece, but Guilin offers so much more. To make your trip truly unforgettable, consider weaving these related hot spots and activities into your itinerary.

Exploring Yangshuo

Your Li River cruise will end here, and you should definitely plan to stay for a night or two. Yangshuo is a hub for adventure and culture. Rent a bicycle and explore the flat paths through the rice paddies, take a cooking class to learn how to make local dishes like Beer Fish (Pijiu Yu), or watch the breathtaking "Impression Sanjie Liu" light show. Directed by famed filmmaker Zhang Yimou, this performance takes place on the Li River itself, with the karst mountains as its natural backdrop—a truly epic spectacle.

Visiting the Longji Rice Terraces

While not a boat tour, no trip to the Guilin area is complete without a day trip to the Longji (Dragon’s Backbone) Rice Terraces. These spectacular terraces, carved into the mountainsides by the Zhuang and Yao people centuries ago, are a testament to human ingenuity and harmony with nature. The views are especially breathtaking after rainfall when the clouds swirl around the peaks, and during the autumn harvest when the fields turn a brilliant gold.

Practical Tips for First-Time Travelers

  • Best Time to Go: Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November) offer the most pleasant weather and clear skies. Summer can be hot and rainy, while winter is milder but often foggy.
  • What to Pack: Sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses are essential. A light rain jacket is a good idea year-round. For bamboo rafting, a waterproof bag for your phone and camera is highly recommended.
  • Booking Tours: You can book Li River cruises through your hotel or reputable travel agencies in Guilin. For bamboo rafts, it’s often easy to arrange upon arrival in Yangshuo.
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Always be respectful when taking photos of local villagers or fishermen. A smile goes a long way.
  • Cash is King: While major hotels and restaurants accept cards, have plenty of cash (RMB) on hand for smaller vendors, tips for boat guides, and market purchases.

Guilin is a destination that captures the heart. Its landscape is not just something you see, but something you feel. By choosing the right boat tour for your style, you are setting the stage for an adventure that you will carry with you long after you’ve returned home. It’s a journey into the very soul of China’s natural beauty.
