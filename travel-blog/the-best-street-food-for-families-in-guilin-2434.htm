The Best Street Food for Families in Guilin

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its breathtaking karst landscapes and serene Li River, is a dream destination for families. But beyond the postcard-perfect views, the city boasts a vibrant street food scene that’s both delicious and family-friendly. From savory rice noodles to sweet glutinous rice treats, Guilin’s street food offers something for every palate. Here’s a guide to the best street food spots and dishes your family shouldn’t miss.

Why Guilin’s Street Food is Perfect for Families

Street food in Guilin isn’t just about quick bites—it’s a cultural experience. The dishes are often simple, fresh, and packed with flavor, making them ideal for kids and adults alike. Plus, the open-air markets and food stalls add an element of adventure to your family trip. Here’s what makes Guilin’s street food stand out:

  • Affordable and Accessible: Most street food items cost just a few yuan, so you can sample a variety without breaking the bank.
  • Kid-Friendly Flavors: Many dishes are mildly spiced or customizable, perfect for picky eaters.
  • Interactive Dining: Watching vendors prepare food right in front of you is entertaining for kids.

Must-Try Street Food Dishes in Guilin

Guilin Rice Noodles (Guilin Mifen)

No visit to Guilin is complete without trying its iconic rice noodles. This dish is a staple for locals and a hit with families because of its simplicity. Thin, silky rice noodles are served in a flavorful broth, topped with sliced beef, peanuts, and pickled vegetables.

Why Kids Love It:
- The noodles are soft and easy to eat.
- Toppings can be added or omitted based on preference.

Best Place to Try:
Head to Chongshan Rice Noodles near the city center, where the broth is rich and the portions are generous.

Stuffed Li River Snails (Lipiao)

For adventurous families, Li River snails are a unique delicacy. The snails are stuffed with minced pork, garlic, and spices, then steamed or stir-fried. While the idea might seem unusual to kids, the tender, flavorful meat inside often wins them over.

Pro Tip:
Ask the vendor to show your kids how to extract the meat with a toothpick—it’s like a fun food puzzle!

Glutinous Rice Balls (Ciba)

These chewy, sweet rice balls are a favorite among children. Made from pounded glutinous rice and often filled with red bean paste or sesame, they’re a great snack for energy during sightseeing.

Where to Find Them:
Look for vendors near Zhengyang Pedestrian Street, where they’re freshly made and served warm.

Guilin Fried Rice Noodles (Chao Mifen)

A drier alternative to the soup version, fried rice noodles are stir-fried with eggs, vegetables, and sometimes meat. It’s a hearty dish that’s less messy for younger kids.

Family-Friendly Twist:
Request less spice and extra veggies to make it more kid-approved.

Best Street Food Markets for Families

Zhengyang Pedestrian Street

This bustling pedestrian zone is packed with food stalls offering everything from skewered meats to sweet desserts. The lively atmosphere and wide variety make it a great spot for families.

Highlights:
- Sugar Painting: Watch artists create edible sugar art—kids can choose their favorite shapes.
- Grilled Skewers: Chicken, tofu, and squid skewers are easy for little hands to hold.

Dongxi Alley (Dongxi Xiang)

A historic alley turned foodie paradise, Dongxi Alley is less crowded than Zhengyang but equally delicious. It’s a great place to introduce kids to Guilin’s culinary traditions.

Must-Try:
- Water Chestnut Cakes: Light, slightly sweet, and fun to eat.
- Steamed Taro Buns: Soft and fluffy, perfect for a quick snack.

Tips for Enjoying Street Food with Kids

  1. Start Small: Order one dish to share so kids can try without commitment.
  2. Hydrate: Carry bottled water to balance salty or spicy flavors.
  3. Go Early: Street food stalls are less crowded in the morning, making it easier with strollers or young children.
  4. Watch for Freshness: Choose stalls with high turnover to ensure the food is freshly made.

Final Thoughts

Guilin’s street food scene is a treasure trove of flavors waiting to be explored. Whether your family is into noodles, snacks, or sweet treats, the city’s vibrant markets and alleys offer endless opportunities for culinary adventure. So grab a bite, soak in the sights, and let Guilin’s street food become a highlight of your family trip.
