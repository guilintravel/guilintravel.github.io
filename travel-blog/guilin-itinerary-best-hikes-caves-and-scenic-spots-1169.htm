Guilin Itinerary: Best Hikes, Caves, and Scenic Spots

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a gem in China’s Guangxi region, is a dream destination for nature lovers. With its dramatic karst landscapes, serene rivers, and hidden caves, it’s no wonder this area has inspired poets and painters for centuries. Whether you’re an avid hiker, a cave explorer, or someone who just wants to soak in breathtaking views, this Guilin itinerary covers the best hikes, caves, and scenic spots to make your trip unforgettable.

The Must-See Scenic Spots in Guilin

Li River Cruise

No trip to Guilin is complete without a Li River cruise. The stretch from Guilin to Yangshuo is legendary, offering postcard-perfect views of limestone peaks, water buffalo grazing by the riverbanks, and local fishermen on bamboo rafts. The best time for this cruise is early morning when the mist wraps around the karst formations, creating an ethereal atmosphere.

Elephant Trunk Hill

This iconic landmark resembles an elephant drinking from the river. It’s one of Guilin’s most photographed spots, especially at sunset when the hill glows golden. You can climb to the top for panoramic city views or explore the nearby water moon cave.

Reed Flute Cave

Known as “the Palace of Natural Arts,” Reed Flute Cave is a mesmerizing underground world of stalactites, stalagmites, and rock formations illuminated by colorful lights. The cave’s name comes from the reeds growing outside, which were once used to make flutes.

Best Hikes in and Around Guilin

Xianggong Mountain

For the best sunrise view in Guilin, hike up Xianggong Mountain. The trail is short but steep, taking about 20-30 minutes to reach the summit. Once at the top, you’ll be rewarded with a sweeping vista of the Li River winding through karst peaks.

Moon Hill in Yangshuo

Moon Hill is famous for its natural moon-shaped arch. The hike to the top takes about 30-45 minutes and offers stunning views of Yangshuo’s countryside. If you’re feeling adventurous, you can even rock climb here with local guides.

Longji Rice Terraces

A bit farther from Guilin (about 2.5 hours by car), the Longji Rice Terraces are worth the trip. The terraces, carved into the mountains by the Zhuang and Yao people, change colors with the seasons—lush green in summer and golden in autumn. The Ping’an and Dazhai villages are great starting points for hikes.

Exploring Guilin’s Fascinating Caves

Silver Cave

Located near Yangshuo, Silver Cave is another must-visit. Its shimmering calcite crystals give the cave its name. The walkways are well-lit, making it easy to admire the intricate formations, including the “Music Waterfall,” a rare geological feature.

Seven Star Cave

Inside Seven Star Park, this cave is one of Guilin’s oldest tourist attractions. It’s named after the seven peaks of the park that resemble the Big Dipper. Inside, you’ll find ancient inscriptions and impressive rock formations.

Crown Cave

For a more interactive experience, Crown Cave offers a boat ride through an underground river. The cave system is massive, and you can even take a mini-train inside to explore deeper sections.

Hidden Gems & Off-the-Beaten-Path Spots

Yulong River Bamboo Rafting

While the Li River gets all the attention, the Yulong River offers a quieter, more intimate experience. Floating down this river on a bamboo raft, you’ll pass small villages, ancient bridges, and lush farmland.

Xingping Ancient Town

This charming town is where you’ll find the view featured on the 20 RMB note. Wander its cobbled streets, visit old temples, or hike up Laozhai Mountain for a bird’s-eye view of the Li River’s most scenic bend.

Fuli Town

Known for its handmade fans, Fuli is a great place to experience local craftsmanship. The town is also a starting point for cycling routes along the river.

Practical Tips for Your Guilin Adventure

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds.
  • Transportation: Biking is popular in Yangshuo, while buses and taxis are convenient in Guilin.
  • Food to Try: Guilin rice noodles, beer fish, and stuffed Li River snails are local specialties.
  • Packing Essentials: Comfortable hiking shoes, a rain jacket (sudden showers are common), and a good camera.

Guilin’s landscapes are like something out of a fantasy novel. Whether you’re hiking to a mountain peak, drifting down a river, or exploring mystical caves, every moment here feels magical. This itinerary ensures you experience the very best of what Guilin has to offer.
