Guilin Travel Map: Exploring the Region’s Waterfalls

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, a jewel of southern China, is renowned for its karst landscapes, winding rivers, and lush greenery. But beyond the iconic Li River and Elephant Trunk Hill lies a lesser-known treasure: the region’s breathtaking waterfalls. Whether you’re an adventurer, a photographer, or simply a nature lover, Guilin’s cascades offer a refreshing escape from the beaten path. This guide will take you through the most stunning waterfalls in the area, along with tips for exploring them like a pro.

The Best Waterfalls in Guilin

1. Detian Waterfall (跨国瀑布)

Straddling the border between China and Vietnam, Detian Waterfall is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in Asia. Its multi-tiered cascades stretch over 200 meters wide, creating a thunderous roar that echoes through the surrounding valleys.

Why Visit?
- The waterfall is at its most powerful during the rainy season (May–October).
- Bamboo raft rides get you up close to the misty base.
- The nearby ethnic villages offer a glimpse into local Zhuang culture.

Travel Tip: Bring a waterproof camera—the spray is intense!

2. Xianren Waterfall (仙人瀑布)

Tucked away in Yangshuo’s countryside, Xianren Waterfall is a hidden gem. Unlike Detian’s grandeur, this waterfall is serene, with crystal-clear pools perfect for a dip.

Why Visit?
- A short hike through rice paddies leads to the falls.
- The surrounding karst peaks create a surreal backdrop.
- Ideal for a quiet picnic away from crowds.

Travel Tip: Visit early in the morning to avoid midday heat and enjoy the best light for photography.

3. Jiulong Waterfall (九龙瀑布)

Located in the Longsheng region, Jiulong Waterfall is a series of nine cascades descending through dense forests. Each tier has its own unique charm, from gentle streams to dramatic plunges.

Why Visit?
- A well-maintained wooden walkway makes it accessible.
- The area is part of the Longji Rice Terraces, so you can combine both attractions.
- Autumn brings fiery foliage that contrasts beautifully with the water.

Travel Tip: Wear sturdy shoes—some sections can be slippery.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Waterfalls

4. Feilong Waterfall (飞龙瀑布)

For those willing to venture deeper into Guilin’s wilderness, Feilong Waterfall is a rewarding challenge. The hike is steep, but the sight of water gushing down a 100-meter cliff is unforgettable.

Why Visit?
- Rarely crowded, offering a true escape into nature.
- The trail passes through bamboo forests and small streams.
- Adventurous travelers can climb behind the falls for a unique perspective.

Travel Tip: Bring plenty of water and snacks—there are no vendors nearby.

5. Baishou Waterfall (百寿瀑布)

Named after the nearby Baishou Village, this waterfall is lesser-known but equally enchanting. Its wide curtain of water creates a natural swimming hole at the base.

Why Visit?
- The surrounding area is rich in biodiversity—keep an eye out for rare birds.
- Local guides offer storytelling sessions about the waterfall’s legends.
- A great spot for a refreshing swim in summer.

Travel Tip: Respect local customs—some villagers consider the falls sacred.

Planning Your Waterfall Adventure

When to Go

  • Best Season: Late spring to early autumn (May–October) for full water flow.
  • Avoid: Winter, when some waterfalls may reduce to a trickle.

How to Get There

  • Detian Waterfall: 3-hour drive from Nanning (closest major city).
  • Xianren Waterfall: Rent a bike or e-bike in Yangshuo for a scenic ride.
  • Jiulong Waterfall: Combine with a trip to the Longji Rice Terraces.

What to Pack

  • Quick-dry clothing and waterproof shoes.
  • A lightweight rain jacket (waterfalls = mist!).
  • Snacks and hydration—many sites lack facilities.

Beyond the Waterfalls: Nearby Attractions

While chasing waterfalls, don’t miss these other Guilin highlights:

  • Li River Cruise: A classic way to see Guilin’s karst peaks.
  • Yangshuo’s West Street: Bustling with cafes and souvenir shops.
  • Longji Rice Terraces: A stunning man-made landscape.

Guilin’s waterfalls are more than just scenic stops—they’re gateways to adventure, culture, and unforgettable memories. Whether you’re standing in the mist of Detian or swimming under Xianren’s gentle cascade, these natural wonders will leave you in awe. So grab your map, lace up your boots, and let the waterfalls guide your next Guilin journey.
