Top Family-Friendly Attractions in Guilin for All Ages

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|
  • Blog Details

Guilin, with its ethereal karst landscapes and serene rivers, is more than just a destination for honeymooners and solo travelers—it’s a paradise for families. The city offers a magical blend of natural wonders, cultural experiences, and interactive fun that captivates travelers of all ages. From cruising down the Li River to exploring mystical caves and engaging with local ethnic cultures, Guilin ensures that every family member, from toddlers to grandparents, leaves with cherished memories.

1. Li River Cruise: A Scenic Journey for the Whole Family

A trip to Guilin is incomplete without a Li River cruise. This iconic experience offers breathtaking views of limestone peaks, lush bamboo groves, and local fishing villages. For families, it’s an opportunity to bond while immersed in one of China’s most famous landscapes.

Why Families Love It

The cruise is relaxing and suitable for all ages. Kids will enjoy spotting water buffaloes along the riverbanks and trying to identify unique mountain shapes, like Elephant Trunk Hill. Many boats offer open decks for photo opportunities and onboard meals featuring local dishes. Opt for a shorter cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo if traveling with young children to avoid restlessness.

Tips for a Smooth Experience

Book tickets in advance, especially during holidays like Golden Week. Pack snacks, sunscreen, and hats for sun protection. Consider a private boat tour for more flexibility with stops and pacing.

2. Yangshuo: Adventure and Culture in One Destination

Yangshuo, the final stop of the Li River cruise, is a hub for family-friendly activities. Its compact town center, surrounded by dramatic scenery, offers a mix of adventure, culture, and relaxation.

Top Family Activities in Yangshuo

Rent bicycles or electric carts to explore the countryside. The flat terrain along the Yulong River is perfect for cycling with kids. Stop at a local farm to taste fresh fruit or try bamboo rafting on the calmer sections of the river—a hit with children and adults alike.

Must-Visit Spots

Visit West Street (Xi Jie) for souvenir shopping and street food. Try mijiu (sweet rice wine) treats or stick to international options like pizza for picky eaters. The Yangshuo Night Show, directed by Zhang Yimou, is a cultural spectacle featuring hundreds of performers against a natural backdrop—great for introducing kids to Chinese folklore.

3. Reed Flute Cave: A Subterranean Wonderland

The Reed Flute Cave (Ludi Yan) is a mesmerizing limestone cave illuminated by colorful lights. Its otherworldly formations, resembling everything from dragons to forests, spark the imagination of young and old.

Why It’s Great for Kids

The cave’s pathways are well-paved and stroller-friendly. The light displays keep children engaged, and the cool interior provides a respite from Guilin’s humidity. Audio guides are available in multiple languages, sharing legends that bring the formations to life.

Practical Advice

Wear non-slip shoes as the cave can be damp. Visit during off-peak hours to avoid crowds. The surrounding park area is ideal for a family picnic after the tour.

4. Elephant Trunk Hill: Guilin’s Iconic Landmark

This natural rock formation resembling an elephant drinking water is Guilin’s most famous symbol. The site includes parks, trails, and cultural relics, making it a versatile stop for families.

Family-Friendly Features

Kids love climbing the gentle slopes and exploring the water caves. The onsite museum showcases Guilin’s history, while the nearby shops sell elephant-themed souvenirs. Boat rides around the hill offer unique photo angles.

Best Time to Visit

Arrive early in the morning to avoid tourist groups. The area is beautifully lit at night, but daytime visits are better for families with young children.

5. Longji Rice Terraces: A Cultural and Scenic Hike

The Longji (Dragon’s Backbone) Rice Terraces, about two hours from Guilin, offer a glimpse into rural China. The terraces change with the seasons—from lush green in spring to golden yellow in autumn—creating a stunning backdrop for family photos.

Family-Friendly Hiking

Choose the Ping’an Village route, which has well-maintained paths and shorter trails suitable for kids. Local Zhuang and Yao ethnic families run guesthouses and restaurants where you can try traditional meals. Kids might enjoy watching villagers in traditional dress or learning about rice farming.

Tips for Visiting

Wear comfortable walking shoes and bring layers—the weather can change quickly. Consider staying overnight in a guesthouse to fully experience the tranquility. For families with strollers, carriers are recommended due to stepped paths.

6. Seven Star Park: Urban Oasis for Play and Learning

Seven Star Park (Qixing Gongyuan) is Guilin’s largest green space, combining natural beauty with recreational activities. It’s perfect for a day of picnics, animal encounters, and light exploration.

Attractions Within the Park

The zoo houses pandas, monkeys, and other native animals—always a favorite with children. The Flower Bridge and Camel Hill provide scenic spots for family photos. Kids can burn energy at playgrounds or join locals flying kites on open lawns.

Educational Opportunities

Visit the Seven Star Cave for another cave experience or explore the historical inscriptions and teahouses. Rent a tandem bike for a fun way to cover more ground.

7. Jingjiang Prince City: A Step Back in Time

This Ming-era palace complex offers a historical counterpoint to Guilin’s natural attractions. The well-preserved architecture and gardens engage older children and history buffs, while younger kids enjoy the open spaces.

Interactive Elements

Dress-up in traditional costumes for photos or watch cultural performances. The city walls provide panoramic views of Guilin, and the onsite exhibitions detail the region’s past in an accessible way.

Visitor Tips

Hire a guide to bring stories to life, or use audio guides for self-paced tours. Combine with a visit to nearby Solitary Beauty Peak for a fuller historical experience.

8. Guilin Merryland Theme Park: Thrills for All Ages

For a break from nature, head to Guilin Merryland, a theme park with rides, shows, and attractions designed for families. It’s one of Guangxi’s largest parks, offering something for every age group.

Rides and Entertainment

From gentle carousels for toddlers to roller coasters for teens, the park balances excitement and safety. Cultural shows feature acrobatics and dance, highlighting local traditions in an entertaining format.

Planning Your Visit

Check the show schedule in advance and arrive early to avoid long lines. The park has plenty of dining options, though bringing snacks is wise for younger children.

9. Tea Culture and Cooking Classes: Hands-On Fun

Guilin’s tea plantations and cooking schools provide immersive experiences for families. Learn to pick tea leaves or cook local dishes like Guilin mifen (rice noodles) together.

Family-Friendly Classes

Many farms offer shorter sessions tailored to children, including tea tasting with sweet snacks. Cooking classes often include market tours to select fresh ingredients—a cultural adventure in itself.

Why It’s Memorable

These activities foster teamwork and creativity. Plus, you take home skills to recreate Guilin flavors long after the trip ends.

10. Night Markets and Street Food: A Feast for the Senses

Guilin’s night markets, such as Zhengyang Pedestrian Street, are bustling with energy. Families can sample local snacks, shop for souvenirs, and enjoy street performances.

Kid-Friendly Food Options

Stick to mild options like mifen or sweet zongzi (sticky rice dumplings). Avoid spicy dishes if children are sensitive. The vibrant atmosphere is entertaining even for those who prefer familiar foods.

Safety and Enjoyment

Hold younger children’s hands in crowded areas. Visit earlier in the evening to avoid late-night crowds.
