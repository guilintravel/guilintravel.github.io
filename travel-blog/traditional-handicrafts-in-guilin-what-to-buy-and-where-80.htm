Traditional Handicrafts in Guilin: What to Buy and Where

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog

Guilin, located in the Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, is not only famous for its breathtaking karst landscapes but also for its rich cultural heritage. Among the many ways to experience Guilin's unique culture, shopping for traditional handicrafts is a must. These crafts reflect the artistry, history, and lifestyle of the local people, particularly the Zhuang, Miao, and Yao ethnic minorities. From intricate embroidery to hand-carved woodwork, Guilin offers a variety of handcrafted treasures. Here’s a detailed guide to Guilin’s traditional handicrafts and the best places to find them.

Zhuang Brocade

One of the most iconic handicrafts in Guilin is Zhuang Brocade, a traditional textile created by the Zhuang ethnic group. Known for its vibrant colors and intricate patterns, Zhuang Brocade often features motifs inspired by nature, such as flowers, birds, and geometric designs symbolizing blessings and prosperity.

  • What to Look For:
    Zhuang Brocade is commonly made into scarves, wall hangings, cushion covers, and decorative panels. The craftsmanship involves hand-weaving techniques passed down through generations.
  • Where to Buy:
    • Guilin Zhuang Ethnic Handicraft Center: This shop specializes in authentic Zhuang Brocade and provides information about its history and cultural significance.
    • Yangshuo West Street: You’ll find small boutiques selling Zhuang Brocade products along this lively pedestrian street.

Miao Silver Jewelry

The Miao ethnic group in Guilin is renowned for their silver jewelry, which features elaborate designs and traditional motifs. These pieces are not just accessories but are deeply rooted in the Miao culture, often symbolizing social status and blessings.

  • What to Look For:
    Miao silver jewelry includes necklaces, earrings, bracelets, and headpieces. The craftsmanship often involves engraving, filigree work, and molding techniques.
  • Where to Buy:
    • Longsheng Rice Terraces Scenic Area: Local Miao artisans sell handmade silver jewelry in the villages surrounding the terraces.
    • Guilin Night Market: Located near Zhongshan Road, this market offers a variety of silver jewelry at reasonable prices.

Bamboo Weaving

Bamboo weaving is a traditional craft practiced across Guilin, especially in rural areas. The artisans use locally sourced bamboo to create baskets, trays, and decorative items.

  • What to Look For:
    Look for finely woven items like bamboo fans, tea trays, and storage containers. Some pieces are designed with both functionality and aesthetics in mind.
  • Where to Buy:
    • Daxu Ancient Town: This historic town, just outside Guilin, is a hub for traditional bamboo weaving. You can watch artisans at work and purchase their creations directly.
    • Local Craft Markets: Various markets across Guilin sell bamboo products, often made by families in nearby villages.

Painted Fans

Hand-painted fans are a classic Guilin souvenir, combining artistry and practicality. These fans often depict Guilin’s stunning landscapes, including Li River scenes, karst mountains, and rural villages.

  • What to Look For:
    Fans are typically made of silk or paper with wooden or bamboo frames. The paintings are done using traditional Chinese brush techniques, making each piece unique.
  • Where to Buy:
    • Guilin Art Gallery: This venue offers high-quality painted fans, often crafted by local artists.
    • Yangshuo Market: You’ll find a variety of painted fans here, ranging from simple designs to more intricate works of art.

Yao Embroidery

The Yao ethnic group in Guilin is celebrated for their exquisite hand embroidery, which adorns traditional clothing, bags, and accessories. These pieces often feature vibrant colors and symbolic patterns.

  • What to Look For:
    Popular items include embroidered bags, shoes, and table runners. Each piece tells a story, with motifs representing cultural beliefs and daily life.
  • Where to Buy:
    • Longji Rice Terraces: Yao women in this area often sell their embroidered goods directly to visitors.
    • Local Handicraft Shops in Guilin: Many shops specialize in Yao embroidery, offering a range of products for different budgets.

Clay Figurines

Guilin is also known for its clay figurines, which often depict local characters, animals, or mythological scenes. These figurines are hand-sculpted and painted, making them delightful keepsakes.

  • What to Look For:
    Look for figurines that capture the spirit of Guilin’s culture and natural beauty. Some depict traditional Chinese opera characters or scenes from Guilin’s daily life.
  • Where to Buy:
    • Guilin Folk Art Market: This market showcases clay figurines made by skilled artisans.
    • Yangshuo Fuli Town: Known as the birthplace of painted fans, this town also has shops selling handmade clay crafts.

Paper-Cut Art

Paper-cutting is a traditional Chinese art form that is especially popular in Guilin. These intricate designs are often used as decorations for festivals or gifts.

  • What to Look For:
    Paper-cut designs usually feature themes like flowers, animals, and traditional symbols. They are available framed for display or as standalone pieces.
  • Where to Buy:
    • Guilin Craft Workshops: Many workshops in the city offer paper-cut art along with demonstrations of the craft.
    • Tourist Areas: You can find paper-cut souvenirs in shops near popular attractions like Elephant Trunk Hill.

Where to Shop for Traditional Handicrafts

In addition to specific locations mentioned above, here are some general shopping destinations in Guilin where you can find a variety of traditional handicrafts:

  • Zhengyang Pedestrian Street: This bustling street in downtown Guilin is a great place to browse for souvenirs, including handicrafts. The lively atmosphere makes it a fun shopping experience.
  • Guilin Night Market: Open every evening, this market offers a wide selection of handmade items at affordable prices. Bargaining is common, so don’t hesitate to negotiate.
  • Yangshuo West Street: Known for its vibrant nightlife and eclectic shops, West Street is an excellent spot to shop for unique crafts and gifts.
  • Daxu Ancient Town: This historic town is a treasure trove of traditional crafts, from bamboo weaving to pottery.

Tips for Shopping in Guilin

  • Quality vs. Price: Not all handicrafts are of the same quality. Look for well-crafted items and be wary of mass-produced imitations.
  • Support Local Artisans: Buying directly from artisans ensures that your purchase supports traditional craftsmanship.
  • Learn the Stories: Many handicrafts come with rich cultural stories. Don’t hesitate to ask the sellers about the significance of the items you’re buying.
  • Bargain Politely: In markets, bargaining is expected. Start with a lower offer and work your way up, but always remain respectful.

Bringing Handicrafts Home

When purchasing handicrafts in Guilin, consider how easy they are to transport. Fragile items like clay figurines should be carefully packed, while textiles and paper-cut art are lightweight and easy to carry.

Guilin’s traditional handicrafts are not just souvenirs but also windows into the region’s rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for Zhuang Brocade, Miao silver jewelry, or bamboo crafts, these treasures will serve as lasting memories of your journey through one of China’s most beautiful regions.
